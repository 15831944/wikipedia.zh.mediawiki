'''寬恕'''，或'''饒恕'''、'''原諒'''，是一種自願行爲，其中行爲人（即受害者）放棄針對加害方的消極感情（[[復仇|復仇]]等），甚至還能為對方祝好<ref>{{cite book|last1=Graham|first1=Michael C.|title=Facts of Life: ten issues of contentment|date=2014|publisher=Outskirts Press|page=268|ISBN=978-1-4787-2259-5}}</ref><ref name="apa">{{cite web | year = 2006 | archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20110626153005/http://www.apa.org/international/resources/forgiveness.pdf | archive-date=2011-06-26 | url = http://www.apa.org/international/resources/forgiveness.pdf | title = American Psychological Association. ''Forgiveness: A Sampling of Research Results.'' | dead-url=yes | accessdate = 2009-02-07}}</ref><ref>[http://greatergood.berkeley.edu/topic/forgiveness/definition What Is Forgiveness?] The Greater Good Science Center, University of California, Berkeley</ref>。相較之下，[[赦免|赦免]]則是由特定的社會代表（如[[法官|法官]]）宣佈不再追究加害方的責任，未必代表受害人的主觀意見<ref name="apa"/>。但在法律上寬恕實際也有效力，如債權人可借此放棄對借債者的追究<ref>[http://stats.oecd.org/glossary/detail.asp?ID=555 DEBT FORGIVENESS] OECD, Glossary of Statistical Terms (2001)</ref><ref>[http://studentaid.ed.gov/repay-loans/forgiveness-cancellation/charts/public-service Loan Forgiveness] Glossary, US Department of Education</ref>。寬恕常被視為一種[[美德|美德]]，[[宗教學|宗教學]]、[[心理學|心理學]]和[[社會學|社會學]]等學科會研究其作用及原理。

== 研究 ==
[[File:Determinants_of_Forgiveness_Graphic.JPG|thumb]]中與寬恕近似的情感]]

心理學上尚未對寬恕的定義達成一致，不過最起碼它是一個延續的“過程”，同時心理學家已經提出了一系列描述此過程的模型<ref>Cordova, J., Cautilli, J., Simon, C. & Axelrod-Sabtig, R (2006). Behavior Analysis of Forgiveness in Couples Therapy. ''IJBCT, 2(2),'' Pg. 192 [http://www.behavior-analyst-online.org BAO] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080309131808/http://www.behavior-analyst-online.org/ |date=2008-03-09 }}</ref>。此外還有研究注重寬恕程度在人際間的差異，如[[神經質|神經質]]和易怒的人即使在很長一段時間之後也很難原諒加害者，甚至會籌劃報復行爲<ref>Maltby, J., Wood, A. M., Day, L., Kon, T. W. H., Colley, A., and Linley, P. A. (2008). [http://personalpages.manchester.ac.uk/staff/alex.wood/forgiveness.pdf Personality predictors of levels of forgiveness two and a half years after the transgression.] Journal of Research in Personality, 42, 1088-1094.</ref>。

而容易原諒別人的人通常活得更加開心、健康<ref>{{cite web | year = 2006 | url = http://www.forgiving.org | title = Forgiving (Campaign for Forgiveness Research) | accessdate = 2006-06-19}}</ref>。有研究顯示這類人的[[循環系統|循環系統]]及[[神經系統|神經系統]]都比不容易原諒他人者更加健康<ref>Van Oyen, C. Witvilet, T.E. Ludwig and K. L. Vander Lann, "Granting Forgiveness or Harboring Grudges: Implications for Emotions, Physiology and Health," ''Psychological Science'' no. 12 (2001):117-23</ref><ref>S. Sarinopoulos, "Forgiveness and Physical Health: A Doctoral Dissertation Summary," ''World of Forgiveness'' no. 2 (2000): 16-18</ref>。[[斯坦福大學|斯坦福大學]]的弗雷德·拉斯金（Fred Luskin）博士發現可以通過學習使得人更加寬容，從而更加健康<ref>{{cite web|url=http://learningtoforgive.com/ |title=Learningtoforgive.com |publisher=Learningtoforgive.com |date=2014-06-20 |accessdate=2016-05-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.forgivenessday.org/ |title=Worldwide Forgiveness Alliance |publisher=Forgivenessday.org |date=2015-11-13 |accessdate=2015-12-23 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20050213063928/http://www.yogitimes.com/02_2005/interview.html |archivedate=2005-02-13 }}</ref>。
