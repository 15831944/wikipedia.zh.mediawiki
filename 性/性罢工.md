'''性罢工'''（{{lang-en|sex strike}}），有时又称'''性抵制'''（sex boycott），是一种一人或多人为了达成某些目标而暂时性[[禁欲|禁欲]]（拒绝与[[性伴侶|性伴侶]]发生[[人类性行为|性行为]]）的[[非暴力抵抗|非暴力]][[罢工|罢工]]手段。性罢工曾被用来抗议许多问题，例如[[战争|战争]]、[[帮派|帮派]]暴力等。
== 历史上的性罢工 ==
=== 古希腊 ===
[[File:Lysistrate_(Marble_Stele).jpg|缩略图]]
古希腊最著名的性罢工当属阿里斯托芬笔下的反战喜剧《{{Tsl|en|Lysistrata|吕西斯特拉忒}}》。<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://investorshub.advfn.com/boards/read_msg.aspx?message_id=27673724|title=Tornado Alley (PROG) Post # 59832|accessdate=2018-07-30|work=Investors Hub|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304112147/http://investorshub.advfn.com/boards/read_msg.aspx?message_id=27673724|archive-date=2016-03-04|dead-url=no}}</ref>该剧中的女性角色和主人公通过拒绝和丈夫同房来确保和平，并促进了[[伯罗奔尼撒战争|伯罗奔尼撒战争]]的结束。

=== 尼日利亚 ===
在尼日利亚未被殖民时，[[伊博人|伊博人]]女性自发成立了委员会，其组织者Agba Ekwe对于当时的公众集会拥有最后的发言权。委员会的任务之一就是确保男性检点，发现男性有骚扰行为时对其处罚。<ref>{{Cite book|title=Male Daughters, Female Husbands. Gender and sex in an African society|last=Amadiume|first=Ifi|publisher=Zed Books|year=1987|isbn=|location=|pages=66-67}}</ref>

=== 史前世界 ===
史前时代[[狩猎采集|狩猎采集]]的出现让人类社会变得团结。一些人类学家持有这样的观点：在语言、文化和宗教形成时，文明的本性使得我们的祖先不论分布在何处都一致反对[[强奸|强奸]]。这种具有争议的假设奠定了“{{Tsl|en|Female cosmetic coalitions|4=外貌协会}}”一词的来源。此外，该假设也被称为“性罢工”，<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.chrisknight.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2007/09/Sex-Strike.pdf|title=Sex Strike|accessdate=|author=Knight C|date=1991|publisher=New Haven and London: Yale University Press|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131109184631/http://www.chrisknight.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2007/09/Sex-Strike.pdf|archive-date=2013-11-09|dead-url=no}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|title=Female strategies and collective behaviour: the archaeology of earliest Homo sapiens sapiens|last=|first=|publisher=|year=1996|isbn=|location=|pages=306-330}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|title=Female Proto-symbolic Strategies|last=|first=|publisher=Routledge|year=1997|isbn=|location=|pages=153-171}}</ref>或是直接使用源自古希腊的“吕西斯特拉忒”。<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.radicalanthropologygroup.org/old/pub_lysistrata.pdf|title=pub_lysistrata.pdf|accessdate=|author=Camilla Power|date=1990|publisher=University of East London (Anthropology) Occasional Papers.|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111012153245/http://www.radicalanthropologygroup.org/old/pub_lysistrata.pdf|archive-date=2011-10-12|dead-url=no}}</ref>

== 现代的性罢工 ==
===哥伦比亚===
1997年10月，{{Tsl|en|Military Forces of Colombia|4=哥伦比亚军队}}的司令官曼纽尔·博内公开呼吁左翼游击队员、毒品贩子和民兵组织的妻子和女友发动性罢工作为促进停火的手段。[[圣菲波哥大|圣菲波哥大]]宣布该城仅限女性进出，意图把男人留在家中而不是送往战场；但此决定遭到了游击队员的讽刺，因游击队中已有两千多名女性（[[军妓|军妓]]）。停火持续了短暂的时间。

2006年9月数十名来自[[佩雷拉|佩雷拉]]帮派配偶为反对帮派暴力发起了一场被称为“二郎腿运动”的性罢工。据活动发言人称，运动的目的是迫使帮派分子放下武器，遵守法律。大量帮派分子为了地位和吸引力而战，罢工者的目标则是告诉他们，这种战斗一点都不性感。<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/americas/5341574.stm|title=Colombian gangsters face sex ban|date=2006-09-13|accessdate=2018-07-30|language=en-GB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180521043344/http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/americas/5341574.stm|archive-date=2018-05-21|dead-url=no}}</ref>2010年哥伦比亚的谋杀率是有史以来最低，降低了26.5%。

2011年哥伦比亚西南部的[[巴瓦科阿斯|巴瓦科阿斯]]也爆发了类似的运动，目的是给当地政府施压，要求其修复连接周围城镇的道路。<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://nvdatabase.swarthmore.edu/content/colombian-women-use-sex-strike-pressure-government-repair-road-huelga-de-piernas-cruzadas-20|title=Colombian women use sex strike to pressure government to repair road (Huelga de piernas cruzadas), 2011 - Global Nonviolent Action Database|accessdate=2018-07-30|author=|date=|work=nvdatabase.swarthmore.edu|publisher=|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180326223511/https://nvdatabase.swarthmore.edu/content/colombian-women-use-sex-strike-pressure-government-repair-road-huelga-de-piernas-cruzadas-20|archive-date=2018-03-26|dead-url=no}}</ref>当地男性起初并不支持此次性罢工，但之后也加入了抗议的行列。<ref>{{Cite journal|title=The "Crossed Legs" Movement: How a Sex Strike Got Things Done|url=http://www.alternet.org/story/151940/the_%22crossed_legs%22_movement:_how_a_sex_strike_got_things_done|last=Montes|first=Euclides|date=2011-08-08|journal=AlterNet|author=|access-date=2018-07-30|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170102042027/http://www.alternet.org/story/151940/the_%22crossed_legs%22_movement%3A_how_a_sex_strike_got_things_done|archive-date=2017-01-02|dead-url=no}}</ref>在罢工进行了112天后，政府于10月承诺修路，施工继续进行。<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nbcnews.com/news/other/colombian-women-crossed-legs-sex-strike-over-crumbling-highway-f8C11531725|title=Colombian women on 'crossed legs' sex strike over crumbling highway|newspaper=NBC News|accessdate=2018-07-30|language=en-US|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170405235045/http://www.nbcnews.com/news/other/colombian-women-crossed-legs-sex-strike-over-crumbling-highway-f8C11531725|archive-date=2017-04-05|dead-url=no}}</ref>

=== 肯尼亚 ===
2009年4月，一些肯尼亚女性发动针对政客的性罢工，并鼓励总统和总理的妻子加入，甚至承诺会补偿因此给[[妓女|妓女]]带来的损失。<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/africa/8025457.stm|title=Kenyan women hit men with sex ban|date=2009-04-29|accessdate=2018-07-30|language=en-GB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180714180414/http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/africa/8025457.stm|archive-date=2018-07-14|dead-url=no}}</ref>

===利比里亚===
[[File:Leymah_Gbowee_(October_2011).jpg|缩略图]]
2003年，[[莱伊曼·古博薇|莱伊曼·古博薇]]等人发起了一次包含性罢工的非暴力抗议。这次抗议停下了持续14年之久的{{Translink|en|Second Liberian Civil War|4=利比里亚内战}}，促成了该国第一名女性领导人[[埃伦·约翰逊·瑟利夫|瑟利夫]]的当选，<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pbs.org/moyers/journal/06192009/profile.html|title=Bill Moyers Journal . PRAY THE DEVIL BACK TO HELL - PBS|accessdate=2018-07-30|author=|date=|work=www.pbs.org|publisher=|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171026214522/https://www.pbs.org/moyers/journal/06192009/profile.html|archive-date=2017-10-26|dead-url=no}}</ref>也让古博薇获得了2011年[[诺贝尔和平奖|诺贝尔和平奖]]。<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/peace/laureates/2011/press.html|title=The Nobel Peace Prize for 2011 to Ellen Johnson Sirleaf, Leymah Gbowee and Tawakkul Karman - Press Release|accessdate=2018-07-30|work=www.nobelprize.org|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180728063500/https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/peace/laureates/2011/press.html|archive-date=2018-07-28|dead-url=no}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.oprah.com/omagazine/Leymah-Gbowee-and-Abigail-Disney-Shoot-for-Peace-in-Liberia/2#ixzz1bTSs28cd|title=The Rabble Rousers|newspaper=Oprah.com|accessdate=2018-07-30|language=en-us|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180614004218/http://www.oprah.com/omagazine/Leymah-Gbowee-and-Abigail-Disney-Shoot-for-Peace-in-Liberia/2#ixzz1bTSs28cd|archive-date=2018-06-14|dead-url=no}}</ref>

===意大利===
2008年新年夜，数百名那不勒斯女性要求其伴侣“睡沙发”以抗议对烟火造成人身伤害的不满。<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/europe/7806367.stm|title=Naples sex strike over fireworks|date=2008-12-31|accessdate=2018-07-30|language=en-GB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090207154648/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/europe/7806367.stm|archive-date=2009-02-07|dead-url=no}}</ref>

===菲律宾===
2011年夏天，[[棉兰老岛|棉兰老岛]]乡下女性为终结两村间的斗争发动了持续数周的性罢工。<ref>{{cite web |last1=Smith |first1=Karen |title=Sex strike brings peace to Filipino village |url=http://edition.cnn.com/2011/WORLD/asiapcf/09/19/philippines.sex.strike/index.html?iref=NS1 |publisher=CNN |accessdate=2018-07-30 |date=2011-09-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171013142737/https://edition.cnn.com/2011/WORLD/asiapcf/09/19/philippines.sex.strike/index.html?iref=NS1 |archive-date=2017-10-13 |dead-url=no }}</ref>

===南苏丹===
2014年10月，南苏丹女性政治家{{Tsl|en|Pricilla Nanyang}}号召[[朱巴|朱巴]]的女性活动家开展“和平、康复与和谐”活动，参与者呼吁南苏丹女性在其伴侣确保和平稳定之前“拒绝行使同居权”。

===多哥===
受2003年利比里亚性罢工的影响，多哥反对党“Let's Save Togo”发动女性性罢工，以抗议专政超过45年的[[福雷·纳辛贝|福雷·纳辛贝]]总统家族。罢工的目的是号召不从政的男性“达成目标”。<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-africa-19389625|title=Sex strike against Togo's leader|date=2012-08-27|newspaper=BBC News|accessdate=2018-07-30|language=en-GB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207074636/http://www.bbc.com/news/world-africa-19389625|archive-date=2018-02-07|dead-url=no}}</ref>反对党领袖伊莎贝尔·阿美甘薇将其视为推动政治变局的有力武器。<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://content.usatoday.com/communities/ondeadline/post/2012/08/togo-women-call-sex-strike-to-force-presidents-resignation/1#.UG9ZSpjA_3E|title=Togo women call sex strike to force president's resignation|newspaper=USATODAY.COM|accessdate=2018-07-30}}</ref>
