Incorporated cities in Alaska are categorized as either "general law" (subdivided into "first class" and "second class") or "{{link-en||Home rule in the United States}}".  Home rule cities occur when a community establishes a commission to draft a charter, which is then ratified by voters at an election.  Home rule cities have all legislative powers not prohibited by law or charter. The powers and functions of general law cities are defined by Alaska state laws.
