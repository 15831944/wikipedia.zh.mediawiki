'''杓狀軟骨'''（arytenoid cartilage、arytenoid、{{IPAc-en|ær|ᵻ|ˈ|t|iː|n|ɔɪ|d}}）是一對小三角錐體，形成[[喉|喉]]部的一部分，且[[聲帶|聲帶]](vocal cords)與之相連。這些相連的結構允許及幫助聲帶的運動。

每個杓狀軟骨都是金字塔形或勺狀形（arytenoid 來自[[希臘語|希臘語]]；arytaina(意即勺子) + eidos(意即形狀)），<ref>[http://medical-dictionary.thefreedictionary.com/arytenoid+cartilage Farlex dictionary: arytenoid cartilage] Citing: Mosby's Medical Dictionary, 8th edition.</ref>且有三個表面，一個基部，以及一個頂點。
<!--
==Structure==
===Surfaces===
The '''posterior surface''' is a triangular, smooth, concave, and gives attachment to the [[arytenoid_muscle|arytenoid muscle]] and transversus.

The '''antero-lateral surface''' is somewhat convex and rough. On it, near the apex of the cartilage, is a rounded elevation (colliculus) from which a ridge (crista arcuata) curves at first backward and then downward and forward to the [[vocal_process|vocal process]]. The lower part of this crest intervenes between two depressions or [[foveæ|foveæ]], an upper, triangular, and a lower oblong in shape; the latter gives attachment to the [[vocalis|vocalis]] muscle.

The '''medial surface''' is narrow, smooth, and flattened, covered by [[mucous_membrane|mucous membrane]], and forms the lateral boundary of the intercartilaginous part of the [[rima_glottidis|rima glottidis]].-->
<!--
===Base and apex===
The '''base''' of each cartilage is broad, and on it is a concave smooth surface, for articulation with the [[cricoid_cartilage|cricoid cartilage]].

* Its lateral angle is called the [[muscular_process|muscular process]]
* Its anterior angle is called the [[vocal_process|vocal process]]

The '''apex''' of each cartilage is pointed, curved backward and medialward, and surmounted by a small conical, cartilaginous nodule, the [[corniculate_cartilage|corniculate cartilage]].-->
<!--
==Function==
They allow the [[vocal_folds|vocal folds]] to be tensed, relaxed, or approximated.

The arytenoids articulate with the supero-lateral parts of the [[cricoid_cartilage|cricoid cartilage]] lamina, forming the cricoarytenoid joints at which they can [[adduction|come together]], [[Anatomical_terms_of_location#Relative_motions|move apart]], tilt anteriorly or posteriorly, and rotate.
