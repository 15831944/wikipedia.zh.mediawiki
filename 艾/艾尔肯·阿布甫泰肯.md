'''艾尔肯·阿布甫泰肯''' （{{lang-ug|ئەركىن ئالپتەكىن|}}， {{lang|en|Erkin Alptekin}}，{{bd|1939年|7月4日}}），生于[[喀什地区|喀什地区]][[英吉沙县|英吉沙县]]，[[维吾尔族|维吾尔族]]政治家、[[新疆独立运动|新疆独立运动]]的活动家[[艾沙·玉素甫·阿布甫泰肯|艾沙·玉素甫·阿布甫泰肯]]长子。亦是[[非聯合國會員國家及民族組織|非聯合國會員國家及民族組織]]（UNPO）的創立人之一，及擔任UNPO（1999年 - 2003年）的總秘書長。曾任第一屆[[世界維吾爾代表大會|世界維吾爾代表大會]]主席（2004年4月至2006年11月）。

==生平==
Alptekin is the son of [[Isa_Alptekin|Isa Alptekin]], who in 1933-1934 was the General Secretary of the [[First_East_Turkestan_Republic|First East Turkestan Republic]] in [[western_China|western China]].<ref name=gfp>{{Cite news|url=http://www.german-foreign-policy.com/en/fulltext/56104|title=Strategies of Attrition (IV)|date=2007-10-22|accessdate=2010-07-31|publisher=[[German-Foreign-Policy.com|German-Foreign-Policy.com]]}}</ref>   After the [[Peaceful_Liberation_of_Xinjiang|Peaceful Liberation of Xinjiang]] in 1949 and succession by the new [[People's_Republic_of_China|People's Republic of China]], Alptekin's family fled to [[Srinagar|Srinagar]] in [[Jammu_and_Kashmir|Jammu and Kashmir]]. There he attended [[Catholic_school|Catholic school]] and then Convent College,<ref name=rp>{{Cite web|url=http://www.radicalparty.org/en/content/why-china-fears-uyghur-exile|title=Why China Fears This Uyghur Exile|author=Lawrence, Susan V.|publisher=[[Transnational_Radical_Party|Transnational Radical Party]]|accessdate=2010-07-31}}</ref> completing his studies in the Institute of Journalism in [[Istanbul|Istanbul]].<ref name=wuc/>  Alptekin is based in Germany.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/31792960/|title=Troops flood into China region after riots|agency=[[Associated_Press|Associated Press]]|date=2009-07-08|publisher=[[MSNBC|MSNBC]]}}</ref>

==职业==
In 1971, due to his father's connections, he got a job at [[Radio_Free_Europe|Radio Free Europe]]/Radio Liberty (RFE/RL) in [[Munich|Munich]]. He worked as a "Senior Policy Advisor" and directed the Uygur Division until 1979, when Uyghur-language broadcasts were discontinued for a lack of an audience under RFE/RL jurisdiction.<ref name="shichor">{{cite conference | first=Yitzhak | last=Shichor | year=2003 | title=Virtual Transnationalism: Uygur Communities in Europe and the Quest for Eastern Turkestan Independence | booktitle=Muslim Networks and Transnational Communities in and Across Europe | editor=Stefano Allievi & Jørgen Nielsen (eds.) | publisher=Brill | location=Leiden | pages=295 | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Gh7Q-EsH_EwC&pg=PA295&dq=erkin+alptekin+1939 | accessdate=2008-11-02}}</ref> During his time at the Division, he complained about lack of [[mass_media|media]] coverage of the Uyghurs, lamenting "the international community only reacts when conflict breaks out."<ref name="rp"/> Concurrently in the 1970s and 1980s, the [[Central_Intelligence_Agency|CIA]] looked to connect to Uyghur separatists, and found Alptekin a suitable match because of his leadership in various Uyghur organizations and his tenure at RFE. He became an adviser to the CIA,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.southasiaanalysis.org/papers5/paper499.html|title=US & Terrorism in Xinjiang|author=Raman, B.|publisher=South Asia Analysis Group|date=2002-07-24|accessdate=2010-07-31}}</ref> and retired his RFE/RL post in 1995 as it moved to [[Prague|Prague]] in the [[Czech_Republic|Czech Republic]].<ref name=wuc>{{Cite web|url=http://www.uyghurcongress.org/en/?cat=148|title=Introducing the World Uyghur Congress|publisher=[[World_Uyghur_Congress|World Uyghur Congress]]|accessdate=2010-07-31}}</ref>

Erkin Alptekin has founded various organizations for [[List_of_active_separatist_movements|separatist movements]], mostly related to the Uyghur nationalist cause. In 1985 he participated in the founding of the "Allied Committee of the Peoples of East Turkistan, Tibet and Inner Mongolia", which held its first conference in 1998 in New York. He founded the [[East_Turkistan_Union_in_Europe|East Turkistan Union in Europe]] in Europe.<ref name="shichor"/> In 1991, he also became one of the founders of the [[Unrepresented_Nations_and_Peoples_Organization|Unrepresented Nations and Peoples Organization]] (UNPO), which has its headquarters in [[The_Hague|The Hague]].<ref name="gfp"/> During a conference held in Munich  in April 2004 Alptekin was elected the President of the [[World_Uyghur_Congress|World Uyghur Congress]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.uyghurnews.com/canadian/Read.asp?UighurNews=erkin-alptekin-recently-elected-as-president-of-the-world-uyghur-congress-visi&ItemID=MQ-342008413481427638705|title=Erkin Alptekin, recently elected as President of the World Uyghur Congress, visits the United States|work=[[Voice_of_America|Voice of America]]|first=Stephanie|last=Ho|date=2004-06-30|accessdate=2010-07-31}}</ref> 

Erkin Alptekin has been lobbying, not only for [[Uyghur_people|Uyghur]] nationalism but also for [[List_of_active_separatist_movements|other separatist movements]], peoples, and [[minority_group|minorities]], including the indigenous peoples in [[Western_world|Western countries]] since 1971.

Erkin Alptekin is also on the advisory board of several international organizations situated in [[Asia|Asia]], Europe and in the [[United_States|United States]]. For the last 35 years Alptekin has attended more than 6,000 international conferences worldwide on various topics; published numerous articles, research papers and brochures; and has been the focus of several Western press media.
