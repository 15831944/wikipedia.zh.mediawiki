「'''被认为有害'''」（{{lang-en|Considered Harmful}}）一词是至少65篇[[计算机科学|计算机科学]]及相关学科领域重要论文均在标题中使用的一个短语模板。<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jeff.over.bz/?04_Miscellaneous/03_Considered_Harmful|title=Miscellaneous - Considered Harmful|accessdate=2009-08-17|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090503144621/http://jeff.over.bz/?04_Miscellaneous%2F03_Considered_Harmful|archivedate=2009-05-03|deadurl=yes}}</ref>其起源可以追溯到1968年[[艾兹赫尔·戴克斯特拉|艾兹赫尔·戴克斯特拉]]的文章《Go To语句被认为有害》（''Go To Statement Considered Harmful''）。

== 历史 ==
“被认为有害”一词由[[艾兹赫尔·戴克斯特拉|艾兹赫尔·戴克斯特拉]]发表在1968年3月的[[ACM通讯|ACM通讯]]（CACM）上的《Go To语句被认为有害》中首次出现<ref name="dijkstra1968">{{cite journal|title=Go To Statement Considered Harmful|author=[[Edsger_Dijkstra|Edsger Dijkstra]]|date=March 1968|journal=Communications of the ACM|issue=3|doi=10.1145/362929.362947|volume=11|pages=147–148|format=PDF|quote=The unbridled use of the go to statement has as an immediate consequence that it becomes terribly hard to find a meaningful set of coordinates in which to describe the process progress.  ... The go to statement as it stands is just too primitive, it is too much an invitation to make a mess of one's program.}}</ref><ref name="ewd215">{{Cite EWD|215}}</ref>，他在文章中批评了当时[[Goto|GOTO]][[語句_(程式設計)|语句]]的过度使用，并提出用[[结构化编程|結構化-{zh-tw:程式設計;zh-cn:编程;}-]]替代。<ref>{{cite web|url=http://david.tribble.com/text/goto.html|title=Go To Statement Considered Harmful: A Retrospective|author=David R. Tribble|date=February 2005}}</ref>提交给ACM通讯的原标题实际上是《反对GOTO语句一例》（''A Case Against the Goto Statement''），但ACM通讯的编辑[[尼克劳斯·维尔特|尼克劳斯·维尔特]]将标题修改为了前者。<ref>{{cite EWD|1308|What led to "Notes on Structured Programming"}} (June, 2001)</ref>[[高德纳|尼克劳斯·维尔特]]其实以新标题调侃了“[[後藤英一|後藤英一]]博士（其英文姓为Goto）高兴地抱怨他总是被淘汰。”<ref>{{Citation|last=Kanada|first=Yasumasa|title=Events and Sightings: An obituary of Eiichi Goto|year=2005|journal=IEEE Annals of the History of Computing|volume=27|issue=3|page=92|doi=10.1109/MAHC.2005.37}}</ref>

弗兰克·鲁宾随后于1987年3月的ACM通讯上发表了对此文章的评论，题为《〈Go To语句被认为有害〉被认为有害》（'''GOTO Considered Harmful' Considered Harmful''）。<ref name="rubin87goto">{{cite journal|title="GOTO Considered Harmful" Considered Harmful|author=Frank Rubin|url=http://www.ecn.purdue.edu/ParaMount/papers/rubin87goto.pdf|date=March 1987|journal=Communications of the ACM|issue=3|doi=10.1145/214748.315722|volume=30|pages=195–196|format=PDF|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090320002214/http://www.ecn.purdue.edu/ParaMount/papers/rubin87goto.pdf|archivedate=2009-03-20|deadurl=yes}}</ref>1987年5月ACM通讯又以《〈《Go To语句被认为有害》被认为有害〉被认为有害？》（''<nowiki/>'"GOTO Considered Harmful" Considered Harmful' Considered Harmful?''）为标题刊载了更多正反双方的评论。<ref name="acm_may87">{{cite journal|title=" 'GOTO Considered Harmful' Considered Harmful" Considered Harmful?|url=http://cacm.acm.org/magazines/1987/5/10097-acm-forum/abstract|date=May 1987|journal=Communications of the ACM|issue=5|doi=10.1145/22899.315729|volume=30|pages=351–355|format=PDF|author3=Michael J. Liebhaber|author4=Steven F. Lott|author5=Lee Starr|author1=Donald Moore|author2=Chuck Musciano}}</ref>戴克斯特拉本人则在题为《论一个有点令人失望的通讯》（''On a Somewhat Disappointing Correspondence''）中回应了相关争议。<ref name="ewd1009">{{cite EWD|1009|On a Somewhat Disappointing Correspondence}} (May, 1987)</ref>

实际上在《Go To语句被认为有害》一文发表前，“被认为有害”已经是记者常用的陈词滥调，例如早在1949年[[纽约时报|纽约时报]]就曾刊登过《仓促立法被认为有害》的头条。<ref name="liberman">{{cite web|url=http://itre.cis.upenn.edu/~myl/languagelog/archives/004675.html|title=Language Log: Considered harmful|accessdate=2009-08-17|author=[[Mark_Liberman|Mark Liberman]]|date=2008-04-08}}</ref>
