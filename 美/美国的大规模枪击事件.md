== 定义 ==
美國沒有對「大规模枪击事件」採用固定的定义<ref name="politifact2018">{{Cite web|url=http://www.politifact.com/truth-o-meter/article/2018/feb/14/what-we-know-about-mass-shootings/|title=What we know about mass shootings|accessdate=20 February 2018|date=February 14, 2018|last=Greenberg, Jacobson & Valverde|first=Jon, Louis & Miriam|publisher=[[Tampa_Bay_Times|Tampa Bay Times]]|quote=As noted above, there is no widely accepted definition of mass shootings. People use either broad or restrictive definitions of mass shootings to reinforce their stance on gun control.  After the 2012 Sandy Hook school shooting, Congress defined "mass killings" as three or more homicides in a single incident. The definition was intended to clarify when the U.S. Attorney General could assist state and local authorities in investigations of violent acts and shootings in places of public use.}}</ref>。2003年1月，美国国会通过了一项有关暴力犯罪的法案（The Investigative Assistance for Violent Crimes Act of 2012），该法案定义“大规模杀害事件”为除凶手自身以外，至少三人死亡的事件<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/112/hr2076/text|title=H.R. 2076 (112th): Investigative Assistance for Violent Crimes Act of 2012|accessdate=20 February 2018|publisher=[[United_States_Congress|United States Congress]]|quote="''(I)the term mass killings means 3 or more killings in a single incident; ''"}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/wonk/wp/2015/12/03/what-makes-a-mass-shooting-in-america/|title=What makes a 'mass shooting' in America|last=Ingraham|first=Christopher|date=3 December 2015|work=Washington Post|accessdate=5 November 2017|quote="''But starting in 2013, federal statutes defined "mass killing" as three or more people killed, regardless of weapons.''"}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.motherjones.com/mojo/2012/08/what-is-a-mass-shooting|title=What Exactly Is A Mass Shooting|accessdate=August 9, 2015|last=Follman|first=Mark|publisher=Mother Jones|quote="''In January 2013, a mandate for federal investigation of mass shootings authorized by President Barack Obama lowered that baseline to three or more victims killed. ''"}}</ref>。2015年，[[國會研究處|美国国会研究服务]]定义大规模枪击事件为“在事件發生及其相邻区域内，至少四名受害者被枪击杀害的多重杀人犯罪”<ref name="fas">{{Cite web|url=https://fas.org/sgp/crs/misc/R44126.pdf|title=Mass Murder with Firearms: Incidents and Victims, 1999-2013|accessdate=21 February 2018|date=July 30, 2015|last=Krouse|first=William J.|format=pdf|publisher=Congressional Research Service}}</ref>。非营利组织“枪支暴力档案”所使用的宽泛定义则是“除凶手自身以外，至少四人被枪击（无论死亡与否）的事件”<ref name="GVA Methodology">{{Cite web|url=http://www.gunviolencearchive.org/methodology|title=General Methodology|accessdate=21 February 2018}}</ref>，而此定义也往往为新闻界和非盈利组织所采用<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.politifact.com/california/article/2017/oct/04/mass-shooting-what-does-it-mean/|title=How is a ‘mass shooting’ defined?|accessdate=21 February 2018|date=4 October 2017|last=Nichols|first=Chris}}</ref><ref name="CNN2">{{Cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2015/08/27/health/u-s-most-mass-shootings/|title=Why the U.S. has the most mass shootings|last=Christensen|first=Jen|date=August 28, 2015|publisher=CNN}}</ref><ref name="shooting tracker">{{Cite web|url=https://www.massshootingtracker.org/about|title=About the Mass Shooting Tracker|accessdate=13 June 2016}}</ref><ref name="MyUser_BBC_13_June_2016c">{{Cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-us-canada-36519450|title=Orlando club shootings: Full fury of gun battle emerges|accessdate=13 June 2016|date=13 June 2016}} Cites Mass Shooting Tracker</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/are-americans-becoming-numb-to-mass-shootings/|title=Are Americans becoming "numb" to mass shootings?|accessdate=21 February 2018|date=6 November 2017|last=Axelrod|first=Jim}}</ref>。
