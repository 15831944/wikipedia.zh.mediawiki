In [[arithmetic|arithmetic]] and [[algebra|algebra]], the '''fifth [[exponentiation|power]]''' of a number ''n'' is the result of multiplying five instances of ''n'' together. So:
:{{math|size=120%|1=''n''<sup>5</sup> = ''n'' × ''n'' × ''n'' × ''n'' × ''n''}}.

通過將[[四次冪|四次冪]]乘以一個數的[[平方數|平方數]]乘以[[立方數|立方數]]，也可以形成五次冪。

The sequence of fifth powers of [[integer|integer]]s is:
 
:[[0|0]], [[1|1]], [[32|32]], [[243|243]], [[1024|1024]], [[3125|3125]], [[7776|7776]], 16807, 32768, 59049, 100000, 161051, 248832, 371293, 537824, 759375, 1048576, 1419857, 1889568, 2476099, 3200000, 4084101, 5153632, 6436343, 7962624, 9765625, ... {{OEIS|id=A000584}}


==屬性==

整數n的五次冪的最後一位，以10為基數，是n的最後一位。

By the [[Abel–Ruffini_theorem|Abel–Ruffini theorem]], there is no general [[algebraic_formula|algebraic formula]] (formula expressed in terms of [[radical_expression|radical expression]]s) for the solution of [[polynomial_equation|polynomial equation]]s containing a fifth power of the [[unknown_(mathematics)|unknown]] as their highest power. This is the lowest power for which this is true. See [[quintic_equation|quintic equation]], [[sextic_equation|sextic equation]], and [[septic_equation|septic equation]].

Along with the fourth power, the fifth power is one of two powers ''k'' that can be expressed as the sum of ''k'' − 1 other ''k''-th powers, providing counterexamples to [[Euler's_sum_of_powers_conjecture|Euler's sum of powers conjecture]]. Specifically,

R. |year=1966 |title=Counterexample to Euler's conjecture on sums of like powers |journal=Bull. Amer. Math. Soc. |doi=10.1090/S0002-9904-1966-11654-3 |volume=72 |issue=6 |page=1079}}</ref>
