'''密歇根－休伦湖'''（{{lang-en|Lake Michigan–Huron}}），或称为'''休伦－密歇根湖'''（{{lang-en|Lake Huron–Michigan}}），是指[[北美洲|北美洲]][[五大湖|五大湖]]中[[密歇根湖|密歇根湖]]与[[休伦湖|休伦湖]]共同组成的水域，其间由5英里（合8千米）宽、20英寻（合37米）深的[[麦基诺水道|麦基诺水道]]相连。 <ref name="Grady">{{cite book |last= Grady |first=Wayne |title= The Great Lakes|publisher= Greystone Books and [[David_Suzuki_Foundation|David Suzuki Foundation]]| location=Vancouver |year= 2007 |isbn= 978-1-55365-197-0 |pages= 42–43}}</ref><ref>"[http://www.infoplease.com/ipa/A0001804.html Michigan and Huron: One Lake or Two]?" Pearson Education, Inc: Information Please Database, 2007.</ref>麦基诺水道使密歇根湖与休伦湖保持着相等的水平面。尽管麦基诺水道的流向一般为自西向东，但根据当地水文条件的变化，其流向有时也会改变。从[[水文学|水文学]]的角度而言，这意味着密歇根湖与休伦湖应属于同一个水体，成为一个单一的湖泊，即密歇根－休伦湖。<ref name="Egan">{{cite web |url=http://www.jsonline.com/news/wisconsin/international-body-urges-engineering-a-boost-in-huron-michigan-lake-water-levels-mu9nh5f-204862971.html| title=International commission wants to look at engineering fix to boost Huron, Michigan levels | publisher=Milwaukee Journal Sentinel Inc. |work=JSOnline | date=April 26, 2013 | accessdate=2013-04-28 | author=Egan, Dan}}</ref><ref name=GLERL>"Lakes Michigan and Huron are considered to be one lake hydraulically because of their connection through the deep Straits of Mackinac." 密歇根－休伦湖的湖泊面积为45300平方英里（合11万7300平方千米），是[[湖泊面积列表|世界第一大淡水湖]]（如将休伦湖与密歇根湖分开计算，它们分别为世界第三与第四大淡水湖，此时[[苏必利尔湖|苏必利尔湖]]为第一大淡水湖）。<ref name=DEQ>{{cite web|url=http://www.michigan.gov/deq/0,4561,7-135-3313_3677-15926--,00.html |title=Great Lakes Map |publisher=[[Michigan_Department_of_Environmental_Quality|Michigan Department of Environmental Quality]] |accessdate=20 September 2012}}</ref><ref name=CG>David Lees in ''Canadian Geographic'' writes, "Contrary to popular belief, the largest lake in the world is not Lake Superior but mighty Lake Michigan–Huron, which is a single hydrological unit linked at the Straits of Mackinac.
