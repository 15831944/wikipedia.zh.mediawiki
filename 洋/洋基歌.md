《'''洋基歌'''》（''Yankee Doodle''）是一首[[美国|美国]][[传统|传统]][[歌曲|歌曲]]，其起源可追溯至美国[[七年战争|七年战争]]时期。今天这首歌在美国通常被当作[[爱国|爱国]]歌曲，它同时還是[[康乃狄克州|康乃狄克州]]的州歌<ref>[http://www.ct.gov/sots/cwp/view.asp?a=3188&q=392608 STATE OF CONNECTICUT, Sites º Seals º Symbols] {{Wayback|url=http://www.ct.gov/sots/cwp/view.asp?a=3188&q=392608 |date=20101210041918 }}; ''Connecticut State Register & Manual''; retrieved on May 23, 2008</ref>。

这首歌的真正源头仍不清楚<ref name="SSBHCYD">{{Cite book | last=Sonneck | first=Oscar George Theodore | authorlink=Oscar George Theodore Sonneck | coauthors= | title=Report on The Star-spangled Banner, Hail Columbia, America, Yankee Doodle | date= | publisher=New York, Dover Publications [1972] | location= | isbn=0-486-22237-3 | page=}}</ref>。常見的说法认为这首歌是在[[美国独立战争|美国独立战争]]以前英军用以嘲笑殖民地居民粗俗的衣着和举止的，而“[[洋基|洋基]]”一词是对参加[[法印战争|法印战争]]的[[新英格兰|新英格兰]]人的轻蔑之词。这首歌的曲调很可能来自与[[儿歌|儿歌]]《露茜的钱袋》（''Lucy Locket''），其中一个版本的歌词可能来自于一名英国外科医生理查德·沙克伯勒（Richard Shuckburgh）<ref>{{Cite book | author=A. Lomax, John; Lomax, Alan | authorlink= | coauthors= | title=American ballads and folk songs | date=1994 | publisher=Dover | location=New York  | isbn=978-0-486-28276-3 | page=521 | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Dn0cSe2ecuoC&}}</ref>。虽然这首歌的歌词带有贬义，然而独立战争期间[[美军|美军]]却采用《洋基歌》作爲他们自己的歌以反諷敵軍，表明他们对自己朴素的家纺衣着和毫不矫揉造作的举止感到自豪。洋基歌的歌词有许多不同版本。多年来，这首歌在美国一直被当作非正式的国歌，也是最受欢迎的[[儿歌|儿歌]]。在各类[[电影|电影]]，[[电视|电视]]和[[动画片|动画片]]中时常能听见这首歌曲。

== 歌词 ==
今天洋基歌最常見版本的第一段歌词如下：
{|
!（[[英文|英文]]原词）
!（[[中文|中文]]意译）
|--
| valign=top |
{{lang|en|
:Yankee Doodle went to town,
:A-Riding on a pony;
:He stuck a feather in his cap,
:And called it macaroni.}}
| valign=top |
:洋基傻小子跑进城，
:骑着隻小马驹，
:他在帽子里插根羽毛，
:叫它做通心粉式假发；
|}
'''Doodle'''这个词最早出现在17世纪初<ref>"doodle", n, Oxford English Dictionary. Accessed April 29th, 2009.</ref>，可能源于[[低地德语|低地德语]]的'''dudel'''或'''dödel'''，意思是“蠢人”或“傻瓜”。而'''{{tsl|en|Macaroni (fashion)|macaroni}}'''（“通心粉”）指的是一种流行于1770年代的假发，在同时期也成了對趕時髦人士的蔑稱<ref>J. Woodforde, ''The Strange Story of False Hair'' (London: Taylor & Francis, 1971), p. 40.</ref>。第一段歌词可能暗示着美国人（洋基佬）没见过世面，以为在头上插根羽毛就算时尚了<ref>R. Ross, ''Clothing: a global history : or, The Imperialists' new clothes'' (Polity, 2008), p. 51.</ref>。

=== 早期版本 ===
最早版本的歌词大约形成在1755年或1758年，具体时间还存有争议<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.ajc.com/metro/content/news/stories/2008/07/05/yankeedoodle.html?cxntlid=inform_sr|title=Wish "Yankee Doodle" a happy 250th birthday. Maybe.|last=Carola|first=Chris|date=07/05/08|agency=Associated Press|accessdate=2009-09-10}}</ref>：
{|
!（[[英文|英文]]原词）
!（[[中文|中文]]意译）
|--
| valign=top |
{{lang|en|
:Brother Ephraim sold his Cow
:And bought him a Commission;
:And then he went to Canada
:To fight for the Nation;

:But when Ephraim he came home
:He proved an arrant Coward,
:He wouldn't fight the Frenchmen there
:For fear of being devour'd.}}
| valign=top |
:埃夫莱姆兄弟卖了他的奶牛，
:给自己买了个军职，
:随后他跑到加拿大，
:好为国家作战；

:但是等到埃夫莱姆回到家，
:大家才发现他是个十足的懦夫，
:他不愿在那儿抗击法国人，
:因为他害怕被活吞了；
|}

其中的埃夫莱姆影射的是战死于[[乔治湖战役|乔治湖战役]]的马萨诸塞州陆军上校[[埃夫莱姆·威廉姆斯|埃夫莱姆·威廉姆斯]]（Ephraim Williams）。

英国人经常与某个版本的歌词联系上，此版本歌词与一个名为[[托马斯·迪特森|托马斯·迪特森]]的马萨诸塞州[[比勒瑞卡|比勒瑞卡]]镇人有关。迪特森在1775年3月在[[波士顿|波士顿]]因为试图购买[[滑膛枪|滑膛枪]]而被英国人[[涂柏油、粘羽毛|涂柏油、粘羽毛]]：
{|
!（[[英文|英文]]原词）
!（[[中文|中文]]意译）
|--
| valign=top |
{{lang|en|
:Yankee Doodle came to town,
:For to buy a firelock,
:We will tar and feather him,
:And so we will John Hancock.}}
| valign=top |
:洋基傻小子跑进城，
:想要买上把燧火枪，
:我们要把他涂上柏油粘上羽毛，
:我们对[[约翰·汉考克|约翰·汉考克]]也要这么干；
|}

也正因为这首歌，比勒瑞卡镇宣称它是洋基歌中傻小子的“故乡”<ref>[http://www.bcmm.org/yankee_doodle.htm The Billerica Colonial Minute Men]{{dead link|date=2018年1月 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}; ''The Thomas Ditson story''; retrieved on July 10, 2008</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billericalibrary.org/main/genealogy/yankee.htm |title=存档副本 |accessdate=2009-06-26 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090626220816/http://www.billericalibrary.org/main/genealogy/yankee.htm |archivedate=2009-06-26 }}; ''Town History and Genealogy''; retrieved on October 20, 2008</ref>，同时认为正是从此开始，美国人把这首本来是用以嘲笑他们的歌拿来嘲笑英国人。在[[列克星敦和康科德战役|列克星敦和康科德战役]]之后，一家波士顿报纸作了如下报导：
“在被民兵赶回波士顿以后，一个英国人问他的军官兄弟对这首歌有什么看法，——‘去他的，’他回答道，‘他们逼着我们就着这歌跳舞，直到跳不动了为止。’——从那以后他们就不再喜欢这首歌了。”

=== 完整版本 ===
今天大家熟知的完整版本歌词如下<ref>Gen. George P. Morris - "Original Yankee Words", ''The Patriotic Anthology'', Doubleday, Doran & Company, Inc. publishers, 1941. Introduction by Carl Van Doren. Literary Guild of America, Inc., New York N.Y.</ref>：
{{lang|en|
:Yankee Doodle went to town
:A-riding on a pony,
:Stuck a feather in his cap
:And called it macaroni'.

:''Chorus'':
:Yankee Doodle keep it up,
:Yankee Doodle dandy,
:Mind the music and the step,
:And with the girls be handy.

:Fath'r and I went down to camp,
:Along with Captain Gooding,
:And there we saw the men and boys
:As thick as hasty puddin'.

:''Chorus''

:And there we saw a thousand men
:As rich as Squire David,
:And what they wasted every day,
:I wish it could be saved.

:''Chorus''

:The 'lasses they eat it every day,
:Would keep a house a winter;
:They have so much, that I'll be bound,
:They eat it when they've mind ter.

:''Chorus''

:And there I see a swamping gun
:Large as a log of maple,
:Upon a deuced little cart,
:A load for father's cattle.

:''Chorus''

:And every time they shoot it off,
:It takes a horn of powder,
:and makes a noise like father's gun,
:Only a nation louder.

:''Chorus''

:I went as nigh to one myself
:As 'Siah's inderpinning;
:And father went as nigh again,
:I thought the deuce was in him.

:''Chorus''

:Cousin Simon grew so bold,
:I thought he would have cocked it;
:It scared me so I shrinked it off
:And hung by father's pocket.

:''Chorus''

:And Cap'n Davis had a gun,
:He kind of clapt his hand on't
:And stuck a crooked stabbing iron
:Upon the little end on't

:''Chorus''

:And there I see a pumpkin shell
:As big as mother's bason,
:And every time they touched it off
:They scampered like the nation.

:''Chorus''

:I see a little barrel too,
:The heads were made of leather;
:They knocked on it with little clubs
:And called the folks together.

:''Chorus''

:And there was Cap'n Washington,
:And gentle folks about him;
:They say he's grown so 'tarnal proud
:He will not ride without em'.

:''Chorus''

:He got him on his meeting clothes,
:Upon a slapping stallion;
:He sat the world along in rows,
:In hundreds and in millions.

:''Chorus''

:The flaming ribbons in his hat,
:They looked so tearing fine, ah,
:I wanted dreadfully to get
:To give to my Jemima.

:''Chorus''

:I see another snarl of men
:A digging graves they told me,
:So 'tarnal long, so 'tarnal deep,
:They 'tended they should hold me.

:''Chorus''

:It scared me so, I hooked it off,
:Nor stopped, as I remember,
:Nor turned about till I got home,
:Locked up in mother's chamber.

:''Chorus''}}

== 文化影响 ==
* 台湾歌手[[阿雅|阿雅]]的《剉冰进行曲》，其主旋律即是来自《洋基歌》。
* [[美国之音|美国之音]]的广播节目总以《洋基歌》开头和结束<ref name="Berg, Jerome S. 1999, page 104">Berg, Jerome S. ''On the Short Waves, 1923-1945: Broadcast Listening in the Pioneer Days of Radio''. 1999, McFarland. ISBN 978-0-7864-0506-0, page 104 </ref>。
* [[詹姆斯·卡格尼|詹姆斯·卡格尼]]主演的1942年电影《[[勝利之歌|勝利之歌]]》主题曲即是《洋基歌》。
* 日本歌曲《阿尔卑斯一万尺》的旋律即来自《洋基歌》。
