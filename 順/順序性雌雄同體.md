'''順序性雌雄同體''' 是一種發生在許多[[魚類|魚類]][[腹足綱|腹足綱]]、[[植物|植物]]之中的[[雌雄同體|雌雄同體]]現象，例如[[小丑魚|小丑魚]]。「順序性雌雄同體」的發生時機為一個生物個體轉變性別的時候。性別轉換的方向可以是雌性變為雄性，也可以是由雄性變為雌性。該生物的性腺在轉換性別時，可以同時擁有雌性和雄性配子，也可以完全改變性別。該現象發生於其生命周期的後期。<ref name='carauth'>{{cite journal | title = Freshwater cichlid ''Crenicara punctulata'' is a protogynous sequential hermaphrodite | journal = Copeia | year = 2000 | first = L. L | last = Carruth | volume = 2000 | pages = 71–82 | doi=10.1643/0045-8511(2000)2000[0071:fccpia]2.0.co;2}}</ref> 花朵的個體也可以稱為是順序性雌雄同體的一種， 即使該株植物的雄花以及雌花可能是分開的。

===雌性先熟===
[[image:Thalassoma_lunare_1.jpg|thumb]]，一個雌性先熟的物種。]]

'''雌性先熟雌雄同體'''意指出生時為雌性，並在其生命的一個時間點轉換性別為雄性的生物。 隨著該生物成長，將因內部或外部刺激， 而改變性別成為雄性。雄性的[[fecundity|fecundity]]隨著年齡大幅增加，和雌性不同。

雌性先熟是大自然之中，最常見的順序性雌雄同體型態。<ref name='Avise'>{{cite journal | title=Evolutionary Perspectives on Hermaphroditism in Fishes | journal=Sexual Development | year=2009 | first=JC | last=Avise |author2=JE Mank  | volume=3 | pages=152–163| id=| doi=10.1159/000223079}}</ref> 500種順序性雌雄同體的魚類之中，大概有75% 的物種是雌性先熟的。 <ref>{{cite book|last1=Pauly|first1=Daniel|title=Darwin's Fishes: An Encyclopedia of Ichthyology, Ecology, and Evolution|date=2004|publisher=Cambridge University Press|isbn=9781139451819|page=108|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=lTDa0fM4vQIC|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last=Pandian|first=TJ|title=Genetic Sex Differentiation in Fish|year=2012|publisher=Science Publishers|location=Boca Raton, FL}}</ref> 這類型的順序性雌雄同體的普遍生物為[[隆頭魚科|隆頭魚科]]的。 They are one of the largest families of coral reef fish and belong to the family Labridae. 隆投頭魚位於世界上所有marine habitats and tend to bury themselves in sand at night or when they feel threatened.<ref name='Animal Planet'>{{cite web | url=http://animal.discovery.com/guides/fish/marine/wrasseintro.html | title=Animal Planet :: Fish Guide -- Wrasse | accessdate=2011-03-28 | publisher=PetEducation.com | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110302092156/http://animal.discovery.com/guides/fish/marine/wrasseintro.html | archive-date=2011-03-02 | dead-url=yes }}</ref> 在隆頭魚科的動物中，最大的兩條魚為雄性，而較小型的為雌性。 在多數狀況下，雌性魚和未熟的魚有著均一，較單調的顏色，而雄性魚最後則有著體色雙色的階段。 <ref name='Warner R'/> Large males hold territories and try to pair spawn while small to mid-size initial-phase males live with females and group [[Spawn_(biology)|spawn]].<ref name='Adreani'>{{cite journal | title=Mating system and reproductive biology of a temperate wrasse, ''Halichoeres semicinctus'' | journal=Copeia | year=2008 | first=M. S. | last=Adreani |author2=Allen, L. G. | pages=467–475 | doi=10.1643/cp-06-265 | volume=2008}}</ref> In other words, both the initial and terminal phase males can breed; they differ however in the way they do it.

In the [[California_sheephead|California sheephead]] (''Semicossyphus pulcher'')，一種隆頭魚，當雌性個體轉變為雄性時， the ovaries degenerate and spermatogenic crypts appear in the gonads. The general structure of the gonads remains ovarian after the transformation and the sperm is transported through a series of ducts on the periphery of the gonad and [[oviduct|oviduct]]. 性別的改變和年齡相關。 For example, the California sheephead stays a female for four years before changing sex.<ref name='Warner R'>{{cite journal | title=The reproductive biology of the Protogynous hermaphrodite Pimelometopon Pulchrum (Pisces: Labridae). | journal=Fishery Bulletin | year=1975 | first=R.R | last=Warner | volume=73 | pages=261–283| id=| accessdate=2011-04-27}}</ref>

[[Thalassoma_bifasciatum|Blue-headed wrasse]]在生命開始時可以是雄性或雌性，但雌性可以改變性別並擁有雄性生殖功能。 年幼的雌性及雄性魚擁有不同的體色，known as the "Initial Phase" before progressing into the "Terminal Phase" coloration, which has a change in intensity of顏色、條紋。 Initial Phase males have larger [[testes|testes]] than larger, terminal phase males, which enables the initial phase males to produce a large amount of sperm. This strategy is able to compete with that of the larger male, who is able to guard his own harem.

''[[Botryllus_schlosseri|Botryllus schlosseri]]'', a colonial [[tunicate|tunicate]], is a protogynous hermaphrodite.  In a colony, eggs are ovulated about two days before the peak of sperm emission.<ref name="pmid25044771">{{cite journal |author1=Gasparini F |author2=Manni L. |author3=Cima F. |author4=Zaniolo G |author5=Burighel P |author6=Caicci F |author7=Franchi N |author8=Schiavon F |author9=Rigon F |author10=Campagna D |author11=Ballarin L |title=Sexual and asexual reproduction in the colonial ascidian Botryllus schlosseri |journal=Genesis |volume= 53|issue= |pages= 105–20| date=July  2014 |pmid=25044771 |doi=10.1002/dvg.22802 |url=}}</ref>  Although self-fertilization is avoided and cross-fertilization favored by this strategy, self-fertilization is still possible. These findings suggest that self-fertilization gives rise to inbreeding depression associated with developmental deficits that are likely caused by expression of deleterious recessive mutations.<ref>{{cite journal | last1=Bernstein | first1=H. | last2=Hopf | first2=F. ===最終後果===

[[Michael_Ghiselin|Michael Ghiselin]] proposed three models for hermaphroditism in 1969 in his paper "The evolution of hermaphroditism among animals".<ref name='Ghiselin'>{{cite journal | title=The evolution of hermaphroditism among animals | journal=The Quarterly Review of Biology | year=1969 | first=Michael T. |authorlink=Michael Ghiselin | last=Ghiselin | volume=44 | pages=189–208| id=| pmid=4901396 | issue=2 | doi=10.1086/406066}}</ref>

# The 'low-density model' states that individuals have characteristics that reduce the opportunity for mating; this model cannot be applied to sequential hermaphroditism.
# The 'gene dispersal model' is based on the idea that limitations on dispersal may influence population structure or genetical environment and it can be separated into two versions: the inbreeding version and the sampling-error version. This theory of gene dispersal can be applied to sequential hermaphrodites, especially the inbreeding version. The inbreeding version is based upon the fact that both protandry and protogyny help prevent inbreeding in plants and thus one can make the same assumption that in animals it works by reducing the probability of this occurring among siblings. The sampling-error version is based on the reality that the genetical environment is influenced by genetic drift and similar phenomena in small populations. The two aspects of these hypotheses influenced by hermaphroditism, that is inbreeding and sampling-error, result in the same thing — reduction of genetic variability. In other words, being a hermaphrodite would increase genetic variability and thus be considered advantageous to the population. 
# Lastly, the 'size-advantage model' states that reproductive functions are carried out better if the individual is a certain size/age. To create selection for hermaphroditism, small individuals must have higher reproductive fitness as one sex and larger individuals must have higher reproductive fitness as the opposite sex. With this size-distribution pattern, an individual would maximize its fitness if it reproduced as a sequential hermaphrodite. For example, eggs are larger than sperm, thus if you are a big you are able to make more eggs so being female when big is advantageous, however the size advantage relationship is really not as simple as the example just mentioned, but it allows for a better understanding.

In most [[ectotherms|ectotherms]] body size and female fecundity are positively correlated.<ref name='Warner'>{{cite journal | title=The Adaptive Significance of Sequential Hermaphroditism in Animals. | journal=The American Naturalist | year=1975 | first=R. R. | last=Warner | volume=109 | pages=61–82| id=| doi=10.1086/282974}}</ref> This supports Ghiselin’s size-advantage model, which is still widely accepted today. Kazancioglu and Alonzo (2010) performed the first comparative analysis of sex change in [[Labridae|Labridae]]. Their analysis supports the size-advantage model by Ghiselin and suggest that sequential hermaphroditism is correlated to the size-advantage. They determined that [[dioecy|dioecy]] was less likely to occur when the size advantage is stronger than other advantages<ref name='Alonzo'>{{cite journal | title=A comparative analysis of sex change in Labridae supports the size advantage hypothesis | journal=Evolution; international journal of organic evolution | year=2010 | first=E | last=Kazancioğlu |author2=SH Alonzo  | volume=64 | pages=2254–64| id=| pmid=20394662 | doi=10.1111/j.1558-5646.2010.01016.x | issue=8}}</ref>

Warner suggests that selection for protandry may occur in populations where female fecundity is augmented with age and individuals mate randomly. Selection for protogyny may occur where there are traits in the population that depress male fecundity at early ages (territoriality, mate selection or inexperience) and when female fecundity is decreased with age, the latter seems to be rare in the field.<ref name='Warner'/> An example of territoriality favoring protogyny occurs when there is a need to protect their habitat and being a large male is advantageous for this purpose. In the mating aspect, a large male has a higher chance of mating, while this has no effect on the female mating fitness.<ref name='Alonzo'/> Thus, he suggests that female fecundity has more impact on sequential hermaphroditism than the age structures of the population.<ref name='Warner'/>

The size-advantage model predicts that sex change would only be absent if the relationship between size/age with reproductive potential is identical in both sexes. With this prediction one would assume that hermaphroditism is very common, but this is not the case. Sequential hermaphroditism is very rare and according to scientists this is due to some cost that decreases fitness in sex changers as opposed to those who don’t change sex. Some of the hypotheses proposed for the dearth of hermaphrodites are the energetic cost of sex change, genetic and/or physiological barriers to sex change, and sex-specific mortality rates.<ref name='Warner' /><ref name='Charnov'>{{cite journal | title=Size Advantage May Not Always Favor Sex Change | journal=Journal of Theoretical Biology | year=1986 | first=E | last=Charnov | volume=119 | pages=283–285| id=| pmid= | doi= 10.1016/s0022-5193(86)80141-2| issue=}}</ref><ref name='Munday'>{{cite journal | title=The energetic cost of protogynous versus protandrous sex change in the bi-directional sex changing fish Gobiodon histrio | journal=Marine Biology | year=2002 | first=P | last=Munday|author2=BW Molony  | volume=141 | pages=429–446| id=| pmid= | doi= 10.1007/s00227-002-0904-8| issue=}}</ref>

In 2009, Kazanciglu and Alonzo found that dioecy was only favored when the cost of changing sex was very large. This indicates that the cost of sex change does not explain the rarity of sequential hermaphroditism by itself.<ref name='Kazancioğlu'>{{cite journal | title=Costs of changing sex do not explain why sequential hermaphroditism is rare | journal=The American Naturalist | year=2009 | first=E | last=Kazancioğlu |author2=SH Alonzo  | volume=173 | pages=327–36| id=| pmid=19199519 | doi=10.1086/596539 | issue=3}}</ref>

===近因===
Many studies have focused on the proximate causes of sequential hermaphroditism. The role of [[aromatase|aromatase]] has been widely studied in this area. Aromatase is an [[enzyme|enzyme]] that controls the [[androgen|androgen]]/[[estrogen|estrogen]] ratio in animals by catalyzing the conversion of [[testosterone|testosterone]] into [[oestradiol|oestradiol]], which is irreversible. It has been discovered that the aromatase pathway mediates sex change in both directions.<ref name='Kroon'>{{cite journal | title=Aromatase pathway mediates sex change in each direction. | journal=[[Proceedings_of_the_Royal_Society_B|Proceedings of the Royal Society B]] | year=2005 | first=F. J. | last=Kroon |author2= Munday, P. L. |author3=Westcott, D. |author4=Hobbs, J.-P. |author5=Liley, N. R. | volume=272 | pages=1399–405 | pmc=1560338 | pmid=16006326 | doi=10.1098/rspb.2005.3097 | issue=1570}}</ref> Many studies also involve understanding the effect of aromatase inhibitors on sex change. One such study was performed by Kobayashi et al. In their study they tested the role of estrogens in male three-spot wrasses (''Halichoeres trimaculatus''). They discovered that fish treated with aromatase inhibitors showed decreased gonodal weight, plasma estrogen level and spermatogonial proliferation in the testis as well as increased androgen levels. Their results suggest that estrogens are important in the regulation of [[spermatogenesis|spermatogenesis]] in this protogynous hermaphrodite.<ref name='Kobayashi'>{{cite journal | title=Role of estrogen in spermatogenesis in initial phase males of the three-spot wrasse (''Halichoeres trimaculatus''): wffect of aromatase inhibitor on the testis. | journal=Developmental Dynamics | year=2011 | first=y | last=Kobayashi |author2=Nozu R |author3=Nakamura M.  | volume=240 | pages=116–121| id=| url=https://www.scopus.com/inward/record.url?eid=2-s2.0-78650344417&partnerID=40&md5=847189402b2dcdb18259e2dc89206111 | accessdate=2011-04-27 | doi=10.1002/dvdy.22507 | pmid=21117145}}</ref>

===遺傳後果===
順序性雌形同體的物種的性別比通常都近似於其出生時的性別, 而且於轉換性別之後出現更多的成功繁殖。理論上它們會減少[[遺傳多樣性|遺傳多樣性]]以及[[有效種群大小|有效種群大小]]。 然而，一個在南非海域對於兩個生態學上相似的物種，[[紫背冬鯛|紫背冬鯛]] ([[兩性異形|兩性異形]])以及[[隆背麗眼鯛|隆背麗眼鯛]] (雌性先熟) 的調查發現，該兩物種的物種多樣性十分相似，而當有效族群大小在變性期間快速下降時，它們在相對較短的時間內依然是相似的。<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Coscia |first1=I. |last2=Chopelet|first2=J. |last3=Waples|first3=R. S. |last4=Mann|first4=B. Q. |last5=Mariani|first5=S. |date=2016 |title=Sex change and effective population size: implications for population genetic studies in marine fish|url=http://usir.salford.ac.uk/39110/|journal=Heredity |volume=117 |pages=251–258 |doi=10.1038/hdy.2016.50 |accessdate=5 January 2017|via=}}</ref>
