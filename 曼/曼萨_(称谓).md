'''曼萨'''（Mansa）是[[曼丁卡语|曼丁卡语]]词汇，意为统治者，即[[国王|国王]]或[[皇帝|皇帝]]，亦可与[[伊斯兰教|伊斯兰教]]的称谓[[苏丹_(称谓)|苏丹]]对应，是[[中世纪|中世纪]][[西非|西非]][[马里帝国|马里帝国]]的君主称谓。马里帝国在13世纪至15世纪支配西非，曼萨垄断贸易，从而极度富有。[[松迪亚塔|松迪亚塔·凯塔]]是马里帝国的第一位曼萨，他开创了马里帝国[[凯塔王朝|凯塔王朝]]的统治。历史上其他著名的曼萨还有[[乌利一世|乌利一世]]和[[曼萨·穆萨|穆萨一世]]<ref>[https://books.google.co.il/books?id=o5L-T9e4KY8C&pg=PA154&dq=Mansa,+meaning+Sultan+or+king&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjEif22jr7JAhUDXhoKHRHQDZ8Q6AEIITAB#v=onepage&q=Mansa%2C%20meaning%20Sultan%20or%20king&f=false An Interview with Ibn Battuta], Kathleen Knoblock, ''Primary Source Fluency Activities: World Cultures'' (In Sub-Saharan Africa), pub. Shell Education 2007 {{ISBN|978-1-4258-0102-1}}</ref><ref>[https://books.google.co.il/books?id=zKqn_CWTxYEC&pg=PA324&dq=Mansa,+meaning+Sultan+or+king&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwj0jLWikr7JAhWBuxQKHRvVBd0Q6AEIQTAG#v=onepage&q=Mansa%2C%20meaning%20Sultan%20or%20king&f=false Travels in Asia and Africa, 1325-1354], by Ibn Battuta, London 2005, p. 324 {{ISBN|0-415-34473-5}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last=Jansen |first=Jan |title=Hot Issues: The 1997 Kamabolon Ceremony in Kangaba (Mali) |journal=[[The_International_Journal_of_African_Historical_Studies|The International Journal of African Historical Studies]] |volume=31 |issue=2 |pages= 253–278 |jstor= 221083 |date=1998 |registration=yes }} On page 256, Jan Jansen writes: “''Mansa'' is generally translated as 'king,' 'ruler' or 'ancestor.'
