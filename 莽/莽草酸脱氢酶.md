'''莽草酸脱氢酶'''（{{lang-en|shikimate dehydrogenase}}，[[EC编号|EC]] [http://enzyme.expasy.org/EC/1.1.1.25 1.1.1.25]）是一种以[[烟酰胺腺嘌呤二核苷酸磷酸|NADP<sup>+</sup>]]为[[受体_(生物化学)|受体]]、作用于[[供体|供体]][[次甲基|CH]]-[[羟基|OH]][[基团|基团]]上的[[氧化还原酶|氧化还原酶]]。这种[[酶|酶]]能[[催化|催化]]以下[[酶促反应|酶促反应]]：

:[[莽草酸|莽草酸]] + [[烟酰胺腺嘌呤二核苷酸磷酸|NADP<sup>+</sup>]] <math>\rightleftharpoons</math> [[3-脱氢莽草酸|3-脱氢莽草酸]] + NADH + [[氢离子|H<sup>+</sup>]]

上述反应中的NADP<sup>+</sup>不能以NAD<sup>+</sup>替代<ref>{{cite journal | author = Yaniv H, Gilvarg C | date = 1955 | title = Aromatic biosynthesis. XIV. 5-Dehydroshikimic reductase | journal = J. Biol. ==功能==
Shikimate dehydrogenase is an enzyme that catalyzes one step of the [[shikimic_acid|shikimate pathway]]. This pathway is found in bacteria, plants, fungi, algae, and parasites and is responsible for the [[biosynthesis|biosynthesis]] of aromatic amino acids ([[phenylalanine|phenylalanine]], [[tyrosine|tyrosine]], and [[tryptophan|tryptophan]]) from the metabolism of carbohydrates. In contrast, animals and humans lack this pathway hence products of this biosynthetic route are [[essential_amino_acid|essential amino acid]]s that must be obtained through an animal's diet.

There are seven enzymes that play a role in this pathway. Shikimate dehydrogenase (also known as 3-dehydroshikimate dehydrogenase) is the fourth step of the seven step process. This step converts 3-dehydroshikimate to shikimate as well as reduces NADP<sup>+</sup> to NADPH.
