'''血管硬化'''（英文：Arterial stiffness）是{{tsl|en|Senescence||自然老化}}或[[动脉血管硬化|动脉血管硬化]]導致的結果。

[[發炎|發炎]]是[[动脉血管硬化|动脉血管硬化]]繼續發展的主要原因，因此發炎也成為大規模血管硬化的幕後推手。<ref>Mozos I, Malainer C, Horbańczuk J, Gug C, Stoian D, Luca CT, Atanasov AG. [https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28912780 Inflammatory Markers for Arterial Stiffness in Cardiovascular Diseases]. Front Immunol. 2017 Aug 31;8:1058. [https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5583158/ doi: 10.3389/fimmu.2017.01058].</ref>
血管硬化的程度與發生[[心肌梗死|心肌梗死]]及[[中風|中風]]等[[心血管疾病|心血管疾病]]的風險呈[[正相關|正相關]]（心肌梗死及中風是已開發國家的兩個主要死因）。 發生在富含[[彈性蛋白|彈性蛋白]]的[[動脈|動脈]]的許多種老化被認為與血管逐漸硬化有關。

2018年4月，{{tsl|en|American Heart Association|美國心臟醫學會}}旗下的《高血壓 Hypertension》期刊曾發布一篇論文表示，童年時期和成年時期的[[氣喘|氣喘]]病史和老年時血管硬化（包含[[主動脈|主動脈]]硬化）機率在統計上有相關性，若氣喘合併[[體重過重|體重過重]]和[[高血壓|高血壓]]等病史，則相關性將再進一步提高。<ref name="News on Heart.org 2018">{{cite web | title=Asthma as a kid may mean stiffer arteries as an adult | website=News on Heart.org | date=2018-04-09 | url=https://news.heart.org/asthma-as-a-kid-may-mean-stiffer-arteries-as-an-adult/ | access-date=2018-04-12}}</ref><ref name="Ovid Technologies (Wolters Kluwer Health) pp. 928–936">{{cite journal | title=History of Asthma From Childhood and Arterial Stiffness in Asymptomatic Young AdultsNovelty and Significance | journal=Hypertension | publisher=Ovid Technologies (Wolters Kluwer Health) | volume=71 | issue=5 | date=2018-04-09 | issn=0194-911X | doi=10.1161/hypertensionaha.118.10916 | pages=928–936}}</ref>研究人員推測，這可能與氣喘導致的血管長期[[發炎|發炎]]有關。<ref name="News on Heart.org 2018"/><ref name="Ovid Technologies (Wolters Kluwer Health) pp. 928–936"/>
論文指出，有氣喘病史的人，應該從小就定期監測[[血壓|血壓]]、更著重心臟保健，例如：不抽菸、控制[[膽固醇|膽固醇]]、[[減重|減去多餘的體重]]、健康飲食、規律[[有氧運動|運動]]。<ref name="News on Heart.org 2018"/><ref name="Ovid Technologies (Wolters Kluwer Health) pp. 928–936"/>

研究指出，長期規律的[[有氧運動|有氧運動]]對心臟血管健康帶來的正面影響會逆轉先天基因不良因素對心臟血管帶所來的負面影響，最終將使心血管疾病的發病率下降。<ref name="News on Heart.org 2018 II">{{cite web | title=Exercise could outsmart genetics when it comes to heart disease | website=News on Heart.org | date=2018-04-09 | url=https://news.heart.org/exercise-might-be-able-to-outsmart-genetics-when-it-comes-to-heart-disease/ | access-date=2018-04-12}}</ref><ref name="Ovid Technologies (Wolters Kluwer Health) p=CIRCULATIONAHA.117.032432">{{cite journal | title=Associations of Fitness, Physical Activity, Strength, and Genetic Risk With Cardiovascular Disease: Longitudinal Analyses in the UK Biobank Study | journal=Circulation | publisher=Ovid Technologies (Wolters Kluwer Health) | date=2018-04-09 | issn=0009-7322 | doi=10.1161/circulationaha.117.032432 | page=CIRCULATIONAHA.117.032432}}</ref>
