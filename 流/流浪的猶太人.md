'''流浪的猶太人'''（wandering Jew、徘徊的猶太人）是一個神話裡[[长生不老|长生不老]]的人，流浪的猶太人這個[[传说|传说]]在13世紀開始在[[欧洲|欧洲]]傳播。<ref>as described in the first chapter of ''Curious Myths of the Middle Ages'' where [[Sabine_Baring-Gould|Sabine Baring-Gould]] attributed the earliest extant mention of the myth of the Wandering Jew to [[Matthew_Paris|Matthew Paris]]. The chapter began with a reference to [[古斯塔夫·多雷|Gustave Doré]]'s series of twelve illustrations to the legend, and ended with a sentence remarking that, while the original legend was so 'noble in its severe simplicity' that few could develop it with success in poetry or otherwise, Doré had produced in this series 'at once a poem, a romance, and a chef-d'oeuvre of art'. First published in two parts in 1866 and 1868, the work was republished in 1877 and in many other editions.[https://archive.org/stream/curiousmythsofmi00bariuoft/curiousmythsofmi00bariuoft_djvu.txt]</ref>

原來的傳說是關於一個[[猶太人|猶太人]]在向被驅趕往[[耶穌被釘十字架|十字架]]路上走的[[耶穌被釘十字架|耶穌]]嘲弄，然後這位猶太人被詛咒在塵世行走，直到[[耶稣再临|耶稣再临]]。這位流浪者的輕率行為性質的確在不同版本的故事中是有所不同，正如他所表現出來的品格特質一樣；有時他被說成是{{le|補鞋|Shoemaking}}匠或者是其他的匠人，有時候他被說成是[[本丟·彼拉多|本丟·彼拉多]]房產的門衛。
