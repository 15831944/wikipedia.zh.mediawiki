The surface of the planet [[水星|水星]] has been divided into fifteen {{tsl|en|quadrangle (geography)|}}s, designated H-1 to H-15 (the 'H' stands for [[赫耳墨斯|赫耳墨斯]], the Greek equivalent of Mercury).<ref name="Iron Planet">{{cite book|title=Exploring Mercury: The Iron Planet|last1=Strom|first1=Robert G.|last2=Sprague|first2=Ann L.|year=2003|page=79}}</ref> The quadrangles are named for prominent surface features visible within each area.<ref name="Iron Planet"/> The quadrangles were initially named for {{tsl|en|List of albedo features on Mercury|}}, as these were the most prominent features visible before mapping was carried out by spacecraft. The mapping carried out with the images obtained by the [[水手10號|水手10號]] flybys in 1974 and 1975 led至nine of the quadrangles being renamed for newly mapped prominent features.<ref name="Iron Planet"/> The remaining six quadrangles were completely unmapped by Mariner 10 and were still referred to by their albedo feature names. Following the arrival of [[信使号|信使号]] in orbit in 2011, these six quadrangles were mapped and renamed.<ref>{{cite journal|title=IAU Information Bulletin|date=July 2011|issue=108|publisher=International Astronomical Union|url=http://www.iau.org/static/publications/IB108.pdf|format=pdf|page=23}}</ref>
