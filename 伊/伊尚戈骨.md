'''伊尚戈骨'''是一个[[骨器|骨器]]，可追溯至[[旧石器时代晚期|旧石器时代早期]]。它是一条暗褐色的狒狒的腓骨，<ref>[http://www.maths.uwa.edu.au/~schultz/3M3/history.html A very brief history of pure mathematics: The Ishango Bone] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080721075947/http://www.maths.uwa.edu.au/~schultz/3M3/history.html|date=2008-07-21}} </ref>其一端嵌有一块锋利的[[石英|石英]]，也许为了用于雕刻。一开始它被认为是一根记数棍，因为上面的三列刻痕被解释为计数符号，但是也有人认为这些刻痕只是为了方便抓握，或者其他的非数学的理由。<ref>{{Cite book|title=How Mathematics Happened: The First 50,000 Years|last=Rudman|first=Peter Strom|publisher=Prometheus Books|year=2007|isbn=978-1-59102-477-4|page=63}}</ref>

伊尚戈骨于1960年由比利时人Jean de Heinzelin de Braucourt发现，当时他正在探索[[比屬剛果|比属刚果]]区域。<ref>de Heinzelin, Jean: "Ishango", ''[[科学美国人|Scientific American]]'', 206:6 (June 1962) 105--116.</ref>发掘之处位于塞姆利基河附近的伊尚戈地区。<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ishango.naturalsciences.be/Flash/flash_local/Ishango-04-EN.html|title=Lake Edward|accessdate=12 October 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160623215609/http://ishango.naturalsciences.be/Flash/flash_local/Ishango-04-EN.html|archive-date=2016-06-23|dead-url=yes}}</ref>[[爱德华湖|爱德华湖]]汇入塞姆利河，形成了[[尼罗河|尼罗河流域]]的部分源头（今[[乌干达|乌干达]]和[[刚果民主共和国|刚果民主共和国]]的边境上）。骨头发现的地方是非洲这个区域一个小型的渔猎采集社区的遗迹。这个定居点焚毁于一次[[火山|火山爆发]]。<ref>Williams, Scott W.: "[http://www.math.buffalo.edu/mad/Ancient-Africa/ishango.html Mathematicians of the African Diaspora]" The Mathematics Department of The [[紐約州立大學水牛城分校|State University of New York at Buffalo]].</ref><ref>D. Huylebrouck, [http://www.springerlink.com/content/p5028653585x380j/fulltext.pdf?page=1 ''The Bone that Began the Space Odyssey''], The Mathematical Intelligencer vol 18 no. 4</ref>

这个人造物最初被估计起源于公元前9000年到公元前6500多年。<ref>Gerdes, Paulus (1991): [http://www.math.buffalo.edu/mad/AMU/amu_chma_09.html#beginnings On The History of Mathematics in Africa South of the Sahara]; African Mathematical Union, Commission on the History of Mathematics in Africa.</ref>然而，在重新评估了发掘地点的年代后，现在它其历史超过20000年（公元前18000年到公元前20000年之间）。<ref name="Marshack">Marshack, Alexander (1991): ''The Roots of Civilization'', Colonial Hill, Mount Kisco, NY.</ref><ref>Brooks, A.S. and Smith, C.C. (1987): "Ishango revisited: new age determinations and cultural interpretations", ''The African Archaeological Review'', 5 : 65-78.</ref>

伊尚戈骨永久性展览于[[比利时|比利时]][[布鲁塞尔|布鲁塞尔]][[比利时皇家自然科学研究所|比利时皇家自然科学研究所]]处。<ref name="RBINS_intro">[https://www.naturalsciences.be/expo/old_ishango/en/ishango/introduction.html Introduction] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304051733/https://www.naturalsciences.be/expo/old_ishango/en/ishango/introduction.html|date=2016-03-04}}, Royal Belgian Institute of Natural Sciences, Brussels, Belgium.</ref><ref name="RBINS_flash">[http://ishango.naturalsciences.be/Flash/flash_local/Ishango-02-EN.html Flash presentation] {{Wayback|url=http://ishango.naturalsciences.be/Flash/flash_local/Ishango-02-EN.html |date=20160527164619 }}, Royal Belgian Institute of Natural Sciences, Brussels, Belgium.</ref><ref name="UNESCO">[http://www2.astronomicalheritage.net/index.php/show-entity?identity=4&idsubentity=1 The Ishango Bone, Democratic Republic of the Congo], on permanent display at the Royal Belgian Institute of Natural Sciences, Brussels, Belgium. </ref>

== 数学解释 ==
[[File:IshangoColumnB.png|右]]
[[File:IshangoColumnC.png|右]]
[[File:IshangoColumnA.png|右]]
骨头上的刻痕分成不对称的三列，这导致了“各种诱人的假设”,<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=2f1aDgAAQBAJ&pg=PA35|title=Numbers and the Making of Us: Counting and the Course of Human Cultures|last=Everett|first=Caleb|publisher=Harvard University Press|year=2017|isbn=9780674504431|page=35}}</ref>例如被用来作为一个计算工具、用于简单的数学流程或者用来构造一个[[记数系统|数字系统]]。 

第三列被解释为“质数表”，<ref>Williams, Scott W.: "Mathematicians of the African Diaspora" The Mathematics Department of The State University of New York at Buffalo.[http://www.math.buffalo.edu/mad/Ancient-Africa/ishango.html]</ref> 但这更有可能只是一个巧合。数学史家彼得‧S‧鲁德曼认为，直到约公元前500年人类才理解了质数，而质数的概念直接依赖于整除的概念，这一概念的出现应该不早于公元前10,000年。<ref>{{Cite book|title=How Mathematics Happened: The First 50,000 Years|last=Rudman|first=Peter Strom|publisher=Prometheus Books|year=2007|isbn=978-1-59102-477-4|page=64}}</ref>

亚历山大‧马沙克则推测，伊尚戈骨代表一个六个月为周期的太阴历（基于月相的历法）。克劳迪娅‧扎斯拉夫斯凯就此认为，这个工具创造者可能是一个女人，用来跟踪[[月相|月相]]周期相关的[[月經|月经周期]]。<ref>Zaslavsky, Claudia: ''Africa Counts: Number and Pattern in African Culture'', L. Hill, 1979.</ref><ref>Zaslavsky, Claudia: "[http://web.nmsu.edu/~pscott/isgem71.htm Women as the First Mathematicians]", ''International Study Group on Ethnomathematics Newsletter'', Volume 7 Number 1, 1992|January 1992.</ref>也有人反对这一观点，他们认为马沙克过度解释了数据，证据并不足以支持这是太阴历。<ref>Robinson, Judy. 1992. </ref>

== 第二块骨头 ==

1959年对伊尚戈地区的早期挖掘中，还发现了另外一块骨头。颜色更淡，被刮削、打薄并打磨过，一端已经破损，暴露出空腔。这个古物可能和著名的那块骨头一样，一端嵌有石英，作为一个工具的把手使用。14厘米的长骨的六个侧面，刻有90个刻痕，根据长度被归类为“主要的”或“轻微的”。Jean de Heinzelin的解释认为主要刻痕为单位或倍数，而轻微的是分数或者余数。他认为这块骨头是用来记录10进制和12进制换算规则的。<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ishango.naturalsciences.be/Flash/flash_local/Ishango-24-EN.html|title=The Second Bone|accessdate=12 October 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160623220133/http://ishango.naturalsciences.be/Flash/flash_local/Ishango-24-EN.html|archive-date=2016-06-23|dead-url=yes}}</ref>
