'''鴨子曲線'''（duck curve）是在有使用可再生能源的商業[[發電|發電]]中，一天中{{link-en|尖峰負載|peak demand}}及可再生能源發電量之間的落差。若當地架設了許多太陽能發電設備，在日落後，其他發電方式需要的供電量會快速上昇，約在傍晚中間到達最高峰，因此電量落差對時間的圖會出現[[鴨子|鴨子]]的輪廓<ref name=":1">Paul Denholm, Matthew O’Connell, Gregory Brinkman, and Jennie Jorgenson. "[http://www.nrel.gov/docs/fy16osti/65023.pdf Overgeneration from Solar Energy in California: A Field Guide to the Duck Chart]" NREL/TP-6A20-65023. ''National Renewable Energy Laboratory'',  November 2015 </ref><ref name=":2">{{cite web|url= http://insideenergy.org/2014/10/02/ie-questions-why-is-california-trying-to-behead-the-duck/ |title=IE Questions: Why Is California Trying To Behead The Duck? |first=Jordan |last=Wirfs-Brock |date=2 October 2014|work=Inside Energy  |accessdate= 29 October 2016}}</ref>。{{link-en|夏威夷太陽能|Solar power in Hawaii|在夏威夷}}，太陽能發電相當普遍，產生的曲線稱為「尼斯曲線」（Nessie curve）<ref name=":3">{{cite web |url=http://theenergycollective.com/jeffstjohn/2188461/charting-hawaiis-spectacular-solar-growth |title=Charting Hawaii's Spectacular Solar Growth |accessdate=2015-02-04 |work=The Energy Collective}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.greentechmedia.com/articles/read/hawaiis-solar-grid-landscape-and-the-nessie-curve |title=Hawaii’s Solar-Grid Landscape and the ‘Nessie Curve’|publisher= |date=10 February 2014  |accessdate=10 January 2017}}</ref>。
