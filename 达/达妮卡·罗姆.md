'''达妮卡·罗姆'''（{{lang-en|Danica Roem}}，{{bd|1984年|9月30日}}），美国记者、[[弗吉尼亚州|弗吉尼亚州]][[民主党_(美国)|民主党]]籍政治人物。

罗姆出生于弗吉尼亚北部的[[威廉王子县|威廉王子县]]，<ref name=noisey/>毕业于[[纽约州|纽约州]]{{le|圣波拿文都大学|St. Bonaventure University}}新闻学专业。<ref name=nbc-12jun2017 /> 此后罗姆回到弗吉尼亚州，成为了一名记者。<ref name=olivo>{{cite news |author=Olivo, Antonio |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/virginia-politics/danica-roem-a-policy-wonk-in-a-rainbow-headscarf-trying-to-make-history/2017/10/19/93a0bf00-9e2a-11e7-8ea1-ed975285475e_story.html |title=Danica Roem: Policy wonk in a rainbow headscarf |newspaper=The Washington Post |date=October 20, 2017 |accessdate=8 November 2017}}</ref>2013年，她作为[[跨性别者|跨性别者]][[出柜|出柜]]<ref name=noisey />，并于2015年更改了自己的名字。<ref> Antonio Olivo. "Five Things to Know About Danica Roem." ''Washington Post'', October 20, 2017. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/virginia-politics/five-things-to-know-about-democrat-danica-roem/2017/10/20/75d4273c-b50e-11e7-9e58-e6288544af98_story.html?tid=a_inl&utm_term=.900996d02eb5]. </ref>

2017年，罗姆在弗吉尼亚众议院第13选区的选举中击败已连任25年、反对[[LGBT|LGBT]]的[[共和党_(美国)|共和党]]籍议员鲍勃·马歇尔（Bob Marshall），成功当选弗吉尼亚州众议员。<ref name="wapo-13jun2017">{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/virginia-politics/democratic-challenger-to-marshall-would-be-vas-first-openly-transgender-lawmaker/2017/06/13/2cae81e4-4f95-11e7-b064-828ba60fbb98_story.html|title=Democratic challenger to Marshall would be Va.'s first openly transgender lawmaker|newspaper=The Washington Post|first=Fenit|last=Nirappil|date=June 13, 2017|accessdate=June 14, 2017}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/virginia-politics/virginia-democrat-highlights-transgender-status-in-new-political-ad/2017/09/26/586b201e-a2ae-11e7-ade1-76d061d56efa_story.html|title=‘Just who I am’: In new ad, Va. Democratic candidate discusses being transgender|last=Olivo|first=Antonio|date=September 26, 2017|work=Washington Post|access-date=October 12, 2017|language=en-US|issn=0190-8286}}</ref>由此，她也成为了首位当选美国州议员并就任的公开跨性别者。<ref name="wapo-07nov2017">{{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/virginia-politics/danica-roem-will-be-vas-first-openly-transgender-elected-official-after-unseating-conservative-robert-g-marshall-in-house-race/2017/11/07/d534bdde-c0af-11e7-959c-fe2b598d8c00_story.html |author=Olivo, Antonio |date=November 8, 2017 |newspaper=The Washington Post |title=Danica Roem of Virginia to be first openly transgender person elected, seated in a U.S. statehouse |accessdate=8 November 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.washingtonblade.com/2017/06/21/danica-roem-campaign-not-based-gender/|title=Danica Roem: Campaign is 'not based on my gender'|last=Lavers|first=Michael|date=June 21, 2017|work=Washington Blade: Gay News, Politics, LGBT Rights|access-date=October 12, 2017|archive-url=|archive-date=|dead-url=|language=en-US}}</ref>
