'''圣体共在论'''是[[基督教神学|基督教神学]]中有关[[圣体实在|圣体实在]]的理论之一。与[[聖餐變體論|聖餐變體論]]相反，它相信[[圣餐礼|圣餐礼]]中[[基督|基督]]的肉与血是和面包与酒同在的，而不是通过弥撒转换而成的。此学说是[[罗拉德派|罗拉德派]]的核心思想之一，[[天主教|天主教]]视其为异端。一些[[路德派|路德派]]教徒认同这种观点，但大多数并不赞成<ref>Formula of Concord, Epitome, VII.42 and Solid Declaration VII.127 in F. Bente, ''Triglot Concordia'', (St Louis: CPH, 1921), 817, 1015.</ref><ref name=WELS>{{cite web |url=https://www.wels.net/cgi-bin/site.pl?1518&cuTopic_topicID=58&cuItem_itemID=11345 |archive-url=https://wayback.archive-it.org/all/20080102105710/https://www.wels.net/cgi-bin/site.pl?1518&cuTopic_topicID=58&cuItem_itemID=11345 |dead-url=yes |archive-date=2 January 2008 |work=WELS Topical Q&A |title=Real Presence Communion – Consubstantiation? |publisher=Wisconsin Evangelical Lutheran Synod |quote=Although some Lutherans have used the term 'consbstantiation' [sic] and it might possibly be understood correctly (e.g., the bread & wine, body & blood coexist with each other in the Lord's Supper),  most Lutherans reject the term because of the false connotation it contains...either that the body and blood, bread and wine come together to form one substance in the Lord’s Supper or that the body and blood are present in a natural manner like the bread and the wine. Lutherans believe that the bread and the wine are present in a natural manner in the Lord’s Supper and Christ’s true body and blood are present in an illocal, supernatural manner. |accessdate=26 Jan 2015 |df= }}</ref><ref name="America1888">{{cite book|title=Lectures on the Augsburg Confession|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=XWwrAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA350|accessdate=13 June 2014|year=1888|publisher=Lutheran Publication Society|quote=But in neither sense can that monstrous doctrine of Consubstantiation be attributed to our church, since Lutherans do not believe either in that local conjunction of two bodies, nor in any commingling of bread and of Christ's body, of wine and of his blood.|work=Theological Seminary of the United Lutheran Church in America|page=350}}</ref>。
