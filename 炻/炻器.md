'''炻器'''（shí，英语：Stoneware）是介于[[陶器|陶器]]与[[瓷器|瓷器]]之间的[[陶瓷器|陶瓷器]]<ref name="temps">[http://www.bigceramicstore.com/info/ceramics/tips/tip90_temp_fire_clay.html Clay vitrifying temperatures]</ref>，原料主要是石器土或非[[耐火材料|耐火的]]火泥<ref>''Standard Terminology of Ceramic Whiteware and Related Products: ASTM Standard C242''.</ref>，吸水率通常小于6％<ref name="Dodd">Arthur Dodd & David Murfin. </ref>，有的施釉，有的不施釉<ref>Encyclopædia Britannica [http://www.britannica.com/art/Wedgwood-ware Jasperware is unglazed stoneware]</ref>。
[[File:Man_Sheng_teapot.JPG|缩略图]]]]

==名称==
炻器这个名称来源于欧洲，本意是石器。日本人翻译时为了表明它不是用石头加工成的，而是经过烧制的，所以加上‘火’字旁，写作炻器。中国传统上没有炻器这一分类，例如[[定窑|定窑]]烧制的炻器被称为瓷器<ref>Valenstein, S. (1998). </ref>。根据欧洲工业标准Combined Nomenclature：
: "炻器，这虽然密集，不可渗透和硬足以抵抗刮过钢点，不同于 [[瓷器|瓷器]] ，因为它是更加 不透明的，并通常只部分 陶瓷中。 它可能是玻璃质或半玻璃体。 它通常是色灰色和褐色，因为杂质在土用于其制造的，通常是玻璃。"

==种类==
[[File:Red_Wing_Pottery_2006_109_1.jpg|缩略图]]
炻器按其坯体的细密性，均匀性以及粗糙程度可将炻器分为粗炻器与细炻器两大类。粗炻器常见于工业用的耐酸化工陶瓷<ref name="autogenerated1994">''Dictionary Of Ceramics''. </ref>，建筑陶瓷和缸器，细炻器常见于日用炻器和陈设品。<ref>F. Singer & S. S. Singer. </ref>

炻器在公元前2600至前1900年[[印度河流域文明|印度河流域文明]]时期被大量烧制<ref>{{Cite book|title=Ancient Cities of the Indus Valley Civilization|last=Mark Kenoyer|first=Jonathan|date=1998|publisher=Oxford University Press|pages=260}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|title=Proto-Historic Pottery of Indus Valley Civilization; Study of Painted Motif|last=Satyawadi|first=Sudha|date=July 1, 1994|publisher=D.K. Printworld|isbn=978-8124600306|pages=324}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|title=The Production and Distribution of Stoneware Bangles at Mohenjo-daro and Harappa as Monitored by Chemical Characterization Studies|last=Blackman et all|date=1992|publisher=Prehistory Press|location=Madison, WI, USA|pages=37–44}}</ref>中国在晚商时期出现炻器<ref>Sato, Masahiko. </ref>，在汉代大量生产<ref>Li, He. ''Chinese Ceramics: A New Comprehensive Survey''. </ref><ref>Rhodes, Daniel. </ref>。宜兴[[紫砂陶|紫砂陶]]即是一种不施釉的有色细炻器。

欧洲炻器有：
* Böttger Ware是一种暗红色的炻器，在欧洲瓷器史上意义重大。<ref>''The Discovery Of European Porcelain By Bottger - A Systematic Creative Development''. </ref><ref>''300th Anniversary. ''</ref><ref>''Invention Of European Porcelain''. </ref>
* Cane Ware英国18世纪一种浅棕色的炻器。 <ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.wedgwood.com/GB/About_Wedgwood/_Design_and_Craftsmanship/Heritage_Products/Heritage_Products_Page_3|title=WedgwoodŽ Official UK Site: Wedgwood China, Fine China Tableware and Gifting|accessdate=2012-04-26|publisher=Wedgwood.com|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100803205657/http://www.wedgwood.com/GB/About_Wedgwood/_Design_and_Craftsmanship/Heritage_Products/Heritage_Products_Page_3|archivedate=2010-08-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.wedgwoodsocalif.org/caneware.html|title=Cane Ware|accessdate=2012-04-26|date=2012-01-23|publisher=Wedgwoodsocalif.org}}</ref>
* Crouch Ware是18世纪初的一种浅色炻器，被认为是英格兰最早的炻器。<ref>Salt glazed stoneware. </ref> 
* Rosso Anticos是18世纪英格兰一种红色、无釉炻器。<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.wedgwood.com/GB/About_Wedgwood/_Design_and_Craftsmanship/Heritage_Products/Heritage_Products_Page_4|title=Wedgwood Official UK Site: Wedgwood|accessdate=2012-04-26|publisher=Wedgwood.com|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110120082623/http://www.wedgwood.com/GB/About_Wedgwood/_Design_and_Craftsmanship/Heritage_Products/Heritage_Products_Page_4|archivedate=2011-01-20}}</ref><ref>''Wedgwood and his imitators''. </ref>

== 材料和烧制 ==

炻器土坯常含有铁或碳等杂质，[[塑性變形|可塑性]]差异很大<ref>Cuff, Yvonne Hutchinson. </ref>。耐火火泥中高岭石含量高，并有少量的[[云母|云母]]和[[石英|石英]]。非耐火的火泥主要是云母和长石。<ref>Cripss, J.C.; Reeves, G.M.; and Sims, I. ''Clay Materials Used in Construction.'' </ref>

炻器坯料的化学成分变化范围大致为：SiO2 55～65％；Al2O3 25～35℃；熔剂氧化物总量5～8%。<ref>Rhodes, Daniel and Hopper, Robin. </ref>

瓷器烧制温度从1100 °C至1300 °C不等<ref>Paul Rado ''An Introduction to the Technology of Pottery''; 2nd ed. </ref>。

炻器的吸水率可以低于1%。<ref>W. Ryan & C. Radford. </ref>

另一个类型，Flintless石器、也已确定。 它被定义，在英国陶(卫生和福利)的特殊规定的1950为："石器、身体，其中包括自然粘土没有 [[燧石|火石]] 或 [[石英|石英]] 或其他形式的免费的 [[二氧化硅|二氧化硅]] 已被添加。"<ref>Arthur Dodd & David Murfin. </ref>

传统 [[东亚|东亚]] 只有思想进行分类陶器进入"低发射"和"高发射"产品，等同于陶器和瓷器、不间欧洲的类的石器、以及众多的当地类型的石制主要是认为这是瓷器，但往往没有白色的和透明。<ref>Valenstein, S. (1998). </ref>
