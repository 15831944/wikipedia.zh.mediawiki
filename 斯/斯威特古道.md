'''斯威特古道'''（{{lang-en|Sweet Track}}）是一條位於[[英國|英國]]{{tsl|en|Somerset Levels|索美塞特平原}}的{{tsl|en|causeway|堤道}}，它的歷史可追溯到[[新石器時代|新石器時代]]，在西元前3807年或3806年建成，目前是[[不列顛群島|不列顛群島]]上第二古老的{{tsl|en|timber trackway|木造古道}}。已知斯威特古道主要建築在一條更早期的堤道（{{tsl|en|Post Track|波斯特古道}}）所經路線上。

數千年前{{tsl|en|Westhay|威斯特黑}}曾有一座小島，此古道連結它和一條位於沙普維克的脊狀高地，中間跨越現今已嚴重枯竭的[[河流濕地|河流濕地]]，長度接近{{convert|2000|m|ft}}。這條路徑在過去是縱橫索美塞特平原的交通網絡的一部份。周遭的[[泥炭|泥炭]]濕地已出土各式各樣的手工藝品和其他史前物品，包括一個{{tsl|en|jadeitite|硬玉岩}}製、禮儀用斧頭的刀刃。<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/services/aop-cambridge-core/content/view/CD29F07E5335AC5B12F5670959B19A01/S0003598X00057987a.pdf/div-class-title-a-jade-axe-from-the-somerset-levels-div.pdf|title=Antiquity|last=|first=|date=|website=www.cambridge.org|archive-url=|archive-date=|dead-url=|access-date=2017-06-28}}</ref>

走道由許多首尾相連的[[橡樹|橡樹]]版構成，其下木樁被交錯排放，釘入一片泥濘中而達到支撐的效果。這條古道只被使用大約十年，之後可能因為逐漸上升的水位而遭人遺棄。1970年人們發現這條古道後，古道的絕大部分都依照古蹟保留措施留在其原本的位置，另有一個抽、供水系統讓木材保持潮濕。有些部分則保存在[[大英博物館|大英博物館]]和位於[[湯頓|湯頓]]的{{tsl|en|Museum of Somerset|索美塞特博物館}}。現在到訪的遊客能漫步在{{tsl|en|Shapwick Heath|沙普維克荒野}}一條模仿原古道重建的道路上。

==所在地==
[[File:Moors_zh.png|300px]]
這條古道位於威斯特黑的一座島嶼和沙普維克一條靠近{{tsl|en|River Brue|布魯河}}的狹長高地之間。威斯特黑的一群土墩顯示此處曾有史前時代的湖上木排屋，並且與在{{tsl|en|Glastonbury Lake Village|格拉斯頓伯里湖泊村落}}找到的遺址類似，建造在一片填有柴枝、蕨類等物的木頭築成的人工地基上。<ref>{{cite book|last=Cunliffe|first=Barry|title=Iron Age Communities in Britain (4th Ed)|year=2005|url=https://books.google.com/?id=3lkEgdtOvGEC&printsec=frontcover&dq=isbn=0-415-34779-3&cd=1#v=onepage&q|publisher=Routledge|location=Abingdon, Oxon|isbn=0-415-34779-3|page=266}}</ref>

其他相似古道的遺跡直接在附近顯露出來，連結泥炭沼澤上的居住地，這些古道包括哈尼果爾、阿伯茲韋、貝爾斯、貝克斯、威斯特黑和尼登斯古道。<ref name="digitaldigging">{{cite web|title=Sweet Track – Somerset Levels|url=http://digitaldigging.co.uk/maps/trackways/prehistoric-sweet-track-somerset-levels.html|publisher=Digital Digging|accessdate=2017-06-28|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120305145358/http://digitaldigging.co.uk/maps/trackways/prehistoric-sweet-track-somerset-levels.html|archivedate=2012-03-05}}</ref>諸如{{tsl|en|Meare Pool|米爾湖}}的遺址證明這些古道的目的是提供居地更便利的交通。針對米爾湖的調查指出它是由周遭[[高位沼澤|高位沼澤]]入侵形成，特別在{{tsl|en|Subatlantic|亞大西洋期}}，還有{{tsl|en|core sample|岩心標本}}表示它充滿了至少{{convert|2|m|ft}}深的腐植泥土。<ref>{{cite journal|last=Rippon|first=Stephen|year=2004|title=Making the Most of a Bad Situation? Glastonbury Abbey, Meare, and the Medieval Exploitation of Wetland Resources in the Somerset Levels|journal=Medieval Archaeology|publisher=Maney Publishing|volume=48|page=119|doi=10.1179/007660904225022816|url=https://ore.exeter.ac.uk/repository/bitstream/handle/10036/20952/Glastonbury%20Abbey.pdf?sequence=1|ref=harv|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150703002414/https://ore.exeter.ac.uk/repository/bitstream/handle/10036/20952/Glastonbury%20Abbey.pdf?sequence=1|archivedate=2015-07-03|df=}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|last1=Godwin|first1=H.|last2=Macfadyen|first2=W. A.|year=1955|title=Studies of the Post-Glacial History of British Vegetation. XIII. The Meare Pool Region of the Somerset Levels|journal=Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B|publisher=Royal Society|volume=239|issue=662|pages=161–190|doi=10.1098/rstb.1955.0008|url=http://rstb.royalsocietypublishing.org/content/239/662/161|ref=harv|bibcode=1955RSPTB.239..161G|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303172740/http://rstb.royalsocietypublishing.org/content/239/662/161|archivedate=2016-03-03|df=}}</ref>

兩座米爾湖中的{{tsl|en|Meare Pool Village|米爾湖村落}}似乎由一些立在乾燥泥炭上的建物組成，像是帳篷、防風林和畜欄。鋪在泥炭上的黏土形成高起的平台，提供居民日常生活、從事製造和活動的空間，有些地方有較厚的黏土鋪層承載用黏土或石頭造成的[[灶|灶]]。<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Chapmana|first1=Henry P.|last2=Van de Noort|first2=Robert|year=2001|title=High-Resolution Wetland Prospection, using GPS and GIS: Landscape Studies at Sutton Common (South Yorkshire), and Meare Village East (Somerset)|journal=Journal of Archaeological Science|volume=28|issue=4|pages=365–375|doi=10.1006/jasc.2000.0581|accessdate=2017-06-28|url=http://www.sciencedirect.com/science?_ob=ArticleURL&_udi=B6WH8-45BT6MH-S&_user=10&_rdoc=1&_fmt=&_orig=search&_sort=d&view=c&_version=1&_urlVersion=0&_userid=10&md5=5d01004612f0220ca987f9ba64bf1175|ref=harv}}</ref>

==發現與研究==
1970年，古道在開挖泥炭過程中被發掘，並以發現者瑞伊·斯威特（Ray Sweet）的姓氏命名。<ref>{{cite book|last1=Williams|first1=Robin|authorlink=|last2=Williams|first2=Romey|title=The Somerset Levels|year=1992|pages=35–36|publisher=Ex Libris Press|location=Bradford on Avon|isbn=0-948578-38-6}}</ref>他服務的公司E·J·古德溫把一片從古道上取下的木板交給[[劍橋大學|劍橋大學]]考古學助理講師約翰·科爾斯（John Coles）。科爾斯在之前已經執行過一些附近古道的挖掘作業，<ref name="brunning"/>他對這條古道的興趣催生了1973起為期16年的索美塞特平原計畫（Somerset Levels Project），並從[[英格蘭遺產委員會|英格蘭遺產委員會]]等多方捐獻者獲得研究資金。這項計畫承辦一系列地方性考古研究，以及確定這些西元前數千年就存在的古道經濟和地理意義。<ref>{{cite book|last=Coles|first=J.M.|title=Effect of Man on the Landscape: The Lowland Zone|year=1978|publisher=Council for British Archaeology|isbn=978-0-900312-60-1|format=PDF|pages=86–89|url=http://archaeologydataservice.ac.uk/archives/view/cba_rr/rr21.cfm|editor=Susan Limbrey|location=York|chapter=Man and landscape in the Somerset Levels|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161003082045/http://archaeologydataservice.ac.uk/archives/view/cba_rr/rr21.cfm|archivedate=2016-10-03|df=}}</ref>約翰和布萊恩妮·科爾斯（Bryony Coles）在索美塞特平原計畫的努力在1996年獲得肯定，他們以該考古計畫對知識有卓越貢獻為由獲得[[帝國化學工業|帝國化學工業]]獎，<ref>{{cite web|title=British Archaeological Awards|url=http://www.britarch.ac.uk/awards/baa1998.html|publisher=Council for British Archeology|accessdate=2017-06-28|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080603093627/http://www.britarch.ac.uk/awards/baa1998.html|archivedate=2008-06-03}}</ref>2006年又得到{{tsl|en|European Association of Archaeologists|歐洲考古學家協會}}（EAA）頒發的獎項。<ref>{{cite web|title=European Archaeological Heritage Prize 2006|url=http://www.e-a-a.org/EAA/Awards/Heritage_Prize/2006/EAA/Navigation_Prizes_and_Awards/Heritage_Prize_2006.aspx?hkey=e9a184bb-ef4b-4247-807d-7ea9ea84b375|publisher=European Association of Archaeologists|format=PDF|accessdate=2017-06-28|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161001201917/http://www.e-a-a.org/EAA/Awards/Heritage_Prize/2006/EAA/Navigation_Prizes_and_Awards/Heritage_Prize_2006.aspx?hkey=e9a184bb-ef4b-4247-807d-7ea9ea84b375|archivedate=2016-10-01|df=}}</ref>

[[樹木年輪學|樹木年輪學]]有助於更精確的確認古道的年齡，研究結果顯示其在西元前約3807年建造，<ref>{{cite web|title=The day the Sweet Track was built|work=New Scientist, 16 June 1990|url=http://www.newscientist.com/article/mg12617212.800-science-the-day-the-sweet-track-was-built.html|accessdate=2017-06-28|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100311232408/http://www.newscientist.com/article/mg12617212.800-science-the-day-the-sweet-track-was-built.html|archivedate=2010-03-11|df=}}</ref>這個年代推定讓斯威特古道成為世界上最古老的道路。<ref Name="Current_Archaeology_intro">{{cite journal|title=Highlights|volume=XV (4)|issue=172 (Special issue on Wetlands)|work=Current Archaeology|publisher=Current Archaeology|date=February 2001|ref=harv}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last1=Lay|first1=M. G.|title=Ways of the World: A History of the World's Roads and of the Vehicles that Used Them|year=1999|publisher=Rutgers University Press|isbn=978-0-8135-2691-1|url=https://books.google.com/?id=flvS-nJga8QC&pg=PR3&lpg=PR3&dq=%22Ways+of+the+world%22+Rutgers+University+Press,+New+Brunswick&q=Sweet%20Track|last2=Vance|first2=James E.|page=51}}</ref> 一直到2009年，{{tsl|en|Belmarsh prison|貝爾馬許監獄}}附近一條已經超過6000歲的古道被發現後，斯威特古道才成為世界第二。<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.physorg.com/news169297178.html|title=London's earliest timber structure found during Belmarsh prison dig|last=Anon|date=2009-08-12|work=physorg.com News|publisher=PhysOrg.com|accessdate=2017-06-28|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110715214221/http://www.physorg.com/news169297178.html|archivedate=2011-07-15|df=}}</ref>針對斯威特古道組成的分析亦對新石器時代的樹木年輪學研究有所助益；將其和來自[[特倫特河|特倫特河]]的木頭和{{tsl|en|Stolford|史多佛德}}的沉水森林比較，不僅能更完整繪製年輪圖像，還能找出其和氣候變遷的關係。<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Hillam|first1=J.|last2=Groves|first2=C. M.|last3=Brown|first3=D. M.|last4=Baillie|first4=M. G. L.|last5=Coles|first5=J. M.|last6=Coles|first6=B. J.|title=Dendrochronology of the English Neolithic|journal=Antiquity|year=1990|volume=64|issue=243|format=PDF|url=http://www.bosci.net/papers/sweettrackdate.pdf|ref=harv|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110723031911/http://www.bosci.net/papers/sweettrackdate.pdf|archivedate=2011-07-23|df=}}</ref>

那些用於建造這條古道的木材被歸類於{{tsl|en|bog-wood|沼木}}，其為長時間（可達數百至數千年）埋在泥炭沼澤中的木頭，而且因為沼澤[[酸性|酸性]]的{{tsl|en|hypoxia (environmental)|缺氧環境}}得以避免[[分解作用|腐敗]]。沼木通常被溶解在酸性水溶液的[[單寧|單寧]]染成褐色，表現出[[化石#化石化作用|化石化作用]]的早期階段。英國{{tsl|en|Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs|環境食物及農村事務部}}因古道年代久遠而集資並發起一場大規模發掘作業。<ref name="brunning">{{cite book|last=Brunning |first=Richard |title=Somerset Archaeology: Papers to Mark 150 Years of the Somerset Archaeological and Natural History Society |year=2000 |publisher=South West Heritage Trust |url=http://www1.somerset.gov.uk/archives/hes/downloads/HES_150_Years_Chapter_11.pdf |format=PDF |editor=CJ Webster |chapter=11. Neolithic and bronze-age Somerset: a wetland perspective |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120324051317/http://www1.somerset.gov.uk/archives/hes/downloads/HES_150_Years_Chapter_11.pdf |archivedate=2012-03-24 |df= }}</ref>
[[File:Hache_222.1_Prespective.jpg|thumb]]
1973年，一片{{tsl|en|jadeitite|硬玉岩}}斧的刀刃部分在古道邊出土，考古學家認為當時人們把它當作祭品放置於此，<ref>{{cite book|last1=Hunter|first1=John|title=The Archaeology of Britain: An Introduction from Earliest Times to the Twenty-First Century: An Introduction from the Upper Palaeolithic to the Industrial Revolution|year=1999|publisher=Routledge|location=London|isbn=978-0-415-13588-7|pages=63–64|url=https://books.google.com/?id=SkaqGT0wLk4C&pg=PA65&lpg=PA65&dq=Sweet+track+archeology&q|last2=Rolston|first2=Ian}}</ref>這時不列顛群島和愛爾蘭已有超過100件類似斧頭。作為其中之一，它的狀況保持良好，材質也更珍貴，暗示了其具有象徵性，不作為伐木用。<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.britishmuseum.org/explore/highlights/highlight_objects/pe_prb/j/jadeite_axe-head.aspx|title=Jadeite axe-head|accessdate=2017-06-28|publisher=[[British_Museum|British Museum]]|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090221125015/http://britishmuseum.org/explore/highlights/highlight_objects/pe_prb/j/jadeite_axe-head.aspx|archivedate=2009-02-21}}</ref>這種材料加工不易，又來自歐洲的阿爾卑斯山區域，所以此類在英國發現的斧頭被認為本身不具有實際功用，而是當作貨幣流通或贈與物。<ref>{{cite book|last=Barker|first=Graeme|title=Companion encyclopedia of archaeology|publisher=Routledge|location=New York|year=1999|page=378|isbn=0-415-21329-0|oclc=|doi=}}</ref>[[放射性碳定年法|放射性碳定年法]]說明掩埋此斧頭的泥炭大約在西元前3200年沉積該處。<ref>{{cite book|last=Smith|first=I.F.|title=Stone axe studies: archaeological, petrological, experimental and ethnographic|year=1978|publisher=Council for British Archaeology|isbn=0-900312-63-7|pages=13–22|url=http://archaeologydataservice.ac.uk/archives/view/cba_rr/rr23.cfm|format=PDF|editor=T H McK Clough and W A Cummins|accessdate=2017-06-28|chapter=The chronology of British stone implements|location=York|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160321012925/http://archaeologydataservice.ac.uk/archives/view/cba_rr/rr23.cfm|archivedate=2016-03-21|df=}}</ref>此外，遺址還留有各式木製手工藝品，例如：船槳、盤子、弓箭、飛斧、{{tsl|en|Digging stick|挖棒}}、鋤頭、梳子，還有湯匙碎片等物，其他材質的物件有燧石片、箭頭、完好如初的燧石斧等。<ref>{{cite web|title=Neolithic finds, Shapwick Heath, Shapwick|url=http://www.somersetheritage.org.uk/record/11000|work=Somerset Historic Environment Record|publisher=South West Heritage Trust|accessdate=2017-06-28|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161003095137/http://www.somersetheritage.org.uk/record/11000|archivedate=2016-10-03|df=}}</ref>

2008年，一份對該區域的[[地球物理學|地球物理學]]調查得到特殊的[[磁強計|磁強計]]數據——泥炭中的木頭可能對泥炭的[[水文學|水分分布、得失]]產生影響，儀器探測到的正是孔隙水和泥炭[[基質_(地質)|基質]]中的礦物質變化。<ref>{{cite web|last1=Armstrong|first1=K.|title=Archaeological geophysical prospection in peatland environments: Locating the Sweet Track at Canada Farm, Shapwick Heath (Somerset)|url=http://eprints.bournemouth.ac.uk/11145/1/armstrong_cheetham_abstract_v2_.pdf|work=EIGG 8th Meeting on Recent Work in Archaeological Geophysics, 16 Dec 2008, The Geological Society, Burlington House, London, UK.|format=PDF|publisher=Bournemouth University |accessdate=2017-06-28|last2=Cheetham|first2=P|year=2008|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110719185241/http://eprints.bournemouth.ac.uk/11145/1/armstrong_cheetham_abstract_v2_.pdf|archivedate=2011-07-19|df=}}</ref>

==建造者==
{{see also|史前不列顛}}
建造古道之時，其建造者已經在這裡過著有組織的定居生活，證明他們的身分是新石器時代約西元前3900年移居於此的農人。<ref name="Costen">{{cite book|author=Costen, M. D.|title=The origins of Somerset|publisher=Manchester University Press|location=Manchester|year=1992|pages=4, 5|isbn=0-7190-3675-5|url=https://books.google.com/?id=yX-7AAAAIAAJ&pg=PA7&dq=somerset+levels+neolithic&q=somerset%20levels%20neolithic|oclc=|doi=|accessdate=}}</ref>人類進入平原前，四周的高地生長著茂盛的林木，移民到此開始砍伐這些森林，主要將其用於小規模耕種的田園式生活。<ref>{{cite book|author=Scarry, C. Margaret|title=Foraging and farming in the eastern woodlands|publisher=University Press of Florida|location=Gainesville|year=1993|pages=118, 119|isbn=0-8130-1235-X}}</ref>這個聚落從事漁獵、農耕和採集，於冬日降臨期間從平原上被水淹過的區域獲得豐富的魚和野禽；在夏季，乾燥的區域則提供肥沃、空曠的草地，當中有蘆葦、木材、果實、種子、鳥類和其他野生動物，不僅有牧場放養牛隻和羊群，還有可用於建設等其他經濟活動。<ref>{{cite book|author=Aston, Michael|title=Interpreting the landscape: landscape archaeology and local history|publisher=Routledge|location=New York|year=1997|page=26|isbn=0-415-15140-6}}</ref>推測在乾季尾聲、水位最低時，居民為了進行大型公共活動，他們到達沼澤中島嶼的需求變得急切，需要儲備木材、建造步道。<ref name="Costen"/>建造步道的技術表示他們擁有進步的木工技藝，並且暗示工人之中的工作分配存在區別。<ref name="Costen"/>這裡的居民似乎已經使用周圍的林地至少120年了。<ref name="Costen"/>

==建造過程==
[[File:Sweet_track_cross_section2-zh.png|thumb]]
[[File:Sweet_Track_replica.jpg|alt=Two wooden posts set in the ground and crossing at an angle supporting a wooden board which disappears into tall green reeds]]
[[File:Reconstruction_of_the_Sweet_Track..jpg|thumb]]
古道建成於西元前3807或3806年，<ref Name="Current_Archaeology_somerset-levels">{{cite journal|last=Brunning|first=Richard|title=The Somerset Levels|pages=139–143|volume=XV (4)|issue=172 (Special issue on Wetlands)|work=Current Archaeology|publisher=Current Archaeology|date=February 2001|ref=harv}}</ref>主要是一條由[[橡樹|橡樹]]板頭尾相連而成的步道，其下有[[梣樹|梣樹]]、橡樹或[[椴樹|椴樹]]製成的木樁交叉排列、插入泥炭中以利支撐。<ref name="bm"/>

構成步道面的木板最寬達{{convert|40|cm|in}}、最長達{{convert|3|m|in}}並且有{{convert|5|cm|in}}厚，它們來自最多有400歲、直徑{{convert|1|m|in}}的樹。工人只使用石斧、木楔和木槌砍伐及切割木材。<ref name="Coles Bryony">{{cite journal|last=Coles|first=Bryony|date=1987-10-15|title=Archaeology follows a wet track|journal=New Scientist|publisher=IPC magazines|issue=1582|page=46|url=https://books.google.com/?id=CYz2qg55tPYC&pg=PA46&dq=Sweet+Track&q=Sweet%20Track|ref=harv}}</ref>木樁缺乏旁枝和分岔，其長度、筆直度都提示了它們來自曾被使用、修剪過的林地。<ref>{{harvnb|Coles|Coles|1986|page=50}}</ref>木樁以一定的角度與被平放在泥炭中的縱向圓木相交且深入沼澤的泥炭基底，兩者一起在地下被固定著。圓木最長有{{convert|6.1|m|ft}}、直徑{{convert|7.6|cm|in}}，大部分取自[[榛屬|榛樹]]和[[榿木屬|赤楊]]。<ref name="bm"/><ref name="otter">{{cite book|last=Otter|first=R. A.|title=Civil Engineering Heritage: Southern England|publisher=Thomas Telford ltd|location=London|year=1994|page=101|isbn=978-0-7277-1971-3}}</ref>接著在木板上刻下凹槽以吻合木樁後，將木板放置在木樁形成的X形上組成步道。<ref>{{cite book|author1=Fleming, Neil|author2=Grant, Jim|author3=Gorin, Sam|title=The archaeology coursebook: an introduction to themes, sites, methods and skills|publisher=Routledge|location=New York|year=2008|page=277|isbn=0-415-46286-X|url=https://books.google.com/?id=BgRG99HriOUC&pg=PA277&dq=sweet+track&q=sweet%20track}}</ref>為了讓最上層的木板和其他的維持相同高度，步道有些地方會有第二條圓木鋪放在原本的那條上，<ref>{{harvnb|Coles|Coles|1986|p=112}}</ref>一部份的木板尾端被鑽穿小孔，垂直的木樁穿過孔洞刺入泥炭或是其下的黏土層，達到穩定木板的效果。<ref>{{harvnb|Coles|Coles|1980|p=25}}</ref>比較小的樹則用於建造步道南端，這些木板沿著紋理分割，讓建造者得以完全利用這些樹幹。<ref name="Coles Bryony"/>另有發現其他樹種的碎片，像是：[[冬青|冬青]]、[[柳樹|柳樹]]、[[楊樹|楊樹]]、[[山茱萸屬|山茱萸]]、[[常春藤属|常春藤]]、[[樺樹|樺樹]]以及[[蘋果樹|蘋果樹]]。<ref name="sshersweettrack">{{cite web|title=Sweet Track, Shapwick Heath|url=http://www.somersetheritage.org.uk/record/10739|work=Somerset Historic Environment Record|publisher=South West Heritage Trust|accessdate=2017-06-28|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161003094636/http://www.somersetheritage.org.uk/record/10739|archivedate=2016-10-03|df=}}</ref>

濕地的環境指出構成古道的組件必在現場組裝前於他處[[預製組件|處理]]過，<ref name="bm"/>雖然留在步道邊的木片和被切除的旁枝顯示有一些小修整在現場才進行。<ref name="sshersweettrack"/>建造步道一共耗費大約{{convert|200,000|kg|lbs}}重的木料，但是科爾斯估計一旦完成所有材料的搬運後，只需要十個人便可一日內組裝完畢。<ref>{{cite journal|last=Daniel|first=Glyn|date=1986-10-09|title=World's oldest road|journal=New Scientist|volume=261|publisher=IPC magazines Ltd|issue=2529|pages=100|url=https://books.google.com/?id=_35Z2EMyzAkC&pg=PA60&lpg=PA60&dq=Sweet+Track+to+Glastonbury:+Somerset+Levels+in+Prehistory+%28New+Aspects+of+Antiquity&q=Sweet%20Track%20to%20Glastonbury%3A%20Somerset%20Levels%20in%20Prehistory%20%28New%20Aspects%20of%20Antiquity|ref=harv|bibcode=1989SciAm.261e.100C|doi=10.1038/scientificamerican1189-100}}</ref>

斯威特古道僅被使用十年左右，<ref>{{cite book|last1=Rahtz|first1=Phillip|last2=Watts|first2= Lorna|title=Glastonbury Myth and Archaeology|year=2003|publisher=Tempus|location=Stroud|isbn=978-0-7524-2548-1|page=25}}</ref>歸咎於水位上升、吞沒步道，最後令其失去功能並遭棄置。<ref>{{cite web|title=Wood Culture: Programme7-08|url=http://www.ccanw.co.uk/assets/files/Wood_Wisdom_Panels.pdf|publisher=Centre for Contemporary Art and the Natural World|format=PDF|accessdate=2017-06-28|page=2|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110409184902/http://www.ccanw.co.uk/assets/files/Wood_Wisdom_Panels.pdf|archivedate=2011-04-09|df=}}</ref>種種古道邊出土的物件說明它在聚落居民的農耕生活中擔任日常運輸的工作。<ref name="Costen"/>古道被發現後，研究者又確定其有部分建築在一條更早的路徑旁，原來前推三十年{{tsl|en|Post Track|波斯特古道}}已於西元前3838年建成。<ref name="hill-cotttingham">{{cite book|last1=Hill-Cottingham|first1=Pat|authorlink=|last2=Briggs|first2=D.|last3=Brunning|first3=R.|last4=King|first4=A.|last5=Rix|first5=G|title=The Somerset Wetlands|year=2006|publisher=Somerset Books|location=|isbn=0-86183-432-1}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Post Track, Shapwick Heath|url=http://www.somersetheritage.org.uk/record/10740|work=Somerset Historic Environment Record|publisher=South West JHeritage Trust|accessdate=2017-06-28|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161003093027/http://www.somersetheritage.org.uk/record/10740|archivedate=2016-10-03|df=}}</ref>

==保存==
大部分的古道仍然保留在原處，位於{{tsl|en|Shapwick Heath|沙普維克荒野}}，一處[[具特殊科學價值地點_(英國)|具特殊科學價值地點]]及{{tsl|en|National nature reserve|國家自然保留區}}。<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.naturalengland.org.uk/ourwork/conservation/designatedareas/nnr/1006131.aspx|title=Shapwick Heath NNR|publisher=Natural England|accessdate=2017-06-28|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100120055332/http://www.naturalengland.org.uk/ourwork/conservation/designatedareas/nnr/1006131.aspx|archivedate=2010-01-20|df=}}</ref>{{tsl|en|National Heritage Memorial Fund|國家遺產紀念基金}}購買土地，還有一套{{convert|500|m|ft|adj=on}}長的抽、供水系統積極的維護數百公尺長的古道。<ref>{{cite journal|title=4.20.4 The Sweet Track, the Brue Valley, Somerset: assessment of in situ preservation|journal=Archeology Review|year=1996–1997|url=http://www.eng-h.gov.uk/ArchRev/rev96_7/strack.htm|accessdate=2017-06-28|ref=harv|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100529201047/http://www.eng-h.gov.uk/ArchRev/rev96_7/strack.htm|archivedate=2010-05-29}}</ref>這種維持高水位浸透遺跡的方法在保存溼地遺址方面很罕見。<ref>{{cite book|title=Enduring Records. The Environmental and Cultural Heritage of Wetlands|publisher=Oxbow Books|pages=277–286|last1=Van de Noort|first1=Robert|last2=Chapman|first2=Henry|last3=Cheetham|first3=James|editor=B. Purdy|location=Oxford|chapter=Science-based conservation and management in wetland archaeology: the example of Sutton Common, UK|isbn=1-84217-048-1}}</ref>一條長{{convert|500|m|ft|adj=on}}的區段坐落於{{tsl|en|Nature Conservancy Council|英國自然保護委員會}}擁有的土地上，周遭被黏土堤防圍住以防水滲漏至旁邊低窪泥炭地，而水位也被定期監控著。<ref>{{cite book|last=Purdy|first=Barbara A.|title=Wet site archaeology|year=1990|publisher=CRC Press|place=Boca Raton, FL|isbn=978-0-936923-08-6|page=99|url=https://books.google.com/?id=RqFiYB2KI0YC&pg=PA99&lpg=PA99&dq=Sweet+track+archeology&q}}</ref>該方法的可行性藉由比較鄰近的阿伯茲韋古道展現：那條古道沒有這種保護措施，於1996年被發現已經變得脫水、乾燥。<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Cox|first1=Margaret|last2=Earwood|first2=Caroline|last3=Jones|first3=E.B. Gareth|last4=Jones|first4=Julie|last5=Straker|first5=Vanessa|last6=Robinson|first6=Mark|last7=Tibbett|first7=Mark|last8=West|first8=Steven|title=An Assessment of the Impact of Trees upon Archaeology Within a Relict Wetland|date=October 2001|volume=28|issue=10|accessdate=2017-06-28|pages=1069–1084|doi=10.1006/jasc.2000.0642|url=http://www.sciencedirect.com/science?_ob=ArticleURL&_udi=B6WH8-45BT6KX-J&_user=10&_coverDate=10%2F31%2F2001&_rdoc=1&_fmt=high&_orig=search&_sort=d&_docanchor=&view=c&_searchStrId=1363453974&_rerunOrigin=google&_acct=C000050221&_version=1&_urlVersion=0&_userid=10&md5=18ea3f5e0428783178f39e952dbce065|journal=Journal of Archaeological Science|ref=harv}}</ref>沙普維克荒野自然保留區的水位評估與維護牽涉到英國自然保護委員會、環境食物及農村事務部和索美塞特平原計畫三方。<ref name="sshersweettrack"/>

雖然從平原找回的木頭視覺上沒有缺損，但是經過多年歲月已被嚴重分解、十分脆弱。可行範圍內，狀況良好的木材或是經過處理的木樁末端被移至他處保存以待進一步分析。<ref>{{Harvnb|Coles|Coles|1986|p=107}}</ref>木頭被浸泡在[[聚乙二醇|聚乙二醇]]溶液並置於加熱槽中，進行蒸發作用後，蠟質逐漸在超過九個月間取代木材裡的水。之後將木頭從槽中取出、拭淨，當蠟冷卻硬化時，這些工藝品已經變得堅固並能被自由處理。<ref>{{Harvnb|Coles|Coles|1986|p=108}}</ref>

有一部分的步道曾由採掘當地泥炭的{{tsl|en|Fisons|費森斯}}公司持有，後來被捐贈予倫敦的[[大英博物館|大英博物館]]。<ref name="bm">{{cite web|url=https://www.britishmuseum.org/explore/highlights/highlight_objects/pe_prb/s/section_of_the_sweet_track.aspx|title=1986,1201.1–27 Sweet Track exhibition highlight|publisher=[[British_Museum|British Museum]]|accessdate=2017-06-28|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110429101940/https://www.britishmuseum.org/explore/highlights/highlight_objects/pe_prb/s/section_of_the_sweet_track.aspx|archivedate=2011-04-29}}</ref>這一小部分的古道可以在組裝後用於展示，但是它現在正穩定的儲藏於別處。另有一個重建的部分在[[格拉斯頓伯里|格拉斯頓伯里]]附近的{{tsl|en|Peat Moors Centre|泥炭沼地中心}}展出。該博物館由索美塞特歷史環境部門經營，不過因為索美塞特郡議會刪減預算而在2009年10月關閉。主要的展示品仍然存在，可是未來大眾不一定能參觀。其他的古道樣品存放在{{tsl|en|Museum of Somerset|索美塞特博物館}}。<ref name="digitaldigging"/>

古道的各段已被指名為{{tsl|en|Scheduled Monument|排定古蹟}}，<ref name="sshersweettrack"/>表示其為擁有「國家等級的重要性」的歷史性建築及[[遺址|遺址]]，受免於未授權改變的保護。<ref name="schedule">{{cite web|title=Scheduled monuments policy statement|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/scheduled-monuments-policy-statement|publisher=Gov.uk|accessdate=2017-06-28|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151122112341/https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/scheduled-monuments-policy-statement|archivedate=2015-11-22|df=}}</ref>這些區段也被收錄在{{tsl|en|Historic England|Historic England}}的{{tsl|en|Heritage at Risk Register|瀕危遺產名冊}}。<ref>{{cite web|title=South West England|url=http://www.english-heritage.org.uk/publications/HAR_Register_South_West_2009/southwest-2009-har-register.pdf|work=Heritage at Risk|publisher=English Heritage|format=PDF|accessdate=2017-06-28|page=183|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110609181327/http://www.english-heritage.org.uk/publications/HAR_Register_South_West_2009/southwest-2009-har-register.pdf|archivedate=2011-06-09|df=}}</ref>
