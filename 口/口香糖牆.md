'''口香糖牆'''（{{lang-en|Market Theater Gum Wall}}）是一堵上面黏滿了各種各樣的[[口香糖|口香糖]]的磚牆，位於[[美國|美國]][[西雅圖|西雅圖]]市中心的[[派克市場|派克市場]]內。它黏滿口香糖的外觀和位於加州的{{link-en|口香糖巷|Bubblegum Alley}}很類似。这堵墙有50英呎長，15英呎高。<ref name=cnn>{{cite news|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2009/TRAVEL/07/20/germy.tourist.spots/|title=Kissing, chewing -- the 'germiest ' tourist attractions|last=Chen|first=Stephanie|date=2009-07-20|work=CNN|accessdate=2009-08-14}}</ref>

这堵墙是市場戲院售票亭的一部分。1993年，{{link-en|意外製作|Unexpected_Productions}}的顧客把口香糖帶著[[硬幣|硬幣]]黏在牆上<ref name=st/>，劇場工作人員曾两度試著要把口香糖刮掉，但最後放棄了<ref name=cnn/>。到了1999年，當地市場的職員認為這堵牆成了旅遊景點<ref name=st>{{cite news|url=http://www.seattletimes.com/seattle-news/market-lost-found/|title=Market lost & found|last=Eskenazi|first=Stuart|date=2008-06-06|newspaper=[[西雅图时报|西雅图时报]]|accessdate=2009-08-14}}</ref>。一些人持續地往牆上黏口香糖<ref name=AP>{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1314&dat=20060629&id=OLIyAAAAIBAJ&sjid=K_MDAAAAIBAJ&pg=3455,7352509|title=Gumming it: Messages designed to stick on Seattle's Gum Wall|last=Carter|first=Chelsea J.|date=2006-06-30|agency=[[美联社|美联社]]|newspaper={{tsl|en|The Spokesman-Review||The Spokesman-Review}}|accessdate=2012-06-08}}</ref>。它在2009年被評為五大熱門景點之一，僅次於位於[[愛爾蘭|愛爾蘭]][[科克郡|科克郡]]的{{link-en|巧言石|Blarney Stone}}。<ref name=cnn/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://komonews.com/news/local/ewww-seattle-gum-wall-a-top-germy-attraction-11-20-2015|title=Ewww! Seattle gum wall a top germy attraction|date=2009-06-13|work=Komo News|accessdate=2016-07-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mynorthwest.com/?nid=11&sid=177556|title=Seattle Gum Wall ranks in top 5 "Germiest Attractions"|last=Griswold|first=Jamie|date=2009-06-11|work=MyNorthWest.com|accessdate=2009-08-14}}</ref>

在2009年上映、由[[珍妮佛·安妮斯顿|珍妮佛·安妮斯顿]]主演的電影《{{link-en|愛上你，愛上我|Love Happens}}》裡，有一些場景是于2008年在這堵牆前拍攝的。<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.seattlepi.com/local/354713_traveling12.html?source=mypi|title=Movie crews to close streets around Pike Place Market today|last=Mulady|first=Kathy|date=2008-03-12|newspaper=Seattle Post-Intelligencer|accessdate=2009-08-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://blog.seattlepi.com/thebigblog/archives/134535.asp?from=blog_last3|title='Traveling': Why Jen Aniston film was set in Seattle|last=Guzman|first=Monica|date=2008-03-18|newspaper=Seattle Post-Intelligencer|accessdate=2009-08-14}}</ref>
