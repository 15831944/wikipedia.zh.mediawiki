'''黑鷹水壩'''位於[[蒙大拿州|蒙大拿州]]，[[大瀑布城|大瀑布城]]，座落於[[密苏里河|密苏里河]]，是一座用於[[水力發電|水力发电]]的[[重力坝|重力壩]]<ref name="KFBBdam">[http://www.kfbb.com/news/local/56117072.html Black Eagle Dam Open to Public," KFBB-TV, August 29, 2009.] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110720024022/http://www.kfbb.com/news/local/56117072.html |date=2011年7月20日 }}</ref><ref>"Black Eagle Falls (Black Eagle Dam) is the only one in the city." See: "Great Falls Scores a Number of Firsts in Montana." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' March 20, 2005.</ref> 。第一大坝在1890年建成并开放，是一座[[水坝|土石坝]]<ref name="Peterson59">Peterson, p. 59.</ref> 。黑鷹水壩是蒙大拿州第一座使用此結構的水力发电坝<ref>Marcosson, p. 145; Holmes, Dailey, and Walter, p. 397.</ref><ref name="McCormick2">[https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/MT0323/ McCormick, p. 2.]</ref>，也是第一座建造在密苏里河的水壩。水壩使大瀑布城獲得了'''電力城'''的封號<ref>"City's Past Rooted in the River That Runs Through It." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' March 24, 2002.</ref>。第二大坝於1926年開工，1927年啟用，使用混凝土建成。第二大坝在不拆除第一大坝的情況下淹沒並取代它。自1926年以来，水壩為{{Convert|782|ft|m|lk=in}}长，{{Convert|34.5|ft|m}}高，幾乎沒有改變<ref name="FormS4">PPL Montana, Form S-4, p. A-23.</ref>，其發電廠有三台[[水轮机|涡轮机]]，每台能产生7[[瓦特|兆瓦]]的能量。最大输出達18兆瓦。第二大坝為蒙大拿電力公司建造，賓州電力（PPL）公司在1997年买下並在2014年賣給西北公司。水庫沒有正式的名字，多年來被稱作'''长池'''（Long Pool）<ref name="Peterson116">Peterson, p. 116.</ref>。水庫大约{{Convert|2|mi|km}} 长<ref name="Peterson82">Peterson, p. 82.</ref>，蓄水量從{{Convert|1,710|acre.ft|m3|lk=in}}<ref name="PPLMontana">[http://www.pplmontana.com/producing+power/power+plants/Black+Eagle+Dam.htm "Black Eagle Dam," PPL Montana, 2011.] {{Wayback|url=http://www.pplmontana.com/producing+power/power+plants/Black+Eagle+Dam.htm |date=20110715111220 }} Accessed 2011-04-05.</ref>到{{Convert|1,820|acre.ft|m3|lk=in}}<ref name="WaterRightSolutions">"The [//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Energy_Regulatory_Commission FERC] order issuing a new license dated September 27, 2000, states that the Black Eagle reservoir has a storage capacity of 1820 AF at normal maximum water surface elevation of 3290 feet." See: [http://www.greatfallsmt.net/studies_plans/AnalysisofClaimsWaterRights.pdf Water Right Solutions, Inc., p. 4.] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111003121947/http://www.greatfallsmt.net/studies_plans/AnalysisofClaimsWaterRights.pdf |date=2011-10-03 }}</ref>。黑鷹水壩的發電方式是[[川流式發電|川流式發電]] ，也就是它不刻意儲存水源來产生电力。

== 黑鹰瀑布 ==
黑鷹瀑布是[[美國|美國]][[蒙大拿州|蒙大拿州]][[密蘇里河大瀑布群|密蘇里河大瀑布群]]中的第一個瀑布。在蓋水壩之前，此瀑布高低差就有{{Convert|26.42|ft|m}}<ref name="Cutright">Cutright and Johnsgard, p. 26.</ref>。

黑鷹瀑布形成於[[大瀑布構造區|大瀑布構造區]]中，一條不平穩的下降沿上<ref name="Botkin">Botkin, p. 255.</ref>。密苏里河在此流經古代沉積在河流、冰川與湖泊下，大部分為非海[[砂岩|砂岩]]組成的庫圖納哈[[组_(地质学)|組]]<ref name="Fisher">[https://books.google.com/books?id=XUUMAAAAYAAJ&dq=Fisher%20Geology%20of%20the%20Great%20Falls%20Coal%20Field&pg=PA162#v=onepage&q=Fisher%20Geology%20of%20the%20Great%20Falls%20Coal%20Field&f=false Fisher, p. 162.]</ref>，少部分是從淺海堆積而成<ref>See, generally, Farshori and Hopkins, 1989; Haney and Schwartz, 2003.</ref>。河流侵蝕較軟的砂岩，剩下的硬岩便自然形成了瀑布。

[[曼丹族|曼丹族]]和南派崗黑腳族等[[美国原住民|美国原住民]]都知道黑鷹瀑布<ref>Robbins, 2008, p. 165; Howard, p. 16-17; Federal Writer's Project, p. 353.</ref>。1805年6月13日，[[刘易斯与克拉克远征|路易斯和克拉克远征隊]]的[[梅里韦瑟·刘易斯|梅里韋瑟·路易斯]]成为了第一位看到大瀑布的白人（五個瀑布中最大的）。第二天，探險隊在瀑布群附近露營時， 梅里韋瑟·路易斯发现了黑鹰瀑布。

{{Cquote|text=我来到了另一個{{convert|26|ft|m}}高的瀑布，瀑布下面有一個地方，老鷹已築巢於河流中間小島的三葉樹上。我相信她無法找到比那裡還難接近的地方，不論是男人或是野獸都不敢跨越切開她領地與河岸的危險鴻溝。河水也從如此高的地方落下，發散成高大的水霧。除了我在下面提到的那兩座之外，這個瀑布肯定是我見過最大的。它比[[波托马克河|波多馬克河]]或[[斯库尔基尔河|斯庫爾基爾河]]的瀑布還要更棒更高尚更迷人<ref>Lewis and Clark, p. 134-135 (grammar and punctuation in original).</ref>。}}

探險隊中某個成員稱瀑布為'''高音'''（Upper Pitch）<ref>Cutright, p. 156.</ref><ref name="Howard66">Howard, p. 66.</ref>。路易斯看到後稱其為'''黑鷹'''<ref>Vaughn, p. 83.</ref>。1872年，一位設計北太平洋铁路的定線工程師，托马斯‧P‧罗伯茨，在得知記錄在路易斯日誌的事件後，正式命名此瀑布为《黑鹰瀑布》<ref>Peterson, p. 20.</ref>。

=== 1890年的大坝 ===
[[File:Black_Eagle_Dam_-_pre-1908.jpg|左]]
'''大瀑布城水电公司'''（'''GFWPTC'''）成立于1887年<ref name="Almanac">''The Montana Almanac,'' p. 381.</ref>，目標是發展大瀑布城，提供電力、自來水與汙水的處理，以吸引工商業進入城市。鐵路巨頭[[詹姆士·希爾|詹姆士·希爾]]集合大北铁路的主要股東<ref name="Report223">[https://books.google.com/books?id=4vMnAAAAYAAJ&dq=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22%20dam%20Black%20Eagle&pg=PA223#v=onepage&q=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22%20dam%20Black%20Eagle&f=false ''Report of the Bureau of Agriculture Labor and Industry of the State of Montana,'' p. 223.]</ref>，5百万美元資本。 希爾把股票賣给他的朋友，菲利普·丹福斯·阿莫爾，查爾斯·亞瑟·布羅德沃特，馬歇爾·菲爾德，約翰·穆雷·福布斯，丹尼爾·威利斯·詹姆斯，約翰·斯圖爾特·肯尼迪，倫納德·劉易斯和查爾斯·埃利奧特·珀金斯<ref name="Albro">Martin, p. xxi.</ref>。巴黎‧吉布森是希爾的朋友，大瀑布城水电公司的股東之一，也是大瀑布城成立的推手<ref>Malone, 1996, p. 132; Guthrie, p. 20; Taliaferro, p. 120-121.</ref>。吉布森意識到，密蘇里河的大瀑布可以為工業提供豐富，便宜的電力，並在此基礎上推動城鎮發展<ref name="Taliaferro121">Taliaferro, p. 121.</ref>。但吉布森的计划意味着实际上要建设一个或更多水壩來提供想像中的電力。公司立刻開始購買密蘇里河沿岸的土地和水利權，從黑鷹瀑布一路買到綿羊溪，約{{Convert|12|mi}}<ref name="CompanyReport333">[https://books.google.com/books?id=h_UeAQAAIAAJ&dq=%22Black%20Eagle%20Dam%22&pg=PA333#v=onepage&q=%22Black%20Eagle%20Dam%22&f=false "Company Reports," p. 333.]</ref>。到最後，公司幾乎擁有了大瀑布城的所有土地，加上河岸邊的{{Convert|5000|acre|ha}}地产。公司僱傭一位[[水利工程|水利工程師]]J.T. Fanning，在1887年夏天調查黑鷹瀑布，以確定瀑布的適宜性和大壩的最佳位置。當時计划已完成，但没有采取任何行动<ref name="Parker58">[https://books.google.com/books?id=80xDAAAAYAAJ&dq=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&pg=PA58#v=onepage&q=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&f=false Parker, p. 58.]</ref>。

黑鷹水壩當時是為了供電給銅冶煉廠。在1887年7月22日，波士頓與蒙大拿銅銀綜合礦業公司創立，它是由倫納德‧路易森的路易森兄弟（一間銅貿易公司），蒙大拿公司（一間礦業控股公司）與C. X. Larrabee （擁有高產量的礦場，Larrabee和有名的山中礦）合併而成<ref>[https://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive-free/pdf?res=9A02E2DC1430E633A25750C2A9619C94669FD7CF "A Montana Mining Deal." ''New York Times.'' July 23, 1887]; [https://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive-free/pdf?res=FA0C15FE395412738DDDAF0894DB405B828CF1D3 "Death of Leonard Lewisohn." ''New York Times.'' March 6, 1902.]</ref>。 1889年9月12日，波士頓和蒙大拿州與GFWPTC簽署了一項協議，其中電力公司同意在1890年9月1日之前建造一座能夠為該礦業公司提供至少{{Convert|1000|hph|lk=on}}電力的水壩，並在1891年1月1日前達到{{Convert|5000|hph}}<ref name="Hyde86">Hyde, p. 86.</ref><ref name="McCormick3">[https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/MT0323/ McCormick, p. 3.]</ref>。作為交換，波士頓與蒙大拿銅銀綜合礦業公司同意在大壩附近建立一個30萬美元的銅冶煉廠。

1890年黑鷹水壩開始施工<ref name="Peterson60">Peterson, p. 60.</ref>，GFWPTC的工程師設計一個由厚重木材定型，裡面充滿岩石的溢流壩。這個壩並不大，目的是在其後面建造一個蓄水池，以產生足夠的水壓（[[扬程|揚程]]）推動渦輪機發電。這水壩建造在上游邊緣（盡可能創造最深的水庫）<ref name="StreetRailway249">[https://books.google.com/books?id=ogJBAQAAIAAJ&dq=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22%20dam%20Black%20Eagle&pg=PA249#v=onepage&q=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22%20dam%20Black%20Eagle&f=false "Great Falls, Mont.," p. 249.]</ref>，花費約515,000美元<ref name="ArmyCorps1906">[https://books.google.com/books?id=mRItAAAAIAAJ&dq=Boston%20and%20Montana%20smelter%20built%20%22Great%20Falls%22%201892&pg=PA1906#v=onepage&q&f=false ''Report of the Chief of Engineers...'', Part II, 1892, p. 1906.]</ref><ref>Another report claims the cost was only $275,000, but that may be for just the cost of labor and not materials and the associated powerhouses. See: "Great Falls, Mont.," p. 240.</ref>（通貨膨脹至2010年約12,335,000美元<ref name="StreetRailway2492">[https://books.google.com/books?id=ogJBAQAAIAAJ&dq=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22%20dam%20Black%20Eagle&pg=PA249#v=onepage&q=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22%20dam%20Black%20Eagle&f=false "Great Falls, Mont.," p. Parker, p. 58-59.]</ref>。所有投標都被拒絕，[[机械工程|機械工程師]]莫里斯‧帕克被雇來設計與建造大壩<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=80xDAAAAYAAJ&dq=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&pg=PA59#v=onepage&q=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&f=false Parker, p. 59.]</ref>，為了把河流從工作區域轉移開，1890年4月15日開始建造了圍堰壩<ref name="Parker60">[https://books.google.com/books?id=80xDAAAAYAAJ&dq=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&pg=PA58#v=onepage&q=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&f=false Parker, p. 59.]</ref><ref name="Parker64">[https://books.google.com/books?id=80xDAAAAYAAJ&dq=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&pg=PA64#v=onepage&q=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&f=false Parker, p. 64.]</ref>。在工地北邊的岸上蓋了名叫'''河岸'''（River Bank）的棚戶區，提供給幫助建造大壩的[[克罗地亚|克羅地亞人]]、[[斯堪的纳维亚|斯堪地那維亞人]]、[[斯洛伐克|斯洛伐克人]]與[[威爾士|威爾士人]]<ref {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101217074717/http://greatfallsmt.net/people_offices/plancomdev/histprev/RiversEdgeHistory.pdf |date=2010-12-17 Parker, p. 61-62.]</ref>，水壩的下游面間隔為1.05塊／公尺，<ref name="Parker61">[https://books.google.com/books?id=80xDAAAAYAAJ&dq=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&pg=PA61#v=onepage&q=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&f=false Parker, p. 61.]</ref> 頂部間隔1.08塊／公尺，延伸了{{Convert|8|ft|m}}。水壩南端建立一個{{Convert|30|ft|m}} 的石牆以保護發電廠，防止水壩向廠房的壓力。水壩頂部每{{Convert|48|ft|m}} 建造一個{{Convert|14|ft|m}}寬的混凝土疏洪道，共八條<ref name="Parker62">[https://books.google.com/books?id=80xDAAAAYAAJ&dq=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&pg=PA62#v=onepage&q=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&f=false Parker, p. 62.]</ref>。在壩頂加裝一條{{Convert|1|ft|m}}高的檔板以增加蓄水量<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=Ve5UAAAAYAAJ&dq=%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22%20reservoir%20depth&pg=RA7-PA26#v=onepage&q&f=false Geological Survey, p. 26.]</ref>。水壩{{Convert|15|ft|m}}高，{{Convert|1100|ft|m}}寬。

水壩在1890年12月開始發電<ref>Peterson, p. 86.</ref>。1891年1月6日，水可流過水壩的頂部，1891年3月15日，宣布水壩完工。

河的南邊建了一棟佔地{{Convert|40|x|165|ft}}, 磚瓦蓋的發電廠。內部有三台雙組橫流維克托式[[水輪發電機|水輪機]]，每台能夠產生1.27兆瓦的能量<ref name="StreetRailway250">[https://books.google.com/books?id=ogJBAQAAIAAJ&dq=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22%20dam%20Black%20Eagle&pg=PA250#v=onepage&q=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22%20dam%20Black%20Eagle&f=false "Great Falls, Mont.," p. 250.]</ref><ref name="Parker65">[https://books.google.com/books?id=80xDAAAAYAAJ&dq=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&pg=PA65#v=onepage&q=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&f=false Parker, p. 65.]</ref>。南岸和離岸一段距離的石牆間產生一個供給[[壓力鋼管|鋼管]]河水的調整池，水流入鋼管的量一部分由調整池上游的閘門控制<ref name="Minesand350">[https://books.google.com/books?id=mKc5AQAAIAAJ&dq=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22&pg=PA350#v=onepage&q=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22&f=false "Great Falls, Montana," p. 350.]</ref>。主要的鋼管長{{Convert|400|ft|m}}，直徑{{Convert|9|ft|m}}，提供發電機水源（另有其他三條鋼管建成，但沒有立即投入使用）。

河的北邊是另一座在1892年建成的發電廠，裡面有七八組維克托水輪機，共產生1.940兆瓦的能量，從河北岸底部切割出來的石塊堆積在附近形成一座小島：水渠島（Tailrace Island），而發電廠就蓋在這座人造島上<ref name="TalwaniRace">Talwani, Sanjay. "Race Is On at Tailrace Island." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' May 7, 1999.</ref>。島嶼與密蘇里河的北岸間形成了黑鷹水壩的水渠（也是發電廠的水渠）。水渠島由電力公司與波士頓蒙大拿礦業公司共同擁有。電廠與冶煉廠由一座木製天橋連接起來。北岸與石強形成筆直的調整池供應北邊的發電廠（最寬的部分有{{Convert|100|ft|m}}） 水的流量與南邊一樣，由閘門控制。三條鋼管供給北邊發電廠水源<ref name="Parker66">[https://books.google.com/books?id=80xDAAAAYAAJ&dq=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&pg=PA66#v=onepage&q=Parker%20%22Black%20Eagle%20Falls%20Dam%22&f=falsee Parker, p. 66.]</ref>。然而，此發電廠產生的機械能大部分並沒有轉成電能，而是透過繩索與滑輪系統直接轉移到冶煉廠<ref name="Hebgen1539">[https://books.google.com/books?id=zt1QAAAAYAAJ&dq=%22Black%20Eagle%20Dam%22%201890&pg=PA1539#v=onepage&q=%22Black%20Eagle%20Dam%22%201890&f=false Hebgen, p. 1539.]</ref>。

兩間電廠產生的部分機械能，透過湯姆遜-休斯頓發電機轉換成電力，單一員工在電廠裡工作八小時。次要的發電廠蓋在南邊發電廠隔壁，裡面裝載一台Armington & Sims蒸汽發電機，在主發電機關閉時，供應客戶電力。{{Convert|200|ft|m||adj=}}長的[[纜索鐵路|纜索鐵道]]（傾斜的鐵路）接通懸崖上面與南邊發電廠。懸崖上有多條{{Convert|200|ft|m}}長， {{Convert|10|ft|m}}寬的吊橋跨越河上連接各個發電廠<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=ogJBAQAAIAAJ&dq=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22%20dam%20Black%20Eagle&pg=PA250#v=onepage&q=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22%20dam%20Black%20Eagle&f=false "Great Falls, Mont.," p. 251.]</ref>。水壩預估可用50年。

==== 1890年水壩運作歷史 ====
1890年春天，冶煉廠開始動工<ref name="Hofman">[https://books.google.com/books?id=JudfAAAAMAAJ&dq=%22Boston%20and%20Montana%22%20smelter%20%22Great%20Falls%22%201887&pg=PA267#v=onepage&q=%22Boston%20and%20Montana%22%20smelter%20%22Great%20Falls%22%201887&f=false Hofman, p. 267–269.]</ref>；1891年3月，[[选矿机械|選礦機]]啟用<ref>A concentrator is a mechanical device which separates ore from dirt, debris, and tailings.</ref>；1892年4月，Brückner cylinders<ref>A Brückner cylinder is a rotating, brick-lined, horizontal metal cylinder which heats ore, burning off undesirable chemicals such as sulphur.</ref>與反射爐啟用；1892年8月，蒸餾轉換器啟用；1893年1月，精煉爐啟用<ref>A refining furnace melts the metal into a liquid. Undesirable material (or "dross") usually floats to the top, allowing it to be removed.</ref>；1893年2月，[[电解|電解精煉爐]]啟用；1893年4月，[[高爐|高爐]]啟用<ref>Mutschler, p. 13.</ref>。冶煉廠起初預算為200萬美元<ref>Raymer, p. 18.</ref>，在1892年員工數已達1000位。1908年4月7日，大煙囪（Big Stack）開始建造，底部內徑{{Convert|78.5|ft|m}}，頂部{{Convert|50|ft|m}}，將廢氣排到{{Convert|506|ft|m}}的高空<ref name="Minesand351">[https://books.google.com/books?id=mKc5AQAAIAAJ&dq=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22&pg=PA351#v=onepage&q=%22Great%20Falls%20Water%20Power%22&f=false "Great Falls, Montana," p. 351.]</ref>。由紐約的Alphonse Custodis建築公司於1908年10月23日完工，成為當時最高的煙囪<ref>Marcosson, p. 289.</ref>。

1893-1894年，GFWPTC在現有的南岸發電廠下游，建造了第二個發電廠，將電力輸送至懸崖上的皇家研磨公司的麵粉研磨廠。1897年，又在北岸旁建造另一個發電廠以提供冶煉廠更多電力。
[[File:Black_Eagle_Dam_-_Great_Falls_Montana_-_dynamited_in_1908_after_failure_of_Hauser_Dam.jpg|缩略图]]
1908年4月14日，下午2:30左右，大瀑布上游90英里處的豪瑟水壩潰堤<ref>Hall, ''Montana,'' 1912, p. 135; Jackson, ''Dams,'' 1997, p. 65-66; Wegmann, ''The Design and Construction of Dams,'' 1918, p. 298; Terzaghi, Peck, and Mesri, ''Soil Mechanics in Engineering Practice,'' 1996, p. 478.</ref>，高{{Convert|25|ft}}到{{Convert|30|ft}}的浪潮襲捲下游<ref>"Dam Bursts in Montana," ''New York Times,'' April 15, 1908.</ref>。大北鐵路指派一台火車頭沿路警告住在通往大瀑布城的鐵路周邊居民<ref>Smith, 1908, p. 237.</ref>。礦業公司的工人簡易地搭建了一個翼壩，以從冶煉廠轉移洪水，並炸開了一部分的黑鷹水壩以利洪水迅速流向下游<ref name="Peterson61">Peterson, p. 61.</ref><ref name="TwoTowns">"Two Towns Swept By Montana Flood," ''New York Times,'' April 16, 1908.</ref><ref name="EckeFloods">Ecke, Richard. "Floods of 1908: Disaster Struck Great Falls 100 Years Ago." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 8, 2008.</ref>。但當河水到城市時，密蘇里河只漲潮了{{Convert|7|ft}}<ref>Axline, "Hauser Dam," METNet.MT.gov, no date.</ref>。有兩名員工在爆破時身亡：一位在移除水壩的插板時溺死，另一位死於船隻翻覆。

同年的6月6日，黑鷹水壩遭受另一波洪水的攻勢。一天之內地區降雨量達7.6公分，密蘇里河在八小時內漲潮將近41公分<ref name="WilmotShape">Wilmot, Paula. "Today's Great Falls Begins to Take Shape." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' January 31, 1999.</ref>。河水比洪水位高出{{Convert|8.1|ft|m}}（密蘇里河的平均水位為{{Convert|15|ft|m}}），5位居民被淹死。冶煉廠試圖在黑鷹水壩北邊利用4輛滿載的[[鐵路車輛|火車車廂]]阻擋朝向發電廠的洪水 。許多建築被沖走，在當時是大瀑布城最嚴重的水災。一些建築撞上了黑鷹水壩，一些超過水壩的部分掉到瀑布下，大壩上的行人吊橋也被沖毀（今日，可以在第六街地下道看到一個牌子標示高水位的範圍。）大壩本身也有些受損，之後便修復完成，通往水渠島的舊木橋也用[[桁架桥|沃倫桁架橋]]取代，可行駛汽車。現在島上甚至有[[停車場|停車場]]供員工停車<ref name="WilmotEdge">Wilmot, Paula. "On the River's Edge." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 5, 2001.</ref>。

20世紀前期，黑鷹水壩的所有權改變了兩次。1908年，約翰‧D‧萊恩（[[比尤特_(蒙大拿州)|比尤特]]的達利信託與阿納康達銅礦公司總裁，之後成為助理國務卿<ref>Morris, p. 227; "John D. Ryan Dies Unexpectedly." ''New York Times.'' February 12, 1933.</ref>） 購買了GFWPTC絕大部分的股份<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=Gy_nAAAAMAAJ&dq=%22John%20D.%20Ryan%22%20Great%20Falls%20Power&pg=PA116#v=onepage&q=%22John%20D.%20Ryan%22%20Great%20Falls%20Power&f=false "Butte, Montana." ''Mining and Scientific Press.'' July 25, 1908.]</ref>。1910年，GFWPTC組織其子公司大瀑布電力公司，接管了黑鷹水壩及其他水力發電項目（當時正在建設）的所有權與經營權<ref name="McCormick6">[https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/MT0323/ McCormick, p. 6.]</ref>。1912年發生了第二次所有權變更。霍爾特水壩的成本超支，投資者對水壩建設的熱情減弱，以及與豪瑟水壩潰堤的責任使聯合密蘇里河電力公司的最大股東塞繆爾‧托馬斯‧豪瑟幾乎破產<ref>"Holter Hydroelectric Facility," HAER No. MT-94-A/HAER MONT 25-WOCRE-1A, Historic American Engineering Record, National Park Service, November 1994, p. 2.</ref><ref name="Clary137">Clary, ''Lewis & Clark on the Upper Missouri,'' 1999, p. 137.</ref>，使他必須售出聯合密蘇里河電力公司給約翰‧D‧萊恩。1912年10月25日，約翰‧D‧萊恩把兩間公司合併成比尤特電力公司、比靈斯與東蒙大拿電力公司與麥迪遜河電力公司，三間公司組成了蒙大拿電力公司<ref>Aarstad, et al., p. 125; Malone, 2006, p. 204; [https://books.google.com/books?id=MiAUAAAAIAAJ&dq=%22Great%20Falls%20Power%22%201912&pg=PA649#v=onepage&q=%22Great%20Falls%20Power%22%201912&f=false Goodsell and Wallace, p. 649.]</ref>。同年，黑鷹水壩產生的功率（3.9兆瓦至5.7兆瓦）輸往新的冶煉廠，而僅有 0.7兆瓦的電力輸送到附近城市。另外有300[[馬力|馬力]]的機械能來自[[水車|水車]]，並透過繩索與滑輪輸送往皇家麵粉廠。

1913年，蒙大拿電力公司升級了黑鷹水壩的產電能力（而不是機械能）。公司放棄了原先規劃的南岸第二座發電廠，改裝載兩台[[法蘭西斯式水輪機|法蘭西斯水輪機]]。第一座發電廠的舊發電機汰換成兩台可產生1.5兆瓦的新發電機<ref name="BigPowerHouse">[https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=vN1lAAAAIBAJ&sjid=Go8NAAAAIBAJ&pg=7055,4302472&dq=black-eagle-dam&hl=en "Big Power House Will Be Erected," ''The Troy Tribune,'' April 2, 1926.]</ref>。廠內又造了兩座調壓艙，並連接壓力管。翻新後的南方發電廠現在直接提供電力給冶煉廠和磨坊而不是機械能了。

===1926年的水壩===
1909年，大瀑布電力公司的工程師提議在三個地點蓋水壩：彩虹瀑布（彩紅水壩，Rainbow Dam）、大瀑布（瑞安水壩，Ryan Dam）、密蘇里河的峽谷（科克倫水壩，Cochrane Dam，位於前兩個水壩中間）。1914年，蒙大拿電力公司打算加高黑鷹水壩來增加發電能力<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=ER8rAAAAYAAJ&dq=%22Black%20Eagle%20Dam%22%201914&pg=PA1113#v=onepage&q=%22Black%20Eagle%22&f=false "Editorial Correspondence," p. 1113.]</ref>。1916年，大部分的波士頓與蒙大拿冶煉廠被現代銅礦與鋅礦的冶煉廠取代，同時加設電線跟電纜的製造廠<ref>Federal Writer's Project, p. 150.</ref>。雖然黑鷹水壩更新了渦輪機與發電機，它當時仍然只能產生3兆瓦的電力，而電力需求則一直提升。在1920年早期，黑鷹水壩已不足以提供大瀑布城的用電所需，再加上新的鋅冶煉廠需要10兆瓦的電力。1925年，北邊發電廠被認為嚴重的過時。

1925年末，蒙大拿電力公司計畫建設新的水壩與發電廠。查爾斯‧T‧馬恩（Charles T. Main）的公司負責監督水壩的重建，蒙大拿的顧問工程師亨利‧柯克倫（Harry Cochrane）負責重新設計水壩，因此此水壩稱作科克倫水壩（Cochrane Dam）<ref>"Talk of the Town." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 30, 2003.</ref>。1926年4月1日，蒙大拿電力公司宣布要花費100萬美元在黑鷹水壩建造新的發電廠。但隨著工作的進展，他們意識到水壩及其四座發電廠太舊，無法滿足發電需求。他們調整計畫，決定在水壩下游{{Convert|50|ft|m}}處新建一個水壩<ref name="McCormick7">[https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/MT0323/ McCormick, p. 7.]</ref><ref name="Aarstad25">Aarstad, et al., p. 25.</ref>。南邊的調整池被放棄，並保留北邊的調整池且好好地維修一番。1897年蓋的北岸發電廠被拆除，在同一地點蓋了混凝土的新發電廠。他們記取1908年的教訓，廠房設計與位置皆有以此作為考量。

拆除南岸發電廠的同年夏天，開始了新建水壩的工程。工人替新水壩澆灌混凝土時，舊水壩用來當作圍堰。新水壩於1927年8月1日完工，完全淹沒了舊水壩，在9月開始發電。新水壩長{{Convert|782|ft|m}}，高{{Convert|34.5|ft|m}}，溢洪道長{{Convert|646|ft|m}}。它的高度受到上游城市的淡水和污水設施限制，如果水壩更高，會淹沒設施<ref name="Peterson62">Peterson, p. 62.</ref>。水壩頂部呈現O形，可以改善水流。新大壩有八個水閘，與一條帶欄杆的人行道，讓維護人員能夠到達閘門和水壩<ref name="Peterson63">Peterson, p. 63.</ref>。[[File:Black_Eagle_Dam_-_turbines_and_governors_in_1996.jpg|缩略图]]
新發電廠由混凝土建成，不過公司曾計劃要建造成磚瓦屋，但當地磚匠要求獲得城市規定的工資，而不是蒙大拿州政府的，因後者薪資較低<ref name="RiversEdge5">[http://www.greatfallsmt.net/people_offices/plancomdev/histprev/RiversEdgeHistory.pdf River's Edge Trail Foundation, p. 5.] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101217074717/http://greatfallsmt.net/people_offices/plancomdev/histprev/RiversEdgeHistory.pdf |date=2010-12-17 }}</ref>。廠房南邊和北邊的混凝土和石砌牆用於防止水流侵蝕調整池和水渠道。調整池{{Convert|421|ft|m}}長，{{Convert|96|ft|m}}寬，延伸至較下游的新動力室，舊動力室的磚拱門結合到了調整池的牆壁中。新發電廠搭載三台由S.摩根史密斯公司提供的垂直[[卡布蘭式水輪機|卡布蘭式水輪機]]，能夠產生18兆瓦能量，成為蒙大拿電力公司在大瀑布地區五座水壩中最大產能的一座。史密斯公司在渦輪機上加裝油壓調速器（調節每個渦輪機的速度）與導流管（在水離開渦輪機後速度被降下來，並使得從上快速落下的水持續向渦輪機的螺旋槳傳遞能量）。渦輪機螺旋狀入口和導葉門固定在發電廠的地下室，每台渦輪機都安裝在自己的坑槽內。每台渦輪機通過一樓的屋頂連接到額定功率為7000[[伏安|伏安]]的阿里斯－查爾默斯（Allis-Chalmers）發電機。直流發電機作為阿里斯－查爾默斯發電機的激勵器。六台6,600 / 2,200伏[[变压器|变压器]]降低了電壓供使用。

1926年重建後，黑鷹水壩遭遇了四次洪水，但都沒有造成損害。第一次發生在1953年6月4日，春雨和融雪導緻密蘇里河高於洪水位{{Convert|4.1|ft|m}}<ref name="Searl182">Searl, p. 182.</ref>，沒有大壩損毀的報告。第二次在1964年6月10日，洛磯山脈的大雨導緻密蘇里河高於洪水位{{Convert|9.6|ft|m}}，比1908年的洪水高出{{Convert|1.5|ft|m}}。位於樺樹溪的Swift Dam潰堤，造成19人死亡<ref name="Parry">Parry, p. 125-126.</ref>，下雙麥迪遜湖水壩（Lower Two Medicine Lake Dam）也潰堤，造成9人死亡。儘管因大洪水造成3000人被迫離開家園，此次黑鷹大壩仍未受損。 （Mel Ruder因洪水報導而被授予突發新聞報導[[普利策奖|普利策獎]]<ref>Lawrence, p. 8-9.</ref>）。1975年6月21日發生第三次洪水，洛磯山脈24小時內降雨超過{{Convert|2|in|cm}}<ref name="Climate">National Climatic Center, p. June 1975-22.</ref>。在太陽河升至{{Convert|21|ft|m}}（高於洪水位{{Convert|6|ft|m}}）後，超過5000人從大瀑布城撤離<ref>"5,000 Persons Flee Montana Floods." ''Associated Press.'' June 22, 1975.</ref>。損失超過1700萬美元，{{Convert|21000|acre|ha}}的土地被淹沒。洪水幾乎淹沒了黑鷹大壩，但沒有對結構造成傷害<ref>Peterson, p. 64.</ref>。第四次大洪水在1997年6月17日<ref>Peterson, p. 8.</ref>，又多又快的融雪導緻密蘇里河高於洪水位{{Convert|3|ft|m}}<ref>"Floods a Menace in Idaho, Mont." ''USA Today.'' June 16, 1997.</ref>。黑鷹水壩還配備了一個緊急“trip face”可以解除水壩上所有插板，讓河水能夠以每秒鐘{{Convert|100000|cuft|m3}}的水量流過。

== 長池水庫 ==

黑鷹水壩在它後面創建了一個長約{{Convert|2|mi|km}}的水庫。緊鄰第一大壩的水庫大約{{Convert|17|ft|m}}深，容量{{Convert|459.1|acre.ft|m3|lk=in}}<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=qAvOAAAAMAAJ&lpg=PA336&ots=qvm5LjU9vA&dq=Black%20Eagle%20Dam%20reservoir%20depth&pg=PA336#v=onepage&q&f=false Ripley, p. 336.]</ref><ref>1892年測量長池的平均深度為{{Convert|3|ft|m}}。參見：[https://books.google.com/books?id=DZUAAAAAMAAJ&dq=%22Long%20Pool%22%20depth%20%22Missouri%20River%22&pg=PA265#v=onepage&q=%22Long%20Pool%22%20depth%20%22Missouri%20River%22&f=false ''Report of the Chief of Engineers...'', Part I, 1892, p. 265.]</ref>。目前水壩的蓄水量為{{Convert|1,710|acre.ft|m3}}到{{Convert|1,820|acre.ft|m3}}。

這水庫沒有正式名稱，但由於水流緩慢猶如靜止，以及從上游的[[喀斯喀特_(蒙大拿州)|喀斯喀特]]到黑鷹瀑布的坡度低（沿著蜿蜒的密蘇里河約{{Convert|55|mi|km}}長），這個水體一直被稱為「長池」。在1872年，命名黑鷹瀑布的托馬斯P‧羅伯茨，正式命名這個水體<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=4BUXAAAAYAAJ&dq=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&pg=PA256#v=onepage&q=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&f=false Roberts, p. 256-257.]</ref>。[[美國陸軍工兵部隊|美國陸軍工兵部隊]]於1883年和1910年使用過這名稱<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=MM9IAAAAMAAJ&dq=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&pg=PA1340#v=onepage&q=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&f=false ''Report of the Chief of Engineers...'' 1883, p. 1340]; [https://books.google.com/books?id=iWCy5TbiMhUC&dq=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&pg=PA406#v=onepage&q=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&f=false ''Annual Reports of the War Department for the Fiscal Year Ended June 30, 1903,'' p. 406]; [https://books.google.com/books?id=kiYSAAAAYAAJ&dq=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&pg=PA484#v=onepage&q=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&f=false ''Annual Reports of the War Department for the Fiscal Year Ended June 30, 1906,'' p. 484]; [https://books.google.com/books?id=LgtPAAAAIAAJ&dq=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&pg=PA645#v=onepage&q=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&f=false ''War Department Annual Reports,'' p. 645].</ref>。在1891年至1917年就已經廣為流行<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=3289AAAAYAAJ&dq=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&pg=PA513#v=onepage&q=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&f=false Sweetser and King, p. 513]; [https://books.google.com/books?id=DQU3AAAAMAAJ&dq=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&pg=PA1254#v=onepage&q=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&f=false Beach and McMurry, p. 1254].</ref>，[[蒙大拿州议会|蒙大拿州议会]]也已在此時使用這名稱<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=lVhNAAAAYAAJ&dq=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&pg=PA49#v=onepage&q=%22Long%20Pool%22%20Missouri&f=false Strahorn, p. 49.]</ref>。因此現代歷史學家也用這名稱提及它<ref>Lass, p. 379, 380.</ref>，1949年後被電力業出版物使用<ref>Nichols, p. 329.</ref>。現在，從第一林道北橋<ref>第一林道北橋在黑鷹水壩上游{{Convert|3.4|mi|km}}。</ref>到密蘇里河與太陽河交匯處（距離約{{Convert|1|mi|km}}）為知名的布羅德沃特灣（知名人士如：查爾斯‧阿瑟‧布羅德沃特、蒙大拿鐵路執行長、房地產投資者和銀行家）<ref>Peterson, p. 77.</ref>。

== 水壩開發 ==
1926年大壩重建後，工人和居民可以穿越大壩頂部上班或上學<ref name="WilmotStack">Wilmot, Paula. "The Big Stack." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' September 18, 2002.</ref>。出於安全原因，蒙大拿電力公司在[[第二次世界大战|二戰時期]]關閉了道路，並封閉到現今。

1950年，根據《聯邦電力法》，蒙大拿電力公司被迫授權黑鷹水壩等大部分發電用水壩。聯邦電力法案於1935年通過，在1937年12月，聯邦電力委員會（FPC）開始執行一項程序（FPC Docket IT-5840），賦予1935年之前建造的所有水壩許可證<ref>Gatchell, p. 50.</ref>。該法案賦予聯邦電力委員會在美國可航行水域中，所有水壩的控制權<ref>Plum, p. 398.</ref>。經過長時間的研究、數據收集以及談判之後，1946年11月18日開始舉行聽證會，以確定蒙大拿電力公司在1935年之前修建水壩前，河流是否可通航以致水壩屬法律生效範圍<ref>Smith, 1951, p. 316.</ref> 。1947年9月30日發布的第170號意見書中，審判員認為此河流可以通航，並且含黑鷹水壩等水壩應該獲得許可<ref>Federal Power Commission, p. 678.</ref><ref name="Navigable">"Navigability of Missouri Argued." ''Associated Press.'' February 17, 1948.</ref>。 蒙大拿電力公司在聯邦電力委員會於1948年2月16日舉行聽證會時訴願。當聯邦電力委員會駁回時，蒙大拿電力公司轉向[[哥伦比亚特区联邦上诉法院|哥伦比亚特区联邦上诉法院]]上訴。1950年10月4日，David L. Bazelon法官以2比1多數決判決「蒙大拿電力公司訴聯邦電力委員會案」<ref>''Montana Power Co. v. Federal Power Commission'', 185 F.2d 491 (App.D.C.Cir.; 1950), ''cert. den'd.'' 340 U.S. 947.</ref>，指出「密蘇里河從本頓堡到斯里福克斯長達263英里，屬美國通航水域，符合《聯邦電力法》的許可要求」<ref>''Montana Power Co. v. Federal Power Commission'', 185 F.2d 491, 495.</ref>。法院認為黑鷹、莫羅尼、彩虹、瑞安水壩都在通航水域，無權侵佔公有地<ref name="Upheld">"F.P.C. is Upheld on Dam Licenses." ''New York Times.'' October 5, 1950.</ref>。豪瑟水壩在通航水域，並持有無效許可證佔用公共土地。霍爾特水壩擁有有效許可證，但該許可證沒有延伸到它使用的通航水域。赫布根和麥迪遜水壩在不通航水域，但沒有有效許可證而佔用公有地。上訴法院將Canyon Ferry水壩的問題退還給地方法院，因為該水壩已被出售給聯邦政府。蒙大納州電力公司向美國最高法院提出上訴， [[美国最高法院|美国最高法院]]於1951年3月否決了特許權（拒絕審理此案）<ref>"United States Supreme Court." ''New York Times.'' March 13, 1951.</ref>。 蒙大拿電力公司隨後於2000年9月27日由聯邦能源管理委員會（FPC的繼承機構）重新授權並贏得了黑鷹水壩營運許可，許可證編號為P-2188。另外還有7座需要法院裁決。<ref>[http://ferc.gov/industries/hydropower.asp "Complete List of Issued Licenses." Licensing. Hydropower. Federal Energy Regulatory Commission. November 23, 2010.] Accessed 2011-04-11.</ref>

1978年，修復三台阿里斯－查爾默斯發電機，1982年，再修復一次。1997年，蒙大拿電力公司修復並重新粉刷了通往水渠島的橋。

1999年11月2日，蒙大拿電力公司宣布以16億美元的價格賣給賓州電力公司（PPL）所有水壩和發電廠<ref>Johnson, Charles S. "MPC to Sell Power Plants." ''The Missoulian.'' December 10, 1997; Anez, Bob. "PP&L Global Buying Montana Power Plants for $1.6 Billion," ''Associated Press,'' November 2, 1998; Kenworthy, Tom. "In Montana, a Volt Out of the Blue." ''Washington Post.'' March 4, 1998.</ref>。這筆包含黑鷹水壩的買賣估計會給蒙大拿州帶來3000萬美元的稅收<ref>Black, JoDee. "Rainbow Dam: PPL to Flow $175 Million Into Upgrade." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' February 27, 2008.</ref>（但蒙大拿電力公司表示稅金會比3000萬少<ref>"MPC Casts Doubt on $30 Million Tax Payment on Dam Sales." ''Associated Press.'' March 3, 1999.</ref>）。2001年11月，因電價上漲而不滿的民眾提出一項[[倡議|倡議]]：要求州政府收購PPL所有用來發電的水壩（包含黑鷹水壩）<ref>Gallagher, Susan. "Drive to Buy Montana Hydroelectric Dams Announced." ''Associated Press.'' November 20, 2001.</ref>。在2002年11月，這項倡議被否決了<ref>Berg, Christian. "Montanans Reject Buying PPL Dams." ''Allentown Morning Call.'' November 7, 2002.</ref>。

在蒙大拿電力公司將黑鷹水壩賣給PPL的時候，蒙大拿電力公司正在安裝控制裝置，使黑鷹水壩能夠從彩虹水壩遠程操作。當時，PPL表示他沒有計劃擴大黑鷹水壩的發電能力。同樣在1999年，蒙大拿電力公司將大部分的水渠島轉移到大瀑布市作為娛樂區<ref name="WilmotRebirth">Wilmot, Paula. "The Rebirth of Smelter Hill." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 21, 1999.</ref>。此外，蒙大拿電力公司和ARCO（波士頓和蒙大拿州的後繼公司）在該島上鋪設了{{Convert|18|in|cm}}（46公分）的表土，花費了50萬美元用於景觀設計，並增強了動力廠附近的安全邊界，並同意資助一部分小島的定期保養費用。

1990年代後期，歷史學家[[史蒂芬·安布羅斯|史蒂芬·安布羅斯]]建議拆除黑鷹水壩，將瀑布恢復原狀<ref>"The Edge." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' December 11, 1999.</ref>。

2010年，黑鷹水壩的發電與輸電系統開始翻新。同年6月，PPL蒙大拿州分公司花費5500萬美元用[[高压电线|高压电线]]取代該地區電力網路<ref name="Upgrade">"PPL Planning $55 Million Transmission Upgrade." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 21, 2010.</ref>。新的電線取代了80多年前的設備，電線間隔更大，使得大型鳥類可以安全地通過或棲息在電線上。PPL也計畫更新電廠旁的變電所，兩項工程都預期會在2012年完工。

=== 沉積物，水流量, debris issues, and emergencies ===
密蘇里河與支流夾帶的沉積物是黑鷹水壩的一大問題。太陽河（Sun river）在黑鷹大壩上游幾英里處與密蘇里河相連，夾帶著汙泥，養份與藻類，因此還被諷刺成“浮渣河（Scum river）”<ref name="Scum">"Sun River Cleanup Effort Has Done Much in Short Time." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' August 3, 2000.</ref>。 太陽河水呈現如[[巧克力牛奶|巧克力牛奶]]的顏色，流入相對清澈的密蘇里河後使其變得汙濁。汙泥降低水庫容量，充滿調整池，也會對發電機組造成損害。1988年，由於密蘇里河從太陽河到彩虹水壩一段的沉積物和懸浮物，蒙大拿州環境質量部（Montana Department of Environmental Quality, MDEQ）根據1972年清淨水法第303（d）節<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.edf.org.tw/news/news_content.asp?web_news_serial=830|title=美國環保署完成清淨水法規制定以保護溪流和濕地|accessdate=2018-08-09|author=|date=2015-05-27|publisher=環境與發展基金會|quote=該法規確保受清淨水法（Clean Water Act）保護的水體有更精確的定義}}</ref>，將其水質狀態列入受損<ref name="DEQB58">[http://cwaic.mt.gov/wqrep/2010/2010FinalIntegratedReport.pdf Montana Department of Environmental Quality, p. B-58.]</ref><ref>[http://www.greatfallsmt.net/studies_plans/watermasterplan/Appendix8.pdf Great Falls Public Water System, p. 7.] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101217060259/http://greatfallsmt.net/studies_plans/watermasterplan/Appendix8.pdf |date=2010-12-17 }}</ref>。黑鷹水壩因為減緩水流流速，被列為問題來源之一<ref name="DEQA64">[http://cwaic.mt.gov/wqrep/2010/2010FinalIntegratedReport.pdf Montana Department of Environmental Quality, p. A-64.]</ref>。為了解決這個問題，1994年，7個聯邦機構，8個州政府機構，10個蒙大拿州政府機構，4個環境組織和幾個蒙大拿州地主共同努力減少進入太陽河及其主要支流（Muddy Creek與Careless Creek）的營養物和沉積物<ref name="OfficeofWater">[http://www.epa.gov/owow/NPS/success/state/pdf/mt_uppersun.pdf Office of Water, October 2007.]</ref>。約623,500美元的清潔水法基金、200萬美元的聯邦基金以及250萬美元的州與地方基金用於恢復河岸植被、改善河邊放牧、復原河床斜度和改善灌溉方法。到2010年，Muddy Creek減少75%沉積物，Careless Creek減少25%<ref>[http://water.epa.gov/polwaste/nps/success319/Section319III_MT.cfm "Montana: Section 319 Success Stories." Vol. III. Office of Water. U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. October 13, 2010.] Accessed 2011-04-09.</ref>。

黑鷹瀑布是否流通，取決於黑鷹水壩。每年5月，理論上密蘇里河的中位水流量為每秒{{Convert|11200|cuft|m3}}<ref name="FallsNotGreat">Lee, Sonja. "Falls Won't Be So Great." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' May 25, 2001.</ref>。2000年10月，水壩水位下降，瀑布停止流水四週，PPL蒙大拿在這段時間修整了調整池，閘門和鋼管濾網<ref>"PPL to Work On Black Eagle Dam." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' October 26, 2000.</ref>。PPL蒙大拿的工作許可證要求水壩在周末與[[亡兵紀念日|亡兵紀念日]]至[[劳动节_(美国)|劳动节]]期間的上午9點至下午8點流出至少每秒{{Convert|200|cuft|m3}}的水量。如果水位或發電需求允許，水量可能更多。2001年5月，PPL蒙大拿表示因為水流量只有平時的40%，他們要在7月4日後截斷瀑布<ref name="NotMuch">"Not Much Waterfall Action Likely in Great Falls This Summer." ''Associated Press.'' May 26, 2001.</ref><ref name="FercApproved">"PPL's Request to Shut Down Dams This Summer Approved by FERC." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 2, 2001.</ref>。2004年9月，水庫水位再次降低，以修復檔板<ref name="BlackLowers">Black, JoDee. "PPL Lowers River to Improve Dam." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' September 18, 2004.</ref>，河流缺水使巨人泉附近的密蘇里河天然通道暴露出來。2008年6-7月，水庫水位又過低以致瀑布再次停流，而在一次大雨後移除了檔板使水流快速通過<ref name="MudFlats">"Replacement of Flashboards Creates Temporary Mud Flats on the Missouri near Black Eagle Dam." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' July 2, 2008.</ref>。當水流通過並把檔板歸位後，水庫內剩沒多少水，而瀑布再次流通也是幾天後的事了。
[[File:1962AlvisStalwart.jpg|左]]
Because Black Eagle Dam is an overflow dam, it is common for debris to jam against the dam or obstruct the penstock intakes. Driftwood and logs are the most common forms of debris, but unusual objects such as [[船塢|boat docks]], bowling balls, [[划艇|canoes]], hot tubs, and small wooden and steel sheds and buildings also sometimes become lodged against the dam.<ref name="SchulzBob">Schulz, Kathleen. "Bob: Bound for New Orleans?" ''Great Falls Tribune.'' March 27, 2004.</ref> Removing hollow items can be dangerous, because the water pressure against them can cause them to implode. In addition to large amounts of driftwood, about 100 animal carcasses (cattle, deer, dogs, and various small animals) become lodged against the dam or the penstock intake screens each year. Floating screens at the entrance to the forebay catch many items, as do the penstock intake screens.<ref name="HallTour">Hall, Ryan. "Hundreds Tour Black Eagle Dam." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' August 30, 2009.</ref> Workers use a crane to remove items from these screens, and bury the items retrieved at a landfill. Dam operators also use a yellow civilian version of the Alvis Stalwart amphibious vehicle to remove objects, and to maintain the dam.<ref>"PPL Montana Holding Open House." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' August 26, 2009.</ref> (This vehicle caused a stir in Great Falls in June 2006, when local residents driving on a nearby road mistook the vehicle for a [[运动型多用途车|sport utility vehicle]] which had fallen into the water.)<ref>"Amphibious Vehicle in River Causes Stir." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 24, 2006.</ref> Ice is another form of dangerous debris for the dam. In December 2010, blocks of floating ice overwhelmed the top of the dam, tearing the handrails off the maintenance walkway.<ref>Evaro, Donna. "Missouri River Ice Jam Being Watched Closely." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' December 23, 2010.</ref>

For many years, Black Eagle Dam's owners were required to hold simulations every five years to plan for various kinds of emergencies (flood, sabotage, mechanical breakdown, etc.) which might affect the dam and public safety.<ref name="AdcockPrepare">Adcock, Clifton. "Are We Prepared?" ''Great Falls Tribune.'' January 16, 2006.</ref> After the [[九一一袭击事件|September 11 attacks]], these exercises were required to happen every year. PPL Montana works with local and state government and law enforcement, other companies, and the news media to plan for various contingencies and improve how it might respond in a disaster or emergency.

=== Tax dispute and Supreme Court riverbed case ===
Taxation of the dam has been an issue of contention since 2000.  During the 2002 ballot initiative fight, PPL Montana said its dams were worth at least $767 million.<ref name="LawyerCalls">[http://billingsgazette.com/news/state-and-regional/montana/article_f07e6bed-67a2-56cd-a1ab-116930a929fa.html "Lawyer Calls PPL Protest Short-Sighted." ''Associated Press.'' April 27, 2004.]</ref><ref>"Montanans Reject Buying PPL Dams." ''Allentown Morning Call.'' November 7, 2002.</ref> But in 2003, PPL argued that the dams were worth much less. PPL Montana challenged the amount of property taxes assessed on Black Eagle and its four other dams in the area, protesting 10.87 percent of the $4.48 million 2000 tax assessment, 6.5 percent of the $4.8 million 2001 tax assessment, and 85.65 percent of the $4.96 million tax assessment.<ref name="Propertytaxes">"Property Taxes Paid By Private Electricity Generating Plants and Dams in Montana." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' January 26, 2003.</ref> This amounted to 30 percent of its total property tax assessment in the state of Montana—with one-third of amount owed payable solely in [[喀斯喀特縣_(蒙大拿州)|Cascade County]]. In February 2005, the Montana State Tax Appeal Board gave the state a partial victory, reducing the state's assessments by 10 percent but otherwise upholding the assessment method.<ref name="DennisonAppeal">Dennison, Mike. "State Wins PPL Tax Appeal." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' February 12, 2005; Johnson, Charles S. "Revenue Dept. Still Assessing Impact of Ruling." ''Billings Gazette.'' February 15, 2005.</ref> However, the tax board did not address PPL Montana's primary claim that the state was assessing its plants differently solely because they were unregulated. PPL Montana appealed the ruling to the Montana Supreme Court in 2007.<ref>[http://www.mtstandard.com/news/state-and-regional/article_2283bd10-1172-5926-9b42-b817c2f0a739.html "Supreme Court Hears PPL Montana Tax Appeal." ''Montana Standard.'' June 13, 2007.]</ref> On December 4, 2007, the Montana Supreme Court unanimously ruled in ''State Dept. of Revenue v. PPL Montana''<ref>[http://caselaw.findlaw.com/mt-supreme-court/1081964.html ''State Dept. of Revenue v. PPL Montana'', 2007 Montana 310 (2007).]</ref> that the tax appeal board had properly assessed PPL Montana's dams.<ref name="DennisonResolve">[http://helenair.com/news/state-and-regional/article_60b9296a-100e-51ae-a44c-99251969d91f.html Dennison, Mike. "PPL Wants to Resolve Tax Protest." ''Helena Independent Record.'' December 14, 2007.]</ref> However, PPL Montana had continued to dispute its tax assessments from 2003 to 2007, and the ruling did not directly address those challenges. But on December 14, 2007, PPL Montana offered to pay its 2003-to-2007 tax assessments at the slightly lower rate established by the tax appeal board.

Another tax issue arose in 2007. The parents of several Montana schoolchildren filed suit in the [[美国蒙大拿联邦地区法院|United States District Court for the District of Montana]] against PPL Montana.<ref name="Hurley">[https://www.nytimes.com/gwire/2010/11/01/01greenwire-supreme-court-asks-obama-admin-to-weigh-in-on-55210.html Hurley, Lawrence. "Supreme Court Asks Obama Admin to Weigh In on Riverbed Dispute." ''New York Times.'' November 1, 2010.]</ref> The parents argued that all riverbeds in Montana are owned by the state, are held in trust for the benefit of all the people, and that such riverbeds constitute a portion of state lands which must be taxed or leased to provide support for public schools.<ref name="Kids3">[http://www.eenews.net/assets/2010/11/01/document_gw_01.pdf ''PPL Montana v. State of Montana'', 2010 MT 64 (2010) at ¶3.]</ref> The plaintiffs argued that PPL Montana and its predecessor, Montana Power, only leased these riverbeds from the state and did not own them. The parents sued to force PPL to pay rent due. (At no time in the past had the state sought rental income from Montana Power, and no payments by Montana Power or PPL Montana had ever been made.) Although the federal suit was dismissed for lack of a federal issue, PPL Montana filed suit in Montana's First Judicial District Court seeking a declaratory judgment in its favor, arguing that the Federal Power Act preempts the parents' suit and denies states the power to make companies pay for the use of riverbeds.<ref>[http://www.eenews.net/assets/2010/11/01/document_gw_01.pdf ''PPL Montana v. State of Montana'', 2010 MT 64 (2010) at ¶6.]</ref> The state district court held against PPL's request for summary judgment on all counts.<ref>[http://www.eenews.net/assets/2010/11/01/document_gw_01.pdf ''PPL Montana v. State of Montana'', 2010 MT 64 (2010) at ¶11-¶66.]</ref> The two parties then went to trial, which lasted from October 22 to October 30, 2007.<ref>[http://www.eenews.net/assets/2010/11/01/document_gw_01.pdf ''PPL Montana v. State of Montana'', 2010 MT 64 (2010) at ¶67.]</ref> On June 13, 2008, the state district court ruled in favor of the state (although it denied the state's request for interest on the rent due).<ref>[http://www.eenews.net/assets/2010/11/01/document_gw_01.pdf ''PPL Montana v. State of Montana'', 2010 MT 64 (2010) at ¶68-¶77.]</ref> PPL Montana appealed to the Montana State Supreme Court.  On March 30, 2010, the Montana State Supreme Court held (5-to-2) that the state district court had not erred in its rulings on various issues of summary judgment, that riverbeds were state public trust lands (but not school trust lands) under the Montana Constitution, and that the district court's calculation of payments due was appropriate.<ref name="DennisonNoCase">[http://billingsgazette.com/news/state-and-regional/montana/article_e3bf1f7a-d007-11df-a698-001cc4c002e0.html Dennison, Mike. "State: PPL, Others Have No Case for U.S. Supco Appeal of Riverbed-Rental Case." ''Billings Gazette.'' October 4, 2010.]</ref><ref>[http://www.eenews.net/assets/2010/11/01/document_gw_01.pdf ''PPL Montana v. State of Montana'', 2010 MT 64 (2010) at ¶78-¶173.]</ref> PPL Montana, appealed to the [[美国最高法院|Supreme Court of the United States]], and in November 2010 the Supreme Court asked the United States Solicitor General for the views of the federal government. Finally, on February 22, 2012, the U.S. Supreme Court held in ''PPL Montana v. Montana'' (No. 10–218, dec. February 22, 2012) that the Montana Supreme Court had erred in finding all of the riverbeds beneath all PPL Montana dams navigable.  The unanimous U.S. Supreme Court held that the correct test is whether the portion of the riverbed under each specific dam (not the entire river, nor even a large or small portion of a river) was navigable at the time of statehood.  If it was, then the riverbed is "navigable" and may be taxed.  The case was remanded back to the state supreme court for further hearings.<ref>Dennison, Mike. "U.S. Supreme Court Overturns Montana Courts on PPL Riverbed Rent." ''Billings Gazette.'' February 23, 2012.</ref>

Flashboards on the dam were slightly damaged during flooding in the spring and early summer of 2011.  Water behind the dam was lowered until it barely crested the dam so repair crews could replace the flashboards.<ref>[http://www.kfbb.com/news/local/Missouri-River-Lowered-for-Maintenance-to-Black-Eagle-Dam-125850413.html "Missouri River Lowered for Maintenance to Black Eagle Dam." KFBB-TV.  July 19, 2011.] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120329151653/http://www.kfbb.com/news/local/Missouri-River-Lowered-for-Maintenance-to-Black-Eagle-Dam-125850413.html |date=2012年3月29日 }} Accessed 2011-09-30.</ref>

== Recreational aspects and fishery management ==

=== Fishing and water quality issues ===
[[File:Sander_vitreus.jpg|左]], a popular sport fish caught at Black Eagle Dam.]]
The river above and below Black Eagle Dam is used for fishing. Westslope cutthroat trout were first identified at Black Eagle Falls by Lewis and Clark in 1805, and the fish still are plentiful in the area.<ref>Behnke and Tomelleri, p. 139.</ref> [[虹鱒|Rainbow trout]] are also plentiful upstream from the dam.<ref name="Evaro">Evaro, Donna. "FWP Seeks Comments on Fishing Regulations for Season Starting in March." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' August 12, 2010.</ref> The area below Black Eagle Falls has been rated by one guide as a good area for [[飛蠅釣|fly fishing]] [[小口黑鲈|smallmouth bass]] and [[玻璃梭鱸|walleye]].<ref>Robbins, 2005, p. 323.</ref> For many years, the state of Montana managed the Long Pool reservoir as part of one fishing management area, and the river below the dam as part of another.<ref name="BabcockPlanOut">Babcock, Michael. "Missouri Fisheries Plan Is Out." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' September 23, 1999.</ref> This was changed in 1999, so that the two are now managed together. The change also instituted a new limit on [[鱒魚|trout]] caught and killed below the dam of five fish per day (only one of which could be over {{Convert|18|in|cm}}). Limits on the number of walleye caught per day were lifted in 2010 to protect the rainbow and brown trout populations (they had been five daily and 10 in possession).<ref>Babcock, Michael. "Anglers Disagree Over Proposed New Fishing Regs." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' September 2, 2010.</ref> According to dam officials, there are no screens to prevent fish from going over the dam or through the penstocks and turbines. Fish "go right through the turbines, it doesn't seem to bother them," dam operators say.

Since 1988, the Long Pool and the Missouri River in and around the city of Great Falls have been listed as an "impaired" waterway under the 1972 Clean Water Act. This area was first listed as impaired due to sedimentation, siltation, and suspended solids in 1988. High levels of [[铬|chromium]], [[汞|mercury]], and [[硒|selenium]] were listed as impairing factors in 1992. High [[濁度|turbidity]] (haziness of water due to suspended particles) was added as an impairing factor in 2000. Sources of these impairments include Black Eagle Dam, upstream abandoned mines, irrigation runoff, industrial sources, and stormwater runoff. The MDEQ has given the area a quality ranking of B-2 (fourth out of 18), declaring it suitable for human consumption and recreation (after treatment) but only marginal for [[鮭科|salmonid]] fish, other aquatic animals, waterfowl and fur-bearing animals.<ref>[http://cwaic.mt.gov/wqrep/2010/2010FinalIntegratedReport.pdf Montana Department of Environmental Quality, p. 3-3, A-64.]</ref> MDEQ has scheduled the Long Pool and Missouri River watershed in this area for future improvement.

In 2011, the former smelter next to Black Eagle Dam was listed by the [[美国国家环境保护局|United States Environmental Protection Agency]] (EPA) as a Superfund hazardous and toxic waste site.<ref name="Puckett">Puckett, Karl. "EPA Adds Black Eagle Site to Superfund List." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' March 9, 2011.</ref> The EPA also said it would begin sampling for the riverbed above and below Black Eagle Dam, as well as residential areas in the town of Black Eagle, for [[重金属|heavy metals]] contamination. Historic records show that plant wastes were routinely dumped into the Missouri River below Black Eagle dam.<ref name="EPARelease">Environmental Protection Agency. "EPA Adds Anaconda Copper Mining Co. Smelter and Refinery in Cascade County to Superfund Site List." Press release. March 8, 2011.</ref><ref name="Dirty">[http://www.greatfallstribune.com/multimedia/stack/page19.html Ecke, Richard. "Stack's Environmental History." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' September 27, 2007.]</ref> MDEQ estimated in 2002 that between {{Convert|27500000|cuyd|m3}} and {{Convert|31000000|cuyd|m3}} of waste were dumped into the river between 1892 and 1915. EPA samples indicated that the contamination could extend as far downstream as [[本顿堡_(蒙大拿州)|Fort Benton]], {{Convert|34|mi|km}} away. Toxins present in the water and riverbed, according to the EPA, include [[锑|antimony]], [[砷|arsenic]], [[镉|cadmium]], chromium, [[钴|cobalt]], [[铜|copper]], iron, [[铅|lead]], [[锰|manganese]], mercury, [[镍|nickel]], [[銀|silver]], [[钠|sodium]], and zinc.

=== Wildlife and hunting ===
The area around the falls, despite its urban setting, is a habitat for many animal and bird species. Among the birds commonly found in the area are [[白頭海鵰|bald eagles]], California gulls, [[加拿大雁|Canada geese]], cliff swallows, [[黑頸鸊鷉|eared grebes]], egrets, [[金雕|golden eagles]], [[美洲金翅雀|American goldfinches]], gray catbirds, house wrens, [[朱鷺亞科|ibises]], [[鹗|ospreys]], [[赤颈鸊鷉|red-necked grebes]], [[沙丘鹤|sandhill cranes]], [[雪雁|snow geese]], [[小天鹅|tundra swans]], [[红头美洲鹫|turkey vultures]], western grebes, [[西草地鷚|western meadowlarks]], white pelicans, and numerous species of [[鸭|duck]], [[貓頭鷹|owl]], and warbler.<ref>Robbins, 2008, p. 165-166; Crawford, p. 167; Tirrell and Reddy, p. 48.</ref> Common animal species in the area include [[河狸|beavers]], [[騾鹿|mule deer]], [[麝鼠|muskrats]], [[北美水獺|river otters]], and [[白尾鹿|white-tailed deer]].<ref>Robbins, 2008, p. 166.</ref> Beavers are so common in the area that officials consider them pests, and trees in city parks have had to be protected against them.<ref>Kotynski, Tom. "Talk of the Town." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 7, 1999.</ref> (At one point, beavers were chewing down two trees nightly on Black Eagle Memorial Island.<ref>Ecke, Richard. "Beavers Leave Their Mark." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' November 30, 2010.</ref> These included two red oak trees found growing on the hill above the dam—the only two red oak trees in northcentral Montana). As of 2006, most of the north shore of the Missouri River below Black Eagle Dam was undeveloped as far down a Sulphur Spring (a distance of about {{Convert|18|km}}).<ref name="LeeCleanup">Lee, Sonja. "Cleanup Project Planned for Today." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' September 30, 2006.</ref> Known as the Lewis and Clark Greenway, this area is under a permanent [[保护地役权|conservation easement]].

Although the area below Black Eagle Dam is open for bird hunting, the reservoir and river upstream from Black Eagle Dam to Sand Coulee Creek is a no-hunting area.<ref>Babcock, Michael. "For Area Goose Hunters." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' January 13, 2005.</ref>

Wildlife hunting is also available on the north shore of the Missouri River from below Black Eagle Dam to Morony Dam.<ref name="CheckRules">"Check Rules Before Heading Out to Hunt on State Lands." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' August 31, 2006.</ref> Most of this land is owned by PPL Montana, but there is some private land here which is not open to hunting. The PPL Montana lands are open to hunting due to a conservation easement, but the Montana Department of Fish, Wildlife and Parks requires hunters to obtain a map of these lands before using them for hunting. There are also restrictions on the type of weapons which may be used in this area, and where hunters may park.

=== Recreation area improvements ===
[[File:Lewis_and_Clark_National_Historic_Trail_Interpretative_Center_-_Great_Falls_Montana.jpg|左]]
Black Eagle Dam is also utilized as a recreation area. The whitewater just below the falls is a good place for [[划艇|canoeing]], inner-tube floating, and [[皮艇|kayaking]].<ref>Fischer and Fischer, p. 129; Babcock, Michael. "Where to Float Your Boat." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 5, 2003; Harrison, Elizabeth. "Outdoor Fun Begins With Indoor Kayak Class." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' January 31, 2010.</ref> Hiking and bicycling also occur in the area. Beginning in 2006, the Black Eagle Dam Run (a {{Convert|5|km|mi|adj=on}} run/walk and {{Convert|10|km|mi|adj=on}} run) has been held at Black Eagle Dam in mid-July.<ref>"Regional Briefs." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' July 8, 2006.</ref> The annual Lewis and Clark Festival, celebrated in late June (the anniversary of the discovery of the Great Falls), focuses on events held around Black Eagle and other dams on the Great Falls of the Missouri.<ref name="Sorich">Sorich, Jake. "Luminaria Walk is Saturday." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 25, 2010.</ref> In mid-July, the River's Edge Trail Luminaria Walk occurs around Black Eagle Dam and the falls, and are illuminated with colored spotlights during the event. On May 5, 1998, the [[美国国家森林局|United States Forest Service]] opened the {{Convert|25000|sqft|m2|adj=on}} Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center, a $6 million museum and education center (which includes an exhibit hall, theater, store, and classrooms) which tells the story of Lewis and Clark's passage through central Montana and their discovery of the Great Falls of the Missouri.<ref>Skornogoski, Kim. "L&C Center's Sixth Birthday." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' May 3, 2004; "Indians Unsung Heroes for Lewis and Clark." ''Deseret News.'' May 23, 1999; "Lewis and Clark Center Details Indian Influence." ''USA Today.'' June 26, 1998; Dresser, Michael and Dresser, Sheila. "A Symbol of Real American West: Waterfall." ''Baltimore Sun.'' October 13, 1997.</ref>

Much work has been done in the past two decades to create public access to and public viewing areas of the dam and falls. For decades, two gravel spaces existed on River Drive North near the Bob Speck Municipal Golf Course where drivers could stop, get out, and view Black Eagle Falls and the dam.<ref>Uda, Take; Newhouse, Eric; Winslow, Larry; and Loznak, Robin. "Lewis & Clark Portage Then and Now." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 26, 2005.</ref> In 1991, the River's Edge Trail, a paved and gravel pedestrian [[小徑|recreational trail]], opened along tracks of the former Burlington Northern Railroad on the south bank of the Missouri River at Black Eagle Dam.<ref name="Survivor">Ecke, Richard. "Trail a Real 'Survivor'." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 2, 2001.</ref> In 2001, Tailrace Island was renamed Black Eagle Memorial Island (in honor of those workers who lost their lives while working on or at the dam) and turned over to the River's Edge Trail. A new {{Convert|10|mi|km|-long|adj=mid}} section of the trail linking the existing {{Convert|14|mi|km|-long|adj=mid}} trail was also opened that year. The same year, a public boathouse was constructed on Black Eagle Memorial Island for use by canoeists, kayakers, and other watercraft; observation decks; public restrooms; and parking lot. The boat landing was completed in October 2001.<ref name="WilmotLanding">Wilmot, Paula. "New Canoe Landing Going In Below Black Eagle Dam." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' October 19, 2001.</ref> Construction also began on a {{Convert|0.5|mi|km|-long|adj=mid}} section of trail designed to link Art Higgins Memorial Park (on the north bank of the Missouri River just behind the dam) with the island. Most of the construction costs were paid for by PPL Montana. In 2001, another segment of the trail was added, leading from the Black Eagle Memorial Island spur up Smelter Hill.<ref name="Improvements">Johannes, Katie N. "2002 Planned Trail Improvements." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' January 14, 2002.</ref> The {{Convert|2950|ft|m|-long|adj=mid}} trail was paid for by a Community Transportation Enhancement Project grant.<ref>The Community Transportation Enhancement Program is a program administered by the Montana Department of Transportation which provides small grants to transportation-related projects that strengthen the cultural, aesthetic, and environmental aspects of transportation. The program's focus is on non-traditional projects.</ref> There is no fee to enter Black Eagle Memorial Island, which is open daily to the public from 9 A.M. to 8 P.M. and is maintained by the Montana Department of Fish, Wildlife and Parks.<ref>"Outdoor Notebook." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' March 25, 2004.</ref> In 2004, the River's Edge Trail was extended along the north bank of the Missouri River from the Black Eagle Dam powerhouse westward about {{Convert|1.3|mi|km}} to the 15th Street Bridge.<ref name="WilmotKicks">Wilmot, Paula. "City Kicks Cash Toward Soccer Park, Trail." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 2, 2004.</ref> About $175,000 of the $204,500 cost of the extension was paid for by the Community Transportation Enhancement Program, and the remainder by the Montana Air Congestion Initiative (a state program), the American Public Land Exchange (a nonprofit organization), and Recreational Trails (a nonprofit which manages the trail).<ref name="LeeIsland">Lee, Sonja. "Island Day-Use Area Open to Public." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 3, 2005.</ref> Black Eagle Memorial Island opened to the public on June 2, 2005.<ref>"River's Edge Trail Runs 25 miles Over Hill and Dale." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' March 20, 2005; Babcock, Michael. "Come for Ice Cream, Stay for the View." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' June 2, 2005.</ref>

The development of the River's Edge Trail and the growing importance of riverside parks and attractions around Black Eagle Dam and along the banks of the Missouri River led the city of Great Falls to develop its first Missouri River Urban Corridor Plan in 2003.<ref name="TrailMaster">"River's Edge Trail." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' May 31, 2003.</ref> The master plan, which covered both banks of the Missouri River from White Bear Island to Black Eagle Dam, inventoried the entire shoreline and assessed the riverbanks for condition, rehabilitation and refurbishment, and improvement opportunities. The plan laid out a number of options above and below Black Eagle Dam for improvements to public access, recreation, and beautification.<ref>Wilmot, Paula. "Commissioners OK Missouri Corridor Plan." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' May 5, 2004; Lee, Sonja. "Neighbors Give Cold Shoulder to Ice Proposal." ''Great Falls Tribune.'' May 12, 2004.</ref>

Black Eagle Dam has changed very little between its construction in 1926 and 2010. The ruins of the powerhouses from the 1890 dam and the 1913 reconstruction were still visible as of 2005.<ref name="RiversEdge8">[http://www.greatfallsmt.net/people_offices/plancomdev/histprev/RiversEdgeHistory.pdf River's Edge Trail Foundation, p. 8.] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101217074717/http://greatfallsmt.net/people_offices/plancomdev/histprev/RiversEdgeHistory.pdf |date=2010-12-17 }}</ref> The large cast-iron sheets which formed the south bank penstocks can also still be seen.
