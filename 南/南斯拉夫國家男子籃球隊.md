'''南斯拉夫國家男子籃球隊'''（{{lang-sh|Košarkaška reprezentacija Jugoslavije / Кошаркашка репрезентација Југославије}}; {{lang-mk|Кошаркарска репрезентација на Југославија}}）是[[南斯拉夫王國|南斯拉夫王國]]和[[南斯拉夫社會主義聯邦共和國|南斯拉夫社會主義聯邦共和國]]的國家男子籃球隊。南斯拉夫曾經在奧運會上獲得過一次金牌，三次銀牌和一次銅牌。南斯拉夫還獲得過三次世界籃球錦標賽的金牌和五次歐洲籃球錦標賽的金牌，是世界籃球強國。

==Competitions==
At the [[Basketball_at_the_Summer_Olympics|Summer Olympic Games]], Yugoslavia captured one [[gold_medal|gold medal]] ([[1980_Summer_Olympics|1980]]), took the [[silver_medal|silver medal]] on three occasions ([[1968_Summer_Olympics|1968]], [[1976_Summer_Olympics|76]], [[1988_Summer_Olympics|88]]) and captured the [[bronze_medal|bronze medal]] once ([[1984_Summer_Olympics|1984]]).

At the [[FIBA_World_Cup|FIBA World Cup]], Yugoslavia captured three gold medals ([[1970_FIBA_World_Championship|1970]], [[1978_FIBA_World_Championship|1978]] and [[1990_FIBA_World_Championship|1990]]), three silver medals (1963, 1967, 1974) and two bronze medals (1982, 1986).

At the [[EuroBasket|EuroBasket]], Yugoslavia captured the gold medal five times (1973, 1975, 1977, 1989, 1991), were silver medalists on five occasions (1961, 1965, 1969, 1971, 1981), and captured the bronze medal four times (1963, 1979, 1987).

==European championships==

===EuroBasket 1947===
Yugoslavia made its European championship debut in [[EuroBasket_1947|EuroBasket 1947]], the fifth edition of the tournament.  The team placed 13th out of 14 teams in the competition, losing to the [[Soviet_Union_national_basketball_team|Soviet Union]] and [[Hungary_national_basketball_team|Hungary]] in the preliminary round, beating the [[Netherlands_national_basketball_team|Netherlands]] but losing to [[Italy_national_basketball_team|Italy]] in the semifinal round (placing third in the three-way tie between the teams), and defeating [[Albania_national_basketball_team|Albania]] in the 13th/14th classification match.

===EuroBasket 1953===
Yugoslavia's second appearance was at [[EuroBasket_1953|EuroBasket 1953]] in [[Moscow|Moscow]].  They dropped an early 27–25 decision against [[Bulgaria_national_basketball_team|Bulgaria]] but finished at 3–1 in their preliminary group.  In the three-way tie-breaker with Bulgaria and [[Israel_national_basketball_team|Israel]], Yugoslavia ended up in second place to advance to the final round.  There, they won 3 but lost 4 to take 6th place overall in the 17 team tournament.

===EuroBasket 1955===
[[File:Internationale_basketbalontmoeting_Weeknummer_55-14_-_Open_Beelden_-_56796.ogv|thumb]]
Yugoslavia again advanced to the final round at [[EuroBasket_1955|EuroBasket 1955]] in [[Budapest|Budapest]], this time in sole second place with a 3–1 record in the preliminary round pool.  Their final round performance was riddled with 6 losses in 7 games, but did include the high point of a 52–49 victory over eventual [[silver_medal|silver medal]]list [[Czechoslovakia_national_basketball_team|Czechoslovakia]] on Yugoslavia's way to an 8th place finish of the 18 entrants.

===EuroBasket 1957===
Yugoslavia's appearance at the [[EuroBasket_1957|EuroBasket 1957]] tournament in [[Sofia|Sofia]] resulted in a 2–1 record for the preliminary round and advancement to the final round robin.  There, they proved capable of two wins, defeating [[Poland_national_basketball_team|Poland]] and [[France_national_basketball_team|France]] to finish at 2–5 for 6th place in the tournament.

==The last years==
The Yugoslav national team of the late 1980s and early 1990s featured what was perhaps the greatest generation in the history of Yugoslav basketball. A common quip about basketball is: "The Americans invented it, the Yugoslavs perfected it." With such players as [[Dražen_Petrović|Dražen Petrović]], [[Vlade_Divac|Vlade Divac]], [[Toni_Kukoč|Toni Kukoč]], [[Dino_Rađa|Dino Rađa]], [[Predrag_Danilović|Predrag Danilović]], [[Žarko_Paspalj|Žarko Paspalj]] and [[Jure_Zdovc|Jure Zdovc]] has pioneered the wave of international [[National_Basketball_Association|NBA]] players well before the early 21st century, when the concept began to gain true global momentum.

Many of the former Yugoslav stars mentioned above were a part of the under-21 national team that won the FIBA World Junior Championships in 1987, defeating the U.S. both in pool play and in the final.
