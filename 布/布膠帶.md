'''布膠帶'''，或稱'''鴨子膠帶'''、'''導風管膠帶'''、'''大力膠帶'''、'''牛皮膠帶'''、'''防水布膠帶'''，是一種含有布層的感壓黏性膠帶，通常在外層包有[[聚乙烯|聚乙烯]]，使膠布具防水功效。一般綑成一卷，常用於大範圍場地作黏貼或定點標示作用。
==歷史==
「Duck tape」最早是指長條形的「Cotton duck」布條，用於製作鞋子與衣飾，也用來包裹鋼纜或電導體來防止鏽蝕或磨損。例如1902年支撐[[曼哈頓大橋|曼哈頓大橋]]的鋼纜先塗上亞麻籽油，再用「Duck tape」包覆，最後裝設定位。<ref name=OED1902>{{Cite web|url=http://eagle.brooklynpubliclibrary.org/Default/Scripting/ArchiveView.asp?skin=BEagle&AppName=2&GZ=T&BaseHref=BEG%2F1902%2F11%2F21&PageSize=3&enter=true&Page=15|title=''Brooklyn Daily Eagle''|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121017204729/http://eagle.brooklynpubliclibrary.org/Default/Scripting/ArchiveView.asp?skin=BEagle&AppName=2&GZ=T&BaseHref=BEG%2F1902%2F11%2F21&PageSize=3&enter=true&Page=15|archivedate=2012-10-17}} 21 Nov 1902 "Considering..that 100,000 yards of cotton duck tape must be wrapped around the cable with neatness and exactitude, it may be imagined that this method of cable preservation is quite expensive."</ref>。
以上都還未加上黏膠層，只是單純的棉布帶或麻布帶。

1943年，Vesta Stoudt寫給[[富蘭克林·D·羅斯福|富蘭克林·D·羅斯福]]一封信，提到她在工廠測試過一種織物膠帶，可以減少彈藥箱的封箱時間。<ref>[http://archives.chicagotribune.com/1943/10/24/page/1/article/couldnt-keep-her-idea-down "Couldn't Keep Her Idea Down"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150506050728/http://archives.chicagotribune.com/1943/10/24/page/1/article/couldnt-keep-her-idea-down/ |date=2015-05-06 }}, 24 October 1943, Chicago Tribune</ref>此信轉交到了[[戰時生產委員會|戰時生產委員會]]，委員會指派了[[強生公司|強生公司]]來開發這項產品，.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.kilmerhouse.com/2012/06/the-woman-who-invented-duct-tape/ |title=The Woman Who Invented Duct Tape |last1=Gurowitz |first1=Margaret |date=June 21, 2012 |website=KilmerHouse.com |publisher=Johnson & Johnson |accessdate=10 January 2014 |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140111053805/http://www.kilmerhouse.com/2012/06/the-woman-who-invented-duct-tape/ |archivedate=11 January 2014 |df= }}</ref>最後開發出了一項可以用手撕而不需剪刀裁斷的產品。這個未命名的產品由「Cotton duck」棉布、防水[[聚乙烯|聚乙烯]]、[[橡膠|橡膠]]基底的灰色黏著物所製成。<ref name=Freeman2003>{{cite news |last=Freeman |first=Jan |title=Tale of the tape |newspaper=Boston Globe |date=March 9, 2003 |url=http://listserv.linguistlist.org/pipermail/ads-l/2005-July/052229.html |accessdate=June 24, 2005 |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208072454/http://listserv.linguistlist.org/pipermail/ads-l/2005-July/052229.html |archivedate=December 8, 2015 |df= }}</ref><ref name=Jumbo>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=kwLnBY7mLcIC&pg=PA10 |page=10 |title=The Jumbo Duct Tape Book |first1=Jim |last1=Berg |first2=Tim |last2=Nyberg |publisher=Workman Publishing |year=2000 |isbn=0761121102 |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131212151021/http://books.google.com/books?id=kwLnBY7mLcIC&pg=PA10 |archivedate=2013-12-12 |df= }}</ref><ref name=Inventions>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=rVQfBSlAZvAC&pg=PA22 |pages=22–23 |first1=David John |last1=Cole |first2=Eve |last2=Browning |first3=Fred E. H. |last3=Schroeder |title=Encyclopedia of Modern Everyday Inventions |year=2003 |publisher=Greenwood Publishing |isbn=0313313458 |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131213155533/http://books.google.com/books?id=rVQfBSlAZvAC&pg=PA22 |archivedate=2013-12-13 |df= }}</ref><ref name=Manco>{{cite web |url=http://www.duckbrand.com/Duck%20Tape%20Club/history-of-duck-tape.aspx |title=History of Duck Tape |publisher=Duck Brand |accessdate=August 27, 2012 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120812084402/http://www.duckbrand.com/Duck%20Tape%20Club/history-of-duck-tape.aspx |archivedate=2012年8月12日 |df= }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Genericide: When a Brand Name Becomes Generic|url=http://www.cbc.ca/ageofpersuasion/episode/season-5/2011/05/06/season-five-genericide-when-a-brand-name-becomes-generic-1/|work=Age of Persuasion|publisher=CBC|accessdate=14 May 2011|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110514150320/http://www.cbc.ca/ageofpersuasion/episode/season-5/2011/05/06/season-five-genericide-when-a-brand-name-becomes-generic-1/|archivedate=14 May 2011|df=}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zFimLDoTkZQC&pg=PA18 |page=18 |last=Steele |first=Randy |title=Tale of the Tape |work=Boating |date=July 2003 |quote=In 1942 research scientists at Johnson & Johnson sandwiched a layer of mesh fabric—cotton duck—between a top layer of green polyethylene plastic and a bottom layer of rubber glue, and pressed them together. |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160424075143/https://books.google.com/books?id=zFimLDoTkZQC&pg=PA18 |archivedate=2016-04-24 |df= }}</ref>此膠帶很容易貼撕，很快地就用於修復載具與武器上。<ref name=Jumbo/>這種膠帶當時製成消光[[橄欖綠|橄欖綠]]色。

軍人們稱這種膠帶為「Duck tape」<ref name=Forbes>{{cite journal |url=https://www.forbes.com/2006/03/14/tools-duct-tape_cx_de_0315ducttape.html |title=The Other Greatest Tool Ever |last=Ewalt |first=David M. |work=Forbes |date=March 15, 2006 |accessdate=August 27, 2012 |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120911010636/http://www.forbes.com/2006/03/14/tools-duct-tape_cx_de_0315ducttape.html |archivedate=September 11, 2012 |df= }}</ref>，這個稱呼的來源有多種說法：包括是由「Cotton duck」發展來的；或因為其防水的特性就像鴨子羽毛一樣；甚至也有來自1942年的兩棲軍用載具[[DUKW|DUKW]]（發音為Duck）的說法。<ref>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zWzeKxSYaHIC&pg=PT31 |page=31 |last=Kruszelnicki |first=Karl |title=It Ain't Necessarily So... Bro |publisher=HarperCollins Australia |year=2010 |isbn=0730445267 |volume=8 |series=New Moments in Science |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131214095029/http://books.google.com/books?id=zWzeKxSYaHIC&pg=PT31 |archivedate=2013-12-14 |df= }}</ref>

二戰後，布膠帶在五金行販售，作為居家維修使用。1950年代，此膠帶製成銀灰色，常用來纏繞排風管，<ref name=Forbes/>因此又有了「Duct tape」的稱呼。

它的標準尺寸為<math>1{7 \over 8}</math>英寸，即約5厘米。常見的有黑色、銀灰色或啡色，但其他顏色的亦會生產。
