Fossil records and egg shell fragments show that the ancestors of this genus originated about 40-58 [[mya_(unit)|million years ago]] (mya) in the [[Asia|Asia]]tic [[steppe|steppe]]s as small flightless birds. The earliest fossils from this genus are from the early [[Miocene|Miocene]] (20-25mya), and are from Africa, so it is proposed that they originated there. Then by the middle to late [[Miocene|Miocene]] (5-13mya) they had spread to [[Eurasia|Eurasia]].<ref>Hou, L. et al. (2005)</ref> By about 12 mya they had evolved into the larger size of which we are familiar.  By this time they had spread to [[Mongolia|Mongolia]] and, later, [[South_Africa|South Africa]].<ref>Davies, S.J.J.F. (2003)</ref>
