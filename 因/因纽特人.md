'''因纽特人'''（因纽特语：{{lang|iu|ᐃᓄᐃᑦ}} ''{{transl|因纽特语|Inuit}}''）是[[美洲原住民|美洲原住民]]之一，分布於[[北極圈|北極圈]]周圍，包括[[格陵兰|格陵兰]]，和[[加拿大|加拿大]]的[[努纳武特地区|努纳武特地区]]、[[西北地區|西北地區]]、[[育空地區|育空地區]]、[[魁北克|魁北克]]等地，说[[因纽特语|因纽特语]]。因纽特人屬於[[爱斯基摩人|爱斯基摩人]]的一支（其餘為[[尤皮克人|尤皮克人]]，并与[[阿留申人|阿留申人]]有亲缘关系），不過他们并不自称为愛斯基摩人，因为这是北美其他[[印第安人|印第安人]]部落對他們的稱呼，意思是“吃生肉的人”，帶有貶義。因此他們自稱為因紐特人，於[[因纽特语|因纽特语]]中的意思為“人”，故外界也逐漸改口作此稱呼，以尊重其文化精神。[[格陵兰|格陵兰]]因纽特人也称为格陵兰人。

==简介==
[[File:Early_20th_Century_Inuit_parka_(UBC).jpg|thumb]]
因纽特人在公元前10000年左右越过白令海峡到达美洲。之后征服了先於他们到达美洲的[[图尼特人|图尼特人]]（两者属于同一民族）。约公元1300年（距今700年前），因纽特人从阿拉斯加向东迁徙，灭绝了[[古爱斯基摩人|古爱斯基摩人]]（[[:en:Paleo-Eskimo|Paleo-Eskimo]]，如[[多尔塞特文化|多尔塞特文化]]等），并在西格陵兰建立了定居点。十五至十六世纪，图尼特人因被南部的印第安部落压迫而北迁，完全灭绝。在[[维京人|维京人]]登陆格陵兰并向东探索之后，他们与欧洲人有了第一次接触。当时被称为skrælingjar（词源来自古挪威语与现在的[[冰岛语|冰岛语]]，意为穿皮草的人）。

[[File:Arctic_cultures_900-1500.png|有框]]不是因紐特人]]
加拿大北部居住着因纽特人，是[[爱斯基摩人|爱斯基摩人]]的一支，在[[格陵兰|格陵兰]]、[[阿拉斯加|阿拉斯加]]能看到他们。身材不高，宽鼻子，头发又黑又直，因纽特人居住得十分分散，在西伯利亚和特肖克彻斯（Tchouktchs）半岛北端，只有几千人。在加拿大，他们主要生活在[[努纳武特|努纳武特]]地区，人口约3万多人。世上共有6万多人。

法国传教士将“爱斯基摩人”拼写为{{lang|fr|''Esquimau''}}（阴性''Esquimaude''，复数为''Esquimaux''）。英语则是''Eskimo''，是我们常用的形式。他们和印第安人一样，只是晚一些从[[白令海峡|白令海峡]]来到美洲。因纽特人主要表现出[[蒙古人种|蒙古人种]]的种族特征，他们是最矮的黄种人。

他们在海岸边安家落户，主要猎捕海鱼（[[鲨|鲨]]，[[鳕鱼|鳕鱼]]，[[鳙鱼|鳙鱼]]，鳟鱼和[[红鲑鱼|红鲑鱼]]）、海生哺乳动物（主要是[[海象|海象]]，[[独角鲸|独角鲸]]和各类鲸）和陆地哺乳动物（[[鸭子|鸭子]]，加拿大[[驯鹿|驯鹿]]，[[白熊|白熊]]，[[麝牛|麝牛]]，极地狐和[[北极熊|北极熊]]），一些地方化的种族也捕捉淡水鱼。

捕猎的方法有很多种，虽然步枪取代了传统武器，但鱼叉还是一种有效的补充工具。因纽特人也从事渔业。捕鲸用的皮划艇后来发展为国际流行的体育运动。

捕鱼活动一般是在大浮冰上，更多时候在浮冰下进行，不同的种族用不同捕鱼工具，捕不同类型的鱼：钓鱼钩，渔网，捕鱼篓，鱼叉。因纽特人在北极地区短暂的夏天也从事采摘业，但他们的食谱中还是主要以肉食为主，这种生活环境中，他们主要依靠海豹和加拿大驯鹿生存。也是那些动物的皮毛为因纽特人提供了抵御严寒的衣服。

至于居住形式，传统的是雪砖垒成的圆屋顶的房屋-[[雪屋|雪屋]]。然而，雪屋这个词不仅仅指这种雪砖房屋，而是指各种居住形式，这要依季节而变化：夏天，因纽特人住在兽皮搭成的帐篷里；冬天则住在雪屋，石头屋或泥土块屋子里。

游牧生活也起源于迁移形式，后来发明了狗拉雪橇——美洲印第安人也用这种工具，和海豹皮小艇、独木舟。不同的海豹皮小艇通常是一人操纵的小船，用双短桨划动，加上窄窄的船体，使海豹皮小艇无论是在海上还是在冰上活动起来都十分灵活。在新魁北克犬牙交錯的海湾之中，海豹皮小艇最显神通，然而，在因纽特人和印第安人中，游牧生活方式已经續漸消失，雪地摩托代替了雪橇，营房也大多代替了雪屋。
  
后来因为气候变化，在十四世纪地球进入小冰期，本来居住在格陵兰岛西边的因纽特人与居住在东边的欧洲大陆殖民者发生冲突，没有武器（格陵兰岛有铁矿，但冶炼铁器需要的木材极其匮乏）和甲胄的欧洲大陆殖民者败于从西边来的因纽特人，同时也因为欧洲大陆的殖民者不愿接受适合新环境的生活方式，拒绝接纳鱼叉和小艇等因纽特人的渔猎技术，在殖民地遗迹中也未发现任何因纽特人文化的物品，作为欧洲生活的象征，殖民者们还饲养着世界上最矮的牛，与此同时，因纽特人所选择的道路尽管可能被后世认为是“文化的衰退”，但认清环境变化并加以灵活对待，最后幸存下来的是后者。

'''''Inuit''''' (ᐃᓄᐃᑦ、singular '''''Inuk''''' or '''''Inuq''''' / ᐃᓄᒃ) is a general term for a group of culturally similar [[indigenous_people|indigenous people]]s of the [[Arctic|Arctic]] who descended from the [[Thule_(people)|Thule]].

The [[Inuit_Circumpolar_Conference|Inuit Circumpolar Conference]] defines its constituency to include [[Canada|Canada]]'s Inuit and [[Inuvialuit|Inuvialuit]]、[[Greenland|Greenland]]'s [[Kalaallit|Kalaallit]] people、[[Alaska|Alaska]]'s [[Inupiaq|Inupiaq]] and [[Yupik|Yupik]] people、and [[Russia|Russia]]'s [[Yupik|Yupik]].  However、the Yupik are not Inuit in the sense of being descended from the Thule and prefer to be called ''Yupik'' or ''Eskimo''.

Canadian Inuit live primarily in [[Nunavut|Nunavut]]、[[Nunavik|Nunavik]] (a region in the northern part of the province of [[Quebec|Quebec]] defined by the [[James_Bay_Agreement|James Bay Agreement]]) and in [[Nunatsiavut|Nunatsiavut]] (a region in [[Labrador|Labrador]] whose borders are yet to be fixed.)  The [[Inuvialuit|Inuvialuit]] live primarily in the [[Mackenzie_River|Mackenzie River]] delta、on [[Banks_Island|Banks Island]] and part of [[Victoria_Island|Victoria Island]] in the [[Northwest_Territories|Northwest Territories]].  There have been Inuit settlements in [[Yukon|Yukon]]、especially at [[Herschel_Island|Herschel Island]]、but there are none at present.  Alaskan [[Inupiaq|Inupiaq]] live on the [[North_Slope|North Slope]] of Alaska、while the Yupik live in western Alaska and a part of [[Chukotka|Chukotka]] Autonomous Area in [[Russia|Russia]].

The [[Inuit_Tapiriit_Kanatami|Inuit Tapiriit Kanatami]] is a national organization in Canada which represents over 40,000 Canadian Inuit.

== Occupation ==
The Inuit are traditionally [[hunter|hunter]]s who fish and hunt [[whale|whale]]、[[walrus|walrus]]、and [[seal_(mammal)|seal]] by [[kayak|kayak]] or by boat or by waiting at airholes the seals make in the ice. They use [[igloo|igloo]]s as hunting or emergency shelters. They make use of animal skins in their clothing (e.g. [[anorak|anorak]]). [[Dog_sled|Dog sled]]s、known as ''qamutiit''、are used for travel pulled by Inuit Sled Dogs in a fan hitch、though [[snowmobile|snowmobile]]s have largely replaced this mode of travel.

== Eskimo ==
''See main article for more information on the term: [[Eskimo|Eskimo]]''

In [[Inuktitut|Inuktitut]]、the language of the Inuit people、"Inuit" means "the people".  The [[English_language|English]] word "Eskimo" comes from the French "Esquimaux" but the origins of this French word are unclear.  Many Inuit consider the word ''Eskimo'' offensive、but is still in general usage to refer to all Eskimo peoples、though it has fallen into disuse throughout Canada、where Canadians use the term ''Inuit''.  The men are traditionally hunters of seals、whales、walrus、and caribou、using harpoons、canoes (or kayaks)、dogs、and sleds. Fishing is also important. The women take care of the children、clean the house and cook.

== Classification ==
The Inuit living in [[North_America|North America]] were formerly classified together with other [[Native_American|Native American]]s、but they are now considered to be an entirely separate ethnic group who arrived in North America a few millennia after the latter did、probably around [[6th_century|500]] as the [[Thule_(people)|Thule]]、replacing the [[Dorset_(culture)|Dorset culture]]. Accordingly、in Canada the Inuit are not considered [[First_Nations|First Nations]].  However、they、the Indians、and the [[Métis_people_(Canada)|Métis]] are collectively recognized by the Canadian [[Constitution_Act、1982|Constitution Act、1982]] as [[Aboriginal_peoples_in_Canada|Aboriginal peoples in Canada]].  Other synonyms include "First Peoples" and "Native Peoples".

Inuit are members of the [[Mongoloid|Mongoloid]] race、which also includes various [[Siberia|Siberian]] tribes such as the [[Yakut|Yakut]]、as well as the [[Chinese|Chinese]] and [[Japanese|Japanese]].

== Losing the traditions ==
The European arrival caused a great deal of damage to the Inuit way of life、causing mass death and other suffering. Circa [[1970|1970]]、Inuit leaders came forward and pushed for respect for the Inuit and their territories. One of the resulting land-claims agreements created the [[Canada|Canadian]] [[Territories_of_Canada|territory]] of [[Nunavut|Nunavut]]、the largest land-claims agreement in Canadian history. In recent years、[[circumpolar|circumpolar]] cultural and political groups have come together to promote the Inuit people and to fight against [[ecology|ecological]] problems、such as the [[greenhouse_effect|greenhouse effect]] and resulting [[global_warming|global warming]]、which heavily affects the Inuit population due to the melting and thinning of the arctic ice and declining arctic mammal populations. Nunavut premier [[Paul_Okalik|Paul Okalik]] took the lead in this regard in a First Ministers' meeting discussing the [[Kyoto_Accord|Kyoto Accord]].

== Culture ==
One of the most famous Inuit artists is [[Pitseolak_Ashoona|Pitseolak Ashoona]]. [[Susan_Aglukark|Susan Aglukark]] is a popular Canadian singer. In 2002 the feature film ''[[Atanarjuat:_the_Fast_Runner|Atanarjuat: the Fast Runner]]'' directed by [[Zacharias_Kunuk|Zacharias Kunuk]] (with all dialogue in the Inuktitut language and written、filmed、produced、directed、and acted almost entirely by Inuit of Igloolik) was released world wide to great critical and popular acclaim. [[Jordin_Tootoo|Jordin Tootoo]] became the first Inuk to play in the [[National_Hockey_League|National Hockey League]] in the 2003-04 season、playing for the [[Nashville_Predators|Nashville Predators]]. Well-known Inuit politicians include Premier [[Paul_Okalik|Paul Okalik]] of Nunavut and [[Nancy_Karetak-Lindell|Nancy Karetak-Lindell]]、MP for the [[Nunavut_(electoral_district)|riding of Nunavut]].
