'''歐洲U-21足球錦標賽'''（'''UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship'''）自1978年起舉辦，为兩年一屆。

這個比賽在奧運會前一年的比賽象徵[[夏季奧林匹克運動會|夏季奧林匹克運動會]]的男子項目歐洲區資格賽。
<!--
The '''European Under-21 Football Championship''' is a [[football_(soccer)|football]] competition organised by the sport's [[Europe|Europe]]an governing body, [[UEFA|UEFA]]. It is held every two years. The competition has existed in its current form since [[1978|1978]]. It was preceded by the Under 23 Challenge Cup which ran from [[1967|1967]] to [[1970|1970]]. A true championship was formed starting in [[1972|1972]]. 

The age limit was reduced to 21 for the [[UEFA_U-21_Championship_1978|1978 championship]] and it has remained so since.  To be eligible for the campaign ending in [[2006|2006]], players needed to be born in or after [[1983|1983]].  Many were actually 23 years old at the finals tournament, but when the qualification process began (late [[2004|2004]]) all players would have been 21 or under.

There is no Under-21 [[Football_World_Cup|World Cup]]; instead, [[FIFA|FIFA]] hold the [[FIFA_U-20_World_Cup|Under-20 World Cup]] - formerly the FIFA World Youth Championship every two years. The European Under-21 teams have two European Championships instead of one European Championship and a World Cup.  Under-21 matches are typically played on the day before senior internationals and where possible, the same qualifying groups and fixtures were played out. This will change for the 2006-7 Championship.

== Competition structure ==
Up to and including the [[UEFA_U-21_Championship_1992|1992 competition]], all entrants were divided into eight qualification groups, the eight winners of which formed the quarter-finals lineup.  The remaining fixtures were played out on a two-legged, home and away basis to determine the eventual winner.

For the [[UEFA_U-21_Championship_1994|1994 competition]], one of the semi-finalists, France, was chosen as a host for the (single-legged) semi-finals, 3rd place playoff and final.  Similarly, [[Spain|Spain]] was chosen to host the last four matches in [[UEFA_U-21_Championship_1996|1996]].

For [[UEFA_U-21_Championship_1998|1998]], nine qualification groups were used, as participation had reached 46, nearly double the 24 entrants in [[UEFA_U-21_Championship_1976|1976]].  The top seven group winners qualified automatically for the finals, whilst the eighth- and ninth-best qualifiers, {{nftu21|Greece}} and {{nftu21|England}}, played-off for the final spot. The remaining matches, from the quarter-finals onward, were held in [[Romania|Romania]], one of the eight qualifiers.

The [[UEFA_U-21_Championship_2000|2000 competition]] also had nine groups, but the 9 winners and 7 runners-up went into a two-legged playoff to decide the eight qualifiers.  From those, [[Slovakia|Slovakia]] was chosen as host.  For the first time, the familiar finals group stage was employed, with the two winners contesting a final, and two runners-up contesting the 3rd-place playoff.  The structure in [[UEFA_U-21_Championship_2002|2002]] was identical, except for the introduction of a semi-finals round after the finals group stage.  [[Switzerland|Switzerland]] hosted the 2002 finals.

In [[UEFA_U-21_Championship_2004|2004]], ten qualification groups were used, with the six best runners-up going into the playoff. [[Germany|Germany]] was host that year. For [[UEFA_U-21_Championship_2006|2006]], the top two teams of eight large qualification groups provided the 16 teams for the playoffs, held in November 2005.  [[Portugal|Portugal]] hosted the finals.

Then followed the switch to odd years.  The change was made because the senior teams of many nations often chose to promote players from their under-21s team as their own qualification campaign intensifies.  Staggering the tournaments will allow players more time to develop in the under-21 team rather than get promoted too early and end up becoming reserves for the seniors.  

The [[UEFA_U-21_Championship_2007|2007 competition]] actually began before the 2006 finals, with a qualification round to eliminate eight of the lowest-ranked nations.  For the first time a host (Netherlands) had been chosen ahead of the qualification section. As hosts, {{nftu21|Netherlands}} qualify automatically.  Coincidentally, the Dutch team won the 2006 competition - the holders would normally have to go through the qualification stage.  The other nations all go into 14 three-team groups and will play one match at home and one away.  The winners of the 14 groups then play-off to decide the seven qualifiers alongside the hosts.

=== 會出現奧運附加賽的成因 ===
* 因為英格蘭、蘇格蘭、威爾斯、北愛爾蘭、法羅群島、直布羅陀等不是國際奧委會會員國。
** 2011年時英格蘭以主辦國英國名義參賽，當時名額為3，且未能取得奧運晉級資格，因此須舉行季軍賽來作為奧運附加賽。
** 2015年時英格蘭無法以英國名義參賽，當時名額為4，且未能取得前4名，因此不須舉行附加賽。當時分組第三可能要舉行附加賽。
** 2019年時，這種情況如果再度發生，將以2隊較差小組第二舉行奧運附加賽。
