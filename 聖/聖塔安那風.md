'''圣塔安娜风'''是一种典型的[[南加州|南加州]]季节性强风。圣塔安娜风是强烈且非常干燥的下坡风，起源于内陆，影响到沿海的[[南加利福尼亞州|南加利福尼亞州]]和[[下加利福尼亞州|北下加利福尼亚半岛]]。該風經常引起在[[加州|加州]]的[[森林大火|森林大火]]。<ref name="KCET Devil Winds">{{cite web|url=http://www.kcet.org/updaily/socal_focus/history/la-as-subject/santa-ana-winds-history.html|title=SoCal's Devil Winds: The Santa Anas in Historical Photos and Literature|last=Masters|first=Nathan|date=October 25, 2012|website=www.kcet.org|publisher=[[KCET|KCET]]|accessdate=May 3, 2012|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121029044650/http://www.kcet.org/updaily/socal_focus/history/la-as-subject/santa-ana-winds-history.html|archivedate=October 29, 2012|df=}} "(White)Scholars who have looked into the name's origins generally agree that it derives from Santa Ana Canyon, the portal where the Santa Ana River -- as well as a congested Riverside (CA-91) Freeway -- leaves Riverside County and enters Orange County. When the Santa Anas blow, winds can reach exceptional speeds in this narrow gap between the Puente Hills and Santa Ana Mountains."</ref><ref name="The Devil Winds Made Me Do It">{{cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1988-03-12/news/li-942_1_santa-ana-winds|title=The Devil Winds Made Me Do It : Santa Anas Are Enough to Make Anyone's Hair Stand on End|last=Needham|first=John|date=March 12, 1988|website=www.latimes.com|publisher=[[洛杉磯時報|Los Angeles Times]]|accessdate=May 3, 2013|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140429213213/http://articles.latimes.com/1988-03-12/news/li-942_1_santa-ana-winds|archivedate=April 29, 2014|df=}}</ref>
