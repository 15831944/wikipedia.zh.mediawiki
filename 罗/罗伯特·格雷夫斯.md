'''罗伯特·冯兰克·格雷夫斯'''（{{lang-en|'''Robert von Ranke Graves'''}}，{{bd|1895年|7月24日|1985年|12月7日|catIdx=Graves}}<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.npg.org.uk/collections/search/person.php?LinkID=mp01882 |title=National Portrait Gallery - Person - Robert Ranke Graves |publisher=Npg.org.uk |date= |accessdate=2010-12-19}}</ref>），英国诗人，学者，小说家暨翻译家。专门从事古希腊和罗马作品的研究。在他漫长的一生中，他创作了140余部作品。他的回忆录《向一切告别》（1929年）讲述了他在[[第一次世界大战|第一次世界大战]]中的经历。他被誉为最优秀的英语爱情诗诗人。

他以写作为生,他的《耶稣王》（King Jesus）、关于[[古罗马皇帝|古罗马皇帝]][[克劳狄一世|克劳狄]]的两部曲《[[我，克劳迪亚斯|我，克劳迪亚斯]]》（I， Claudius）与《被神化的克劳狄和他的妻子梅萨利纳》（Claudius the God and His Wife Messalina）、以及[[拜占庭帝国|拜占庭帝国]]伯爵的生平故事《[[贝利萨里乌斯伯爵|贝利萨里乌斯伯爵]]》（Count Belisarius）是特别受欢迎的历史小说。他也是一个杰出的古典拉丁语和古希腊文的翻译家，1934年格雷夫斯因小说《我，克劳迪斯》被授予[[詹姆斯·泰特·布莱克纪念奖|詹姆斯·泰特·布莱克纪念奖]]。<ref>James Tait Black Prize winners: [http://www.ed.ac.uk/about/people/tait-black/fiction Previous winners – fiction] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110927024752/http://www.ed.ac.uk/about/people/tait-black/fiction |date=2011-09-27 }}</ref>

==代表诗作==
'''Cherry-Time''' <ref>{{cite web |author1=Poemhunter |title=Cherry-Time |url=https://www.poemhunter.com/poem/cherry-time-2/}}</ref><br>

Cherries of the night are riper<br>
Than the cherries pluckt at noon<br> 
Gather to your fairy piper <br>
When he pipes his magic tune: <br>
Merry, merry,<br>
Take a cherry; <br>
Mine are sounder, <br>
Mine are rounder, <br>
Mine are sweeter，<br>
For the eater <br>
Under the moon. <br>
And you’ll be fairies soon. <br><br>
In the cherry pluckt at night, <br>
With the dew of summer swelling, <br>
There’s a juice of pure delight, <br>
Cool, dark, sweet, divinely smelling. <br>
Merry, merry, <br>
Take a cherry; <br>
Mine are sounder, <br>
Mine are rounder, <br>
Mine are sweeter，<br>
For the eater <br>
In the moonlight. <br>
And you’ll be fairies quite. <br><br>
When I sound the fairy call, <br>
Gather here in silent meeting, <br>
Chin to knee on the orchard wall, <br>
Cooled with dew and cherries eating. <br>
Merry, merry, <br>
Take a cherry; <br>
Mine are sounder, <br>
Mine are rounder, <br>
Mine are sweeter. <br>
For the eater <br>
When the dews fall.<br>
And you’ll be fairies all. <br><br> 

'''樱桃时节''' <ref>{{cite web |author1=原灵 |title=美国文心社 |url=http://wxs.hi2net.com/home/blog_read.asp?id=5168&blogid=97326}}</ref><br>
夜晚的樱桃比午间采收的那些熟得更透，<br>
到你们的笛仙这儿来相聚吧。<br>
当他吹奏起他那魔幻般的曲调时：<br>
乐陶陶，乐陶陶，<br>
摘一枚樱桃；<br>
我的更赞，<br>
我的更圆，<br>
我的更甜，<br>
明月之下，静待佳客。<br>
若此，你们即将成仙。<br><br>
伴随夏日露水的逐渐饱满，<br>
那晚间采收的樱桃里，<br>
饱含着至纯喜悦的玉露琼浆，<br>
凉爽，幽深，甘美，气息圣洁。<br>
乐陶陶，乐陶陶，<br>
摘一枚樱桃；<br>
我的更赞，<br>
我的更圆，<br>
我的更甜，<br>
月华之中，静待佳客。<br>
若此，你们几近成仙。<br><br>
每当我听到仙子的召唤，<br>
就悄没声儿地来此聚会，<br>
坐在果园的墙上，支颐于膝，<br>
露水和樱桃美餐令我清凉。<br>
乐陶陶，乐陶陶，<br>
摘一枚樱桃；<br>
我的更赞，<br>
我的更圆，<br>
我的更甜，<br>
露降之时，静待佳客。<br>
若此，你们已然成仙。<br>
（译者：原灵）<br>
（Translated by Yuan-Ling）<br><br>

'''Full Moon''' <ref>{{cite web |author1=Poemhunter |title=Full moon |url=https://www.poemhunter.com/poem/full-moon-25/}}</ref><br>
As I walked out one harvest night <br>
About the stroke of One,<br>
The Moon attained to her full height<br>
Stood beaming like the Sun.<br>
She exorcised the ghostly wheat<br>
To mute assent in Love's defeat<br>
Whose tryst had now begun.<br><br>
The fields lay sick beneath my tread,<br>
A tedious owlet cried;<br>
The nightingale above my head <br>
With this or that replied,<br>
Like man and wife who nightly keep <br>
Inconsequent debate in sleep <br>
As they dream side by side.<br><br>
Your phantom wore the moon's cold mask,<br>
My phantom wore the same,<br>
Forgetful of the feverish task <br>
In hope of which they came, <br>
Each image held the other's eyes <br>
And watched a grey distraction rise <br>
To cloud the eager flame.<br><br>
To cloud the eager flame of love,<br>
To fog the shining gate:<br>
They held the tyrannous queen above <br>
Sole mover of their fate, <br>
They glared as marble statues glare <br>
Across the tessellated stair <br>
Or down the Halls of State. <br><br>
And now cold earth was Arctic sea, <br>
Each breath came dagger keen, <br>
Two bergs of glinting ice were we, <br>
The broad moon sailed between;<br>
There swam the mermaids, tailed and finned,<br>
And Love went by upon the wind <br>
As though it had not been. <br><br>

'''满月''' <ref>{{cite web |author1=原灵 |title=美国文心社 |url=http://wxs.hi2net.com/home/blog_read.asp?id=5168&blogid=97326}}</ref><br>
一个收获之夜，<br>
钟敲一点时，我步出门外，<br>
月伫立中天，辉照如日。 <br>
她祛除了麦子们的鬼魂，<br>
以默许那幽会伊始的爱的挫败。<br><br>
在我的踩踏下，田野萎靡不振，<br>
讨厌的猫头鹰叫个不停，<br>
而夜莺在我头顶之上，<br>
有一搭没一搭地回应着，<br>
好像并卧睡梦里仍在拌嘴的夫妻 <br><br>
你的幽灵戴着月的冷面具<br>
我的也同样如此， <br>
忘掉那狂热的使命吧，<br>
在盼望它们哪个来临之时。<br>
我们的映像令彼此目不交睫,<br>
眼看那扰人心神的灰色之物冉冉升起，<br>
让渴望的火焰蒙上阴影。<br><br>
为云翳那爱的欲望之火，<br>
为雾障那耀眼的门，<br> 
他们尊崇那暴君似的女王，<br>
他们命运的唯一主导者，<br>
他们像大理石雕像那样怒目而视，<br>
目光透过格饰的楼梯，或穿越国议厅。<br><br>
而此时，地球酷寒如北冰洋，<br>
每次的呼吸都锋利如匕首，<br>
我们是两座闪闪发光的冰山呵，<br>
满月航行其间；那里，<br>
麟尾俱全的美人鱼优游自在，<br>
一阵风之后，爱消失不见了，<br>
好像它不曾来过。<br>
（译者：原灵）<br>
（Translated by Yuan-Ling）<br><br>
