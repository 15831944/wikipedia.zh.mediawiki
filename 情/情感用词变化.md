[[修辞学|修辞学]]中的'''情感用词变化'''（{{lang-en|Emotive conjugation}}），是一种不规则动词的[[变位|变位]]，目的是粉饰人类的癖好，把自己同样的行为描述得比别人更好。<ref name="Johnson & Blair, 2006"/>1948年哲学家[[伯特兰·罗素|伯特兰·罗素]]首次在BBC广播节目[[The_Brains_Trust|The Brains Trust]]中提出了此概念<ref name="Audi, 1999"/>，因此也称作'''罗素用词变化'''。罗素在节目中举了这些例子<ref name="Walton, 2006"/>：

<blockquote>I am firm, You are obstinate, He is a pig-headed fool.<br/>
I am righteously indignant, you are annoyed, he is making a fuss over nothing.<br/>
I have reconsidered the matter, you have changed your mind, he has gone back on his word.</blockquote>

可以像[[扣帽子|扣帽子]]的情感用词变化<ref name="Walton, 2006"/>，通过含糊一个争论的[[謬論|謬論]]错误支持某观点。这种用词结构也是一种[[幽默|幽默]]<ref name="Chapman & Foot, 1996"/>，[[英國廣播公司電視|英國廣播公司電視]]情景喜剧《[[是，首相|是，首相]]》中的[[伯纳德·伍利|伯纳德·伍利]]这样说：<ref name="Yes Minister"/><ref name="Yes Prime Minister"/>
<blockquote>It's one of those irregular verbs, isn't it?<br/>
I have an independent mind, You are eccentric, He is round the twist.<ref name="Yes Prime Minister"/></blockquote>

<blockquote>That's another of those irregular verbs, isn't it?<br/>
I give confidential press briefings; you leak; he's being charged under section 2A of the Official Secrets Act.<ref name="Yes Prime Minister S2E1">Yes, Prime Minister: Man Overboard</ref></blockquote>
