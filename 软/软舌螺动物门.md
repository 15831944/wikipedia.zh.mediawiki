'''软舌螺动物'''（[[学名|学名]]：{{lang|la|'''Hyolitha'''}}）是一種從[[古生代|古生代]]開始出現的神秘[[動物|動物]]，具有小圓錐形的[[螺殼|螺殼]]。這些物種目前都已全部[[滅絕|滅絕]]；其[[化石|化石]]一般只能保存锥壳、[[#口蓋|口盖]]和[[附肢|附肢]]三个部分，外壳为[[钙质|钙质]]成分，两侧对称。根據形態學的分類，本物種是一类海生有壳的[[无脊椎动物|无脊椎动物]]，但再具體一點的分类众说纷纭：
*一种意见认为它是[[软体动物门|软体动物门]]之下的一个纲，
*另一种意见则认为它与软件动无本质上的联系，应属一个独立的[[門_(生物)|门]]。
近期以軟組織化石分析發現軟舌螺動物具有[[Lophophore|觸手冠]] (Lophophore) 這種進食器官，指出軟舌螺動物其實應該獨立為一門，而且和包含[[腕足動物門|腕足動物門]]在內的[[觸手冠動物|觸手冠動物]] (Lophophorata) 較相近<ref>{{Cite journal|title=Hyoliths are Palaeozoic lophophorates|author=Joseph Moysiuk|url=http://www.nature.com/nature/journal/vaop/ncurrent/full/nature20804.html|journal=Nature|issue=|doi=10.1038/nature20804|others=Martin R. Smith, Jean-Bernard Caron|year=2017|volume=|page=|pmid=}}</ref>。[[File:Hyoliths02.JPG|thumb]]的软舌螺动物门内部铸件，[[爱沙尼亚|爱沙尼亚]]北部]]

==形態學==
软舌螺动物的[[碳酸鈣|碳酸鈣]]質外殼很大可能是由[[霰石|霰石]]組成<ref name="Mus2007" />。
有{{link-en|口盖|operculum}}保護，還有兩條又名[[#附肢|海倫體]]的彎彎[[附肢|附肢]]（{{lang|la|''helens''}}）去支撐身體，以{{link-en|邊緣增生|marginal accretion}}的形式成長<ref name="Mus2007" />。
體長一般在一公分到四公分，橫切面呈三角形或橢圓形。部份物種有環紋或條紋。

===外殼===
原状壳具有带横向束微结构的内层和包含纵向束的外层。<ref name="Mus2007" />

===附肢===
本物種的附肢叫作「海倫體」，
Helens are long structures that taper as they logarithmically coil gently in a ventral direction.<ref name="Mus2007" />

The helens were calcareous, with an organic component, and had an organic-rich central core surrounded by concentric laminae of calcite.  They grew by the addition of new material at their base, on the cavity side, leaving growth lines.<ref name="Mus2007" />
They were originally described by Walcott as separate fossils under the genus name ''Helenia'', (Walcott's wife was named Helena and his daughter Helen); [[Bruce_Runnegar|Bruce Runnegar]] adopted the name helen when they were recognized as part of the hyolith organism.<ref name="Mus2007" />

===口蓋===
{{link-en|口蓋|operculum}}緊緊的把殼關閉。
The operculum closes perfectly over the aperture of the shell, leaving two gaps through which the helens can protrude.<ref name="Mus2007" />  It comprises two parts: the cardinal shield, a flat region at the top of the shell; and the conical shield, the bottom part, which is more conical.<ref name="Mus2007" />  The inside of the shell bears a number of protrusions, notably the dorsal cardinal processes and the radially-arranged clavicles.<ref name="Mus2007" />

==分类==
{{See also|List of bilaterial animal orders}}
[[Image:Hyoliths02.JPG|thumb]] of northern [[Estonia|Estonia]]; these are internal molds.]]
人们依据有无口唇将软舌螺动物门（纲）动物分成两个纲（目）：
*[[直管螺纲|直管螺纲]]（Orthothecida）
*[[有唇软舌螺纲|有唇软舌螺纲]]（Hyolithida）

Hyolitha have dorso-ventrally differentiated opercula, with the ventral surface of the shell extending forwards to form a shelf termed the ligula.<ref name="Mus2007" />

The Orthothecida are somewhat more problematic, and probably contain a number of non-hyoliths simply because they are so difficult to identify with confidence, especially if their operculum is absent.<ref name="Mus2007" />  They have a straight (planar) opening, sometimes with a notch on the bottom side, and sealed with an operculum that has no ligula, clavicles, furrow or rooflets.<ref name=Malinky2004/>

''[[Hyptiotheca|Hyptiotheca]]'' is an unusual hyolithid, in that it lacks clavicles.<ref name="Malinky2004">{{Cite journal | author1=Malinky, J.M. | author2=Skovsted, C.B | title = Hyoliths and small shelly fossils from the Lower Cambrian of North−East Greenland | journal =  Acta Palaeontologica Polonica |volume=49 |issue=4 |pages=551–578 | year=2004|url=http://app.pan.pl/article/item/app49-551.html}}</ref>

Orthothecids fall into two groups: one, the orcothecida ''sensu stricto'',<ref name="ref_a">{{Cite journal| last1 = Malinky | first1 = J. M.| title = First Occurrence of ''Orthotheca'' Novák, 1886 (Hyolitha, Early Devonian) in North America| journal = Journal of Paleontology| volume = 83| issue = 4| pages = 588–596| year = 2009| doi = 10.1666/08-164R.1}}</ref> is kidney or heart shaped in cross-section due to a longitudinal groove on its ventral surface, and its opercula bear cardinal processes; the other has a rounded cross-section and often lacks cardinal processes, making them difficult to distinguish from other cornet-shaped calcareous organisms.<ref name=Malinky2004/>  All were sessile and benthic; some may have been filter feeders.<ref name="ref_a" />

== Phylogenetic position  ==
[[File:Haplophrentis_Burgess_Shale.jpg|thumb]] (Middle [[Cambrian|Cambrian]]), Burgess Pass, British Columbia, Canada.]]
Because hyoliths are extinct and do not obviously resemble any [[Extant_taxon|extant]] group, it is unclear which living group they are most closely related to.  They may be molluscs; authors who suggest that they deserve their own phylum do not comment on the position of this phylum in the tree of life.<ref name=Sumrall2009>{{Cite journal|pages=147–152|doi=10.1666/08-094R.1|jstor=29739075|title=Permian Hyolithida from Australia: The Last of the Hyoliths?|journal=Journal of Paleontology|volume=83|year=2009|last1=Malinky|first1=John M.}}</ref>  Their grade of organization is considered to be of the 'mollusc-annelid-sipunculid' level,<ref name="Runnegar1980">{{Cite journal | last1 = Runnegar | first1 = B. | doi = 10.1111/j.1502-3931.1980.tb01025.x | title = Hyolitha: Status of the phylum | journal = Lethaia | volume = 13 | pages = 21 | date = January 1980 | pmid =  | pmc = }}</ref> consistent with a Lophotrochozoan affinity, and comparison is usually drawn with the molluscs or [[sipunculid|sipunculid]]s.<ref name="Runnegar1980" /><ref name="Kouchinsky2000">{{Cite journal | last1 = Kouchinsky | first1 = A. V. | title = Skeletal microstructures of hyoliths from the Early Cambrian of Siberia | doi = 10.1080/03115510008619525 | journal = Alcheringa: an Australasian Journal of Palaeontology | volume = 24 | issue = 2 | pages = 65–81 | year = 2000 | pmid =  | pmc = }}</ref>  Older studies (predating the Lophotrochozoan concept) consider hyoliths to represent a stem lineage of the clade containing (Mollusca + Annelida + Arthropoda).<ref name="Runnegar1975" />

==生态==
软舌螺可能是[[底栖生物|底栖生物]]。
寒武纪软舌螺是全球分布，没有迹象显示有地区差异，有迹象显示软舌螺有长寿命浮游幼虫生活期；但在奥陶系不同的组合变得越来越普遍。<ref name=Malinky2004/>

==出现==
最早的软舌螺壳体化石大约出现在5亿4000万年前的[[西伯利亚|西伯利亚]] Purella antiqua Zone 的[[Nemakit-Daldynian|Nemakit-Daldynian]]阶，其类似物发现于[[中国|中国]]''Paragloborilus subglobosus–Purella squamulosa'' Zone 的 [[梅树村阶|梅树村阶]]（[[寒武纪|寒武纪]]第一个阶）。<ref name="钱逸1999" />。软舌螺动物门物種無論在其數量及其多樣性都在[[寒武紀|寒武紀]]達至巔峰，之後逐步遞減，直到滅絕。<ref name="ref_">{{Cite journal | last1 = Malinky | first1 = J.
