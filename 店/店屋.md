'''店屋'''（{{lang-en|'''Shophouse'''}}），香港稱為'''下舖上居'''，是常見於[[華南地區|華南地區]]及[[東南亞|東南亞]]的{{link-en|鄉土建築|Vernacular architecture}}，多為兩至三層高，一樓作[[店舖|店舖]]使用，二三樓為民居<ref name="Zhu">Zhu, Jieming, Sim, Loo-Lee, Liu, Xuan, D., [http://escholarship.org/uc/item/46q7c6jv.pdf Place-Remaking under Property Rights Regimes: A Case Study of Niucheshui, Singapore], IURD Working Paper Series, Institute of Urban and Regional Development, UC Berkeley, 2006,  p.13. Accessed 2012-3-30.</ref><ref name="chua">Chua, Beng Huat, and Edwards, Norman, [http://books.google.com/books?hl=en&lr=%20&id=niNNjUnwploC&oi=fnd&pg=PA40&dq=shophouse+architecture&ots=cj2OnLN6-4&sig=uEvO3hKQ9j5AIaZFbaJhToQfD8E#v=onepage&q=shophouse&f=false Public space: design, use, and management], National University of Singapore, Centre for Advanced Studies, Singapore University Press, 1992, p. 4-5. ISBN 9971-69-164-7</ref>。店舖前設有[[騎樓|騎樓]]，此設計有利通風及避雨<ref name="Rafflesia">Lim, Jon S.H., "The Shophouse Rafflesia: An Outline of its Malaysian Pedigree and its Subsequent Diffusion in Asia", Journal of the Royal Asiatic Society, Volume LXVI Part 1, 1993, pp 47-66.  ISSN 0126-7353</ref>。

東南亞不少店屋，礙於稅務及結構規限，都為窄長設計，當地的騎樓因為法例規定為{{convert|5|ft}}闊，名為[[五腳基|五腳基]]。
