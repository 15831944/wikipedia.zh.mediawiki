在[[古羅馬宗教|古羅馬宗教]]和[[罗马神话|罗马神话]]中，'''赫丘利'''是一位英雄的神化，並納入[[罗马的建立|羅馬的建國]]神話裡面，之後羅馬人將[[希腊神话|希腊神话]]中的[[海克力斯|海克力斯]]形象融入赫丘利，改編成自己的文學和藝術，但英雄相對具有羅馬文化的特性。早在公元前6世紀和5世紀，一些希臘的資料源頭表示一些海克利斯的故事如{{tsl|en|Labours of Hercules||海克利斯的十二項功績}}和羅馬是有所關聯的。<ref>{{tsl|en|T.P. Wiseman}}, ''Remus: A Roman Myth'' (Cambridge University Press, 1995), pp. 39, 41.</ref>

[[哈利卡納蘇斯的狄奧尼修斯|哈利卡納蘇斯的狄奧尼修斯]]將赫丘利歸為羅馬榮譽的神之中：「他的靈魂死後必然離開身體，他已經升天並已獲得等價的榮譽之神。」<ref>[[哈利卡納蘇斯的狄奧尼修斯|哈利卡納蘇斯的狄奧尼修斯]] 7.72.13-14, In describing the images of deities displayed in the ceremonial procession at Rome known as the ''{{tsl|en|pompa circensis}}'', as cited by {{tsl|en|Jörg Rüpke}}, ''Religion in Republican Rome: Rationalization and Ritual Change'' (University of Pennsylvania Press, 2012, p. 41.</ref>他的[[神化|神化]]概念是由於其英雄形象而做為一個神化羅馬國王的象徵。<ref>Peter Herz, "Emperors: Caring for the Empire and Their Successors," in  ''A Companion to Roman Religion'' (Blackwell, 2007), p. 315.</ref>{{-}}

==故事==


暴君皇帝[[柯摩达|柯摩达]]將赫丘利作為自身的護衛，時常穿者赫丘利形象的衣服。在公元184年[[柯摩达|柯摩达]]將一年的月份全部依自己的名子重新命名，其中包括「赫丘利月」此用分可能是古羅馬的{{tsl|en|September_(Roman_month)||九月}}或{{tsl|en|September (Roman month)||十月}}<ref>[[卡西乌斯·狄奥|卡西乌斯·狄奥]] 72.15.3 has ''Herculeus'' as October: M.P. Speidel, "Commodus the God-Emperor and the Army," ''Journal of Roman Studies'' 83 (1993), p. 112; Robert Hannah, "The Emperor's Stars: The Conservatori Portrait of Commodus," ''American Journal of Archaeology'' 90.3 (1986), pp. 341–342. The ''[[罗马帝王纪|罗马帝王纪]]'' is internally inconsistent, at one point identifying September as the month of Hercules, and at another October: ''Scriptores Historiae Augustae'' VII, as cited by A.W. van Buren,  "Graffiti at Ostia," ''Classical Review'' 37 (1923), p. 163 (''Commodus'' 11.8 in the citation of Hannah, "The Emperor's Stars", p. 341, note 23, and ''Historia Augusta'', "Commodus" 11.13).</ref>這個新制度在192年的[[柯摩达|柯摩达]]謀殺案之後也一同廢除了。<ref>John R. Clarke, "The Decor of the House of Jupiter and Ganymede at Ostia Antica," in ''Roman Art in the Private Sphere: New Perspectives on the Architecture and Decor of the Domus, Villa, and Insula'' (University of Michigan Press, 1991, 1994), p. 92; John R. Clarke, ''The Houses of Roman Italy, 100 B.C.–A.D. 250: Ritual, Space, and Decoration'' (University of California Press, 1991), p. 322.</ref>
