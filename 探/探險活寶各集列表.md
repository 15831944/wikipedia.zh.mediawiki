The show originally began in 2007 with a [[Adventure_Time_(pilot)|pilot]],<ref name=anotherera>{{cite web|last=Seibert|first=Fred|title=From Another Era, it Seems Like|url=http://pwcartoons.frederator.com/post/33238852809/from-another-era-it-seems-like-thanks-cartoon|publisher=[[Frederator|Frederator]]|accessdate=March 20, 2013|archivedate=October 9, 2012|archiveurl=http://conepines.tumblr.com/post/33239506926/adventuretime-from-another-era-it-seems-like|date=October 9, 2012}}</ref> which was later re-aired on the incubator series ''[[Random!_Cartoons|Random! Cartoons]]'' on [[Nicktoons_Network|Nicktoons Network]].<ref name=timeenter>{{cite web|last=Feeney|first=Nolan|title=The Weird World of Adventure Time Comes Full Circle|url=http://entertainment.time.com/2013/03/29/the-weird-world-of-adventure-time-comes-full-circle/|work=[[Time_(magazine)|Time]]|publisher=[[Time_Inc.|Time, Inc]]|accessdate=March 29, 2013|date=March 29, 2013}}</ref> The pilot eventually leaked onto the internet and became a cult hit on [[YouTube|YouTube]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://adventuretime.frederator.com/post/462848254/and-the-hit-just-keeps-on-coming|title=Adventure Time with Finn and Jake|publisher=[[Frederator_Studios|Frederator Studios]]|date=April 4, 2008|accessdate=November 7, 2012}}</ref> After [[Nickelodeon|Nickelodeon]] declined to turn the short into a full-fledged show, Cartoon Network purchased the rights, and ''Adventure Time'' launched as a series on April 5, 2010.<ref name="enchiridion">{{cite web|url=http://frederatorblogs.com/adventure_time/2010/04/22/the-enchiridion-storyboards/|title="The Enchiridion" Storyboards|publisher=[[Frederator_Studios|Frederator Studios]]|accessdate=July 14, 2010|date=April 22, 2010|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100805002214/http://frederatorblogs.com/adventure_time/2010/04/22/the-enchiridion-storyboards/|archivedate=2010-08-05|dead-url=no}}</ref><ref name=season1airdate>{{cite web|title=''Adventure Time'' Season 1 Episodes|url=http://www.tvguide.com/tvshows/adventure-time/episodes-season-1/304498|work=[[TV_Guide|TV Guide]]|accessdate=May 30, 2013}}</ref> The show recently finished airing its [[Adventure_Time_(season_6)|sixth season]]. On July 25, 2014, the series was renewed for a seventh season, which will contain a special mini-series.<ref name=formspringansweraboutmini>{{cite web|last1=Muto|first1=Adam|title=[Adam Muto clarifies that the miniseries will be part of season seven]|url=http://new.spring.me/#!/r/at-what-point-in-the-show-is/707799484486144693|publisher=[[Spring.me|Spring.me]]|accessdate=April 3, 2015|date=April 3, 2015}}</ref> Season seven will air sometime in the Fall of 2015. A feature-length film is also in the works. Each episode is at least 11 minutes long, of which 199 have aired. The first four seasons each contain 26 episodes, the fifth contains 52 episodes, and the sixth will contain 43. For the first five and a half seasons, the show aired on Monday nights.{{#tag:ref|An exception to this is the third season episode "Thank You" which aired on a Wednesday as a holiday special.|group="nb"}} However starting with the sixth season episode "Breezy", the show began to shift both its timeslot and its day of airing.<ref>{{cite web|title=Cartoon Network – New Episodes in June (Promo)|url=http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V5GCl-CsX5I&feature=youtu.be|publisher=[[YouTube|YouTube]]|accessdate=May 19, 2014|date=May 2014}}</ref>

==Series overview==
Each ''Adventure Time'' episode is about eleven minutes in length; pairs of episodes are often telecast in order to fill a half hour program time slot.<ref>{{cite web|last=Seibert|first=Fred|title=Tells Us What You Think|url=http://archives.frederatorblogs.com/adventure_time/2010/09/28/tell-us-what-you-think/|publisher=[[Frederator_Studios|Frederator Studios]]|accessdate=January 26, 2013|date=September 28, 2010}}</ref> The series has completed four seasons of 26 episodes each and a fifth consisting of 52 episodes. It is currently in its sixth season. The series previewed on March 11, 2010, and the first season officially premiered on April 5, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.apnadesi.net/2010/03/cartoon-network-premieres-adventure.html|title=Cartoon Network Premieres Adventure Time with Finn & Jake|work=''Apnadesi.net''|date=March 1, 2010|accessdate=April 22, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131012025548/http://www.apnadesi.net/2010/03/cartoon-network-premieres-adventure.html|archive-date=2013-10-12|dead-url=yes}}</ref> The season concluded on September 27 of the same year. The second season premiered several weeks later, on October 11, and it concluded on May 2, 2011. The third season premiered on July 11, 2011, finishing its run on February 13, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|last=Seidman|first=Robert|title=Monday Cable Ratings: 'Pawn Stars' Rules + 'Pretty Little Liars,' 'Being Human,' 'WWE RAW,' 'Housewives BH' & More|url=http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2012/02/07/monday-cable-ratings-pawn-stars-rules-pretty-little-liars-being-human-lost-girl-wwe-raw-housewives-bh-more-2/119040/|work=[[TV_by_the_Numbers|TV by the Numbers]]|publisher=[[Zap2it|Zap2it]]|accessdate=January 11, 2013|date=February 7, 2012}}</ref> Its fourth season ran from April 2, 2012 through October 22, 2012. The show's fifth season ran from November 12, 2012 to March 17, 2014, a span of almost one and a half years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.animationmagazine.net/tv/adventure-time-reveals-cool-season-5-news/|title=Adventure Time Reveals Cool Season 5 News|first=Ramin|last=Zahed|work=[[Animation_Magazine|Animation Magazine]]|publisher=Animation Magazine, Inc|date=October 12, 2012|accessdate=November 4, 2012}}</ref> The series began airing its sixth season on April 21, 2014.<ref
