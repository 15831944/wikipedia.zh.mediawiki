'''死亡喪鐘'''（英語：Death Knell）是[[教堂|教堂]]裏在有人去世後敲響的三次[[教堂的鐘|鐘]]中的第二次。以英國來説，它前面一個為Passing Bell（臨終之鐘）<ref>Walters P156. He also notes  " ..there was sometimes the inconvenience that though the Passing Bell had been duly rung, the dying person might recover"</ref><ref>{{Cite book| location = New York| last = Timbs| first = John| title = Mysteries of Life, Death, and Futurity: illustrated from the best and latest authorities| date = 1863 |url= https://babel.hathitrust.org/cgi/pt?id=hvd.hnnlhk;view=1up;seq=221 }}</ref>，在臨死時敲響；後面一個為Lych Bell或Corpse Bell（葬禮之鐘），在舉行葬禮時敲響<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=rDcFAAAAYAAJ&dq=grose's%20antiquities%20passing%20bell&pg=PA202#v=onepage&q&f=false|title=Observations on the Popular Antiquities of Great Britain: chiefly illustrating the origin of our vulgar and provincial customs, ceremonies, and superstitions|last=Brand|first=John|last2=Ellis|first2=Sir Henry|last3=Halliwell-Phillipps|first3=James Orchard|date=1849-01-01|publisher=Bohn|language=en}}</ref><ref>Walters P 160</ref>，後者也就是今日喪禮中的喪鐘。按19世紀時的傳統，死亡喪鐘要在牧師得知信徒逝世後馬上敲響。但是若此時已經入夜，那麽則在第二天一早敲整整一小時的死亡喪鐘。<ref>E.g. Thomas Hood's poem "Faithless Sally Brown"; "His death which happened in his berth, At forty-odd befell: They went and told the sexton, and The sexton tolled the bell"</ref> <ref>Walters P157</ref>
