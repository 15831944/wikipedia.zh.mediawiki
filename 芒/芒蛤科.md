'''芒蛤科'''（学名：{{lang|la|Solemyidae}}），又名'''蛏螂科'''，是[[雙殼綱|雙殼綱]][[原鳃亚纲|原鳃亚纲]][[芒蛤目|芒蛤目]]之下的一個[[科_(生物)|科]]，其物種皆為[[海洋生物|海洋]]蛤蜊類動物{{r|zgdwz|NZM}}。

==生物學==
本科物種的消化管道 are remarkable in that their digestive tract is either extremely small or non-existent, and their feeding appendages are too short to reach outside the shell.<ref>Reid R. G. B. (1989) The unwhole organism. [http://icb.oxfordjournals.org/cgi/content/abstract/29/3/1133/ American Zoologist 29:1133-1140]</ref>

It has been shown that these clams host [[sulphur|sulphur]]-[[oxidizing|oxidizing]] [[bacteria|bacteria]] intracellularly within their [[gill|gill]] filaments. As [[chemoautotrophs|chemoautotrophs]], these bacterial [[symbionts|symbionts]] synthesize organic matter from CO<sub>2</sub> and are the primary source of nutrition for the whole organism.<ref>Cavanaugh C. M., Abbott M. S., Veenhuis M. (1988) Immunochemical localization of ribulose-1,5-bisphosphate carboxylase in the symbiont-containing gills of Solemya velum (Bivalvia, Mollusca). [http://www.pnas.org/content/85/20/7786 PNAS 85:7786–7789]</ref><ref>Stewart F. J., Cavanaugh, C. M. (2006) Bacterial endosymbioses in Solemya (Mollusca: Bivalvia) – Model systems for studies of symbiont-host adaptation. [http://springerlink.com/content/e24615311h534725/ Antonie van Leeuwenhoek 90: 343-360] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.is/20130202220900/http://springerlink.com/content/e24615311h534725/ |date=2013-02-02 }}</ref> In turn, the animal host provides its symbionts a habitat in which they have access to the substrates of chemoautotrophy (O<sub>2</sub>, CO<sub>2</sub>, and reduced inorganic compounds such as H<sub>2</sub>S). Together, these partners create "animals" with novel metabolic capabilities.
